## LISTENER B
I’m Mark, 46, living just outside Portland, Oregon. I design commercial landscaping, so I spend a lot of time either in my truck driving between job sites or at my drafting table with the radio on. I stumbled on this WZZZ station today. The whole "30 broadcasts left unless you pay me" gimmick is a little theatrical for my taste, but the guy's voice has a good, late-night gravel to it. Fits the mood when it's gray and drizzling here.

He lost me a bit with the initial news rundown, to be honest. Wildfires and cops shooting people in crisis—man, I get enough of that doomscrolling on my phone. I don't really need my indie DJ giving me the apocalypse report before playing a track. I listen to the radio to get out of my own head, not to be reminded that everything is on fire. 

But then he dropped the news about Sam Neill. I actually stopped what I was doing. I hadn't seen the news yet today. Seventy-eight? Damn. I grew up on *Jurassic Park* like everyone else my age, but I really respect that the DJ brought up *Possession*. Most guys on the dial would just make a dinosaur joke and call it a day, but *Possession* is a wild, deeply unsettling, brilliant movie. That showed me this guy actually cares about art and knows his stuff.

Then he fades into Nick Cave? "Into My Arms" is an absolute masterpiece of a song. Perfect, mournful choice for a tribute to a great actor. He won me back entirely with that back half. I can do without the depressing news updates, but I'll toss him a few bucks for the Nick Cave spin and the solid movie taste. I'm curious to see what he does tomorrow.

## LISTENER C
I’m Arthur, 68, living in Grand Rapids, Michigan. I taught high school history for thirty-five years before retiring a few years ago. My wife passed in 2019, so I spend a lot of my evenings reading in my armchair with the radio on. I usually stick to NPR or the local jazz station, but I get tired of the endless political panel shows and pledge drives, so I find myself spinning the dial late at night looking for something with a little more soul. 

I actually really liked the atmosphere of this broadcast. Hearing the hiss and the needle drop—that took me right back to college in the seventies. The DJ has a good, steady voice. No yelling, no gimmicks, just a guy talking to the void. I wasn't thrilled by the news roundup at first; the Memphis shooting and the wildfires are depressing, and at my age, I hear enough about the world falling apart. But he sounded genuinely weary about it, not just reading headlines for shock value. 

The news about Sam Neill really caught me off guard. I hadn't heard that at all today. I always liked him—mostly from *Jurassic Park* and *The Hunt for Red October*, to be honest. I’ve never heard of *Possession*, but I appreciate a DJ who actually knows an actor's catalog and cares about the craft rather than just reciting the standard obituary. And his musical transition? Playing "Into My Arms" by Nick Cave? Absolutely perfect. It's a gorgeous, heartbreaking song. I just sat back and closed my eyes and let it wash over me. 

I don't know about this whole dramatic "30 broadcasts until the station dies" countdown. It sounds a bit like a movie script. But if the man is going to play Nick Cave and talk to me like a real human being, I'd like him to stay on the air.
