PART ONE -- He called me "Steadfast Elara" again! It still gives me a warm feeling, like my voice truly resonates. And this news from the palace... oh, it's almost too much! I laughed out loud when he said the Scepter of Sovereign Stability was mistaken for a fire-poker. And the cats! Honestly, the palace cats probably have more common sense than half the Royal Court.

This is exactly what I was starting to suspect. It's not just the daft, arbitrary laws that are dissolving; it's the *layers* of pomp and circumstance, the things that were supposed to be important but had no real substance behind them. The "Chamber of Grand Proclamations" becoming a cat nap spot, the scepter just a "shiny stick"—it's like the world is saying, "Enough with the show, let's get back to what's real."

It's... unsettling in a way, I suppose. Stability is a good thing, when it's genuine. My bread needs a stable market, stable flour prices, stable customers. But was that scepter *ever* truly about stability for the common folk? Or was it just a prop?

Hemlock said the land was "sorting 'em out," and Maeve said it was "clearing its throat." This, with the scepter and the genealogies, feels like a full-blown spring cleaning. A bit chaotic, perhaps, but a cleaning nonetheless. The Royal Chancellor being "vexed" is just icing on the cake, honestly. He probably valued the *appearance* of tradition more than its actual meaning.

I still have that little knot of worry, the one that asks, "Where does it stop?" But for now, the overwhelming feeling is... amusement, and a growing sense of vindication. It confirms my belief that the monolith, strange as it is, seems to be on the side of common sense. My hope isn't just cautious anymore; it's actively enjoying the show. Perhaps the real stability comes from good bread and honest living, not from shiny sticks and dusty chambers.
