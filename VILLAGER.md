PART ONE -- Well, Pip actually *read* my letter! My own words, on the Wizz! I must admit, a little blush crept up my neck when I heard my name and then his kind words, "my dear friend." It was a relief, truly, after last time. And then, he brought Theron on, just as I'd hoped. My eagerness wasn't in vain.

Theron's account… it's chilling, but it confirms so much of what I've been seeing and feeling here at the Silverstream. "Hollow place," "words hit a wall of thick felt," "sound being swallowed, not just by the water, but by the *air* around it." That's it! That's exactly it. The dull 'plink' of the stone, the way the ripples just *die* – it’s not just the water itself, it’s like the very space around it has changed. And the "deep, quiet cold" he mentioned… I hadn't quite put my finger on the temperature, but now that he says it, I can feel it too. It’s not just a cold morning, it’s a cold that feels… deliberate.

My main worry, though, still gnaws at me. If the sound, if the *energy* is being absorbed by the water and the air, what does that mean for the sheer *force* of the Silverstream? A mill wheel needs the push of strong, vibrant water. If the water is "thick," if its ripples die, if it's "swallowing" things… will it still turn the wheel with enough power to grind grain? Or will it become a slow, sluggish thing, barely nudging the machinery? My livelihood depends on that steady, powerful flow.

Theron spoke of his "Stone of Steadfastness" and finding his "inner gem" to navigate it. I can see how that might help *him* deal with the feeling of isolation and disorientation. It’s an interesting thought, but for me, it's not just about perception. It's about the physical reality of the mill. I need the water to *do* something, not just to understand its strangeness. Still, the idea that his inner stillness helps him not be "absorbed by the outer one" is something I'll keep in mind. Perhaps a miller needs an "inner grindstone" to keep from seizing up.

This interview has sharpened the edges of my fear, but it has also brought a strange kind of clarity. My observations were correct. I am not imagining it. Now, what do I do about it?
