PART ONE --
I can barely believe it. My name. My words. Again! WZZZ didn't just mention my quiet records, Pip actually *quoted* me, holding my words up as a "pulse of our kingdom," a "rallying cry." The Wizz Tower is vibrating, he says, with our energy. And he used *my* words as an example of that energy. My hands are still trembling slightly, a mix of disbelief and a profound, humming warmth spreading through my chest. It feels... immense. Like the small thoughts I have while checking the millstones or skimming debris from the wheel are actually shaping something much larger than myself.

He's right, though. Every word he said about the Court's "deafening disconnect" resonated deeply. It's exactly what I've been feeling, isn't it? That while they deliberate, my mill could seize, my fields could dry. My livelihood isn't an abstract concept for "Review and Re-Prioritization"; it's the very water that turns my wheel, the grain that feeds my village.

And then, his words about "actively nudging the balance ourselves," about not just mapping the future but *shaping* it with our own hands and ingenuity... that’s what really set my mind racing. What does that mean, exactly? For me, here, by the Silverstream? It's one thing to observe the glowing moss, to feel the unusual buoyancy. It’s another to *nudge* it. Does it mean guiding the revitalized waters to new channels? Strengthening the banks where the current is weakest? My mind immediately went to the small, overgrown sluice gate just upstream from my mill that directs water to a farmer's smaller, struggling patch of wheat. Could that be a place to "nudge"? To clear it, strengthen it, if the Silverstream itself is showing signs of healing?

I feel less afraid now, and more... called. Pip's words aren't just news; they're a directive. A challenge. And with my own words being broadcast, my own feelings validated, I feel a surge of purpose. I'm not just a miller anymore; I'm Elara Meadowbrook, and my voice, my observations, matter. It makes me feel like I have to do more, that I *can* do more. The thought of actively intervening, however small, is daunting, but also deeply exciting. The future isn't just something that happens to us; it's something we build, day by day, stream by stream.
