PART ONE --
Blast it all, Pip! He actually read my letter! My own words, about my bees and the saplings, right there on the air! I can almost hear Agnes at The Daily Crumb giving a little satisfied huff, knowing I sent it in. And to be called a "pioneer"! Well, I suppose I *have* been keeping an eye on things, closer than most. It’s not just theory, Pip said, it’s *proof*. And it is. I can see it. My younger apple trees, especially the Braeburns, they’re putting out new shoots that feel… stronger. Not just growing, but *eager*. And the bees, oh, the bees! Their flights are more direct, less that scattered, frantic energy they had right after the Block settled in. There’s a purpose to their foraging, a hum that feels more like the old days. It’s like they’re finding the right pathways again, and my own efforts, my own listening to the land’s rhythm, seem to be helping them along. It makes a man proud to see his hard work, and the work of his little buzzing friends, make a difference.

As for these "ancient memories"... well, Pip certainly spun my words nicely, calling it an "open mind." I suppose that’s one way of looking at it. But he’s right, if these whispers and echoes are part of the land’s instructions, then they’re worth paying attention to. I’m still not expecting to suddenly recall the history of the King’s first pie crust, but I’ll keep an eye out. Perhaps it’s a feeling in the soil, a certain way the light falls, or a new pattern in the growth of the wild hawthorn down by the creek. Something tangible, something that connects to the health of the land. My bees have their own ancient memories, their instincts, guiding them. Maybe the land’s memories are a bit like that, guiding its own recovery. I’ll be watching for anything peculiar, anything that feels… out of place, but also *right*. Something that shows the land remembers how it’s *supposed* to be. And if it helps the Golden Delicious produce fruit again, well, I'll recall whatever the land needs me to recall.
