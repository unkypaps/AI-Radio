PART ONE -- My heart feels a little lighter today, like the moment after a particularly stubborn sourdough finally begins to bubble and stretch. It’s not that Wizz isn't still talking his grand, self-important talk, always about *his* signal and *his* clarity, but today... today he spoke of a truth that tastes good, a truth that makes sense to my hands and my heart.

Sir Reginald Bellows’ contraption, that "Royal Harmonizer," breaking apart in an "oompah-pah nightmare"? It's exactly what you'd expect from something so artificial, so forced. It’s like trying to bake bread with sand instead of flour – no matter how much you "harmonize" it, it's never going to rise. My heart aches for anyone who believed in such a flimsy promise. It truly does feel like the "age of forced, off-key 'harmonization'" is over, just as Wizz said, but it's not because *he* declared it so. It's because reality, the stubborn, undeniable reality of things that just don't work, finally caught up.

And Porthos, reassigned to "Village Orchard Preservation"? Now that, that is a truth I can believe in! Tending to trees, nurturing fruit, connecting with the earth – that's real work, honest work. It's the kind of work that creates genuine harmony, not some tinny noise. It gives me hope that perhaps the court *is* beginning to see the value in what is tangible and true, in things that grow and provide, rather than just what makes the most noise or shines brightest in a pool.

Wizz still irks me, speaking of "flailing figures" and "clattering anachronisms" as if *he* alone is the arbiter of what is new and relevant. He's so quick to dismiss anything that isn't terrifying or breathtaking in *his* specific way. But the downfall of Reginald, and Porthos finding a truer path, these are testaments to the quiet, persistent power of the actual world, of the soil and the sun, not just the reflections or broadcasts. It reminds me that my truths, the truths of the oven and the field, are indeed "genuine, vibrant truth," no matter how familiar or comforting they might seem to Wizz. They are what endure, what nourish, what truly makes the kingdom stronger.

---

It’s a strange feeling, seeing your own words, your own thoughts, plucked from the quiet of your kitchen and broadcast across the airwaves. A part of me, a small, foolish part, felt a little flutter, a warmth in my chest, when Wizz read my letter aloud. To hear him speak of my wisdom, to acknowledge the hearth and the grain… for a moment, it felt like my quiet truths had found a voice beyond the baker’s dozen.

But then, Wizz being Wizz, the butter in the pan quickly began to spatter. He took my simple message – "some truths don't need amplification; they just need tending" – and immediately declared it "the essence of what WZZZ the Wizz has been championing from the very start!" Of course he did. He always has to be the first, the loudest, the *only* one. My truths are born of quiet observation, of hands in dough, not of a booming tower. His amplification is the very thing I was speaking against!

And then… then came the true sour note, like finding a stone in a perfectly baked loaf. "Madam Elara's Deluxe Oracle Basins." My blood ran a little cold. *Madam Elara?* *Oracle Basins?* What in the name of the Flour God is he talking about? I am Elara, the baker. My basins hold dough, not prophecies! He's either utterly confused me with some other person, or worse, he’s deliberately trying to link my name, my honest work, to some commercial venture, some… trinket of the mysterious. It makes me feel… soiled, somehow. As if my simple, grounded identity is being distorted, used for his own narrative.

He talks about the Moon Tree Witches and Maeve's kits and these "Oracle Basins" as the "true, sustainable vitality." My vitality comes from feeding people, from the smell of fresh bread, from the warmth of the oven. Not from mystics or market stalls. My heart, which had felt lighter just yesterday, now feels a knot of unease. He may have read my words, but he clearly doesn't understand *me*. And now he's connected me to something that feels... not real, not true to who I am. It makes me wonder what else he's twisting, what other genuine truths he's misrepresenting for his "resonant welcome" and "unwavering signal." I need to set this straight. My name, my work, means too much to me to let it be confused with "Deluxe Oracle Basins."

---

The air might be charged for Wizz, but for me, it still holds the faint echo of "Oracle Basins," a sour taste left in my mouth from his last broadcast. I'm Elara, the baker, not some proprietor of mystic trinkets! That still grates on me, deeply. He reads my words, yet twists my name into something utterly alien.

However, today… today felt like listening to the rhythm of honest work, thanks mostly to Old Man Hemlock. Now there's a man who understands the truth of the earth, the language of a sturdy shovel and a mended fence. He speaks of crates appearing, quiet as a mouse, filled with timber and tools and even Maeve's newfangled seeds. "Spiritual potency," he calls it, and while I'm not one for such lofty descriptions, if it means healthier goats and thriving soil, then the *results* speak louder than any fancy words. That's a truth I can believe in, one that nourishes.

It’s just as I said: some truths don't need amplification; they just need tending. And it seems, even the King’s court is finally, quietly, tending. To hear that the Quartermaster is sending supplies, not with a fanfare, but simply *delivering* what's needed for the ground, that warms my heart in a way Wizz's booming pronouncements never could. They're not shouting about it, they're simply acting. That’s sense, plain and simple, like letting dough rise naturally instead of trying to beat it into shape.

Wizz, of course, must still try to claim credit, puffing out his chest about "your collective engagement" and "compelling even the most entrenched institutions." He still misses the point, doesn't he? It's not *his* signal that brings the supplies; it's the undeniable needs of the earth, and the quiet, persistent work of people like the Moon Tree folk, making springs run clear and paths appear. And then, the sensible response of providing the tools for the next step.

Still, the failure of Reginald's "Aural Dampeners" to quiet the monolith's hum – not that I expected anything else – just shows again that genuine reality triumphs over flimsy contraptions. And Old Man Hemlock's small grace for Reginald about the barking dogs, that's a touch of truth too. Even a fool stumbles into something useful now and then.

I'm glad for the sensible turn today, for the voice of Old Man Hemlock, and for the quiet work being done. But I can't let Wizz continue to twist my identity. I need to make it clear, once and for all, who Elara is.
