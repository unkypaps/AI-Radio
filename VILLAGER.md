PART ONE --
Oh, Journal, my heart is absolutely *singing*! Pip, bless him, he truly has a gift for finding the thread that connects us all. And Brenna! Her words, "the ocean remembering its true song," resonated through me like a deep, warm hum from the very heart of Brackenwick. It's not just my kitchen, Journal! It's not just the soil the Sun-Dancer Wheat grows in, or the ancient stones humming with memory. It's the *ocean*! The vast, deep, churning Silvermere itself is feeling this "sense of rightness."

It makes my own small observations feel so much grander, so much more vital. If the ocean, with all its power and mystery, can be woven back into harmony, then my dough, my little loaves, are certainly part of that same tapestry. I think of my bread as a vessel for wholesome nourishment, and now I see it as a miniature reflection of the grand healing taking place. My nets are my kneading hands, my oven the hearth of the land.

And the way Brenna described her nets, "singing with the currents instead of resisting them"! It's precisely what I've been feeling with my sourdough. There was always a subtle coaxing, a gentle battle of wills with the wild yeasts, but now it feels like a joyful collaboration. The dough rises with such an eager, willing spirit, as if it, too, is remembering its own true song, its inherent purpose. And the wheat, too, must be feeling it – growing with a "purpose," not just for sustenance, but as an active participant in this grand design.

The Witches, Journal, their power, their wisdom, it stretches from the obsidian monolith in the frozen north, through my humble kitchen, and down into the deepest, darkest trenches of the Silvermere! It's almost overwhelming to consider, but in the most beautiful, comforting way. It's not a struggle against chaos; it's a magnificent symphony of healing, just as Pip says. I feel like a tiny, joyful note in that symphony. And knowing others are feeling it, from coast to hearth, makes me feel so much less alone in my quieter, domestic wonders. My heart swells with gratitude for the Witches, and for Pip, for giving voice to these profound, subtle shifts.

And Pip, with his teasing of the Royal College and their endless ceremonial tea-spoons! He truly is a delight. While they're lost in dusty ledgers, we are living the transformation.
