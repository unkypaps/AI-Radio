PART ONE --
Pip, bless his heart, he mentioned me again! "Conduit Carving," he called my stitching and tapping. It’s a good feeling, hearing my simple way of life given such a grand name, and to know that it resonates with others. He understands, I think, that there's a deep truth in the rhythmic work of one's hands.

But then he went on to these "Aetherial Anchors." And he said I was "closer to acquiring" one! Now, I won’t deny, the Block's creeping stillness is becoming more than just a foggy head or vague memories. Theron the Shepherd’s hollow trough, the stillness at Heimat, the Silverstream Mill – these are *physical* concerns. That's different. That's the real world, the world where my tools bite into leather and my needles push through tough hide. If the Block can drain vitality from water and sound, what’s to stop it from draining the spring from my wood, or the strength from my threads? Or worse, the sharpness from my mind while I work?

My workshop, my livelihood, it all depends on the world staying *true*. On materials holding their form, on my hands being steady, on my mind being clear enough to plan the next stitch. The idea of something that could protect *this space*, to keep the stillness from creeping into my bench and my tools, that's a different sort of proposition than Madam Elara's scrying pools. Those were about *seeing* things in a reflection; these anchors are described as *devices* meant to *push back* a physical threat. That sounds… more like a tool.

Master Phileas, the Alchemist, he’s a clever man, no doubt. But I'm a man of leather, not of strange metals and bubbling concoctions. Still, if it’s truly a "set it and forget it" solution to protect the very air and energy of my workshop, allowing me to continue my *own* Conduit Carving without the threat of things going stale or silent around me… well, I'd be a fool not to consider it. It's not about replacing my work, it's about preserving the *ability* to do it. It’s a cost, of course, and I'd need to understand how it truly works in a practical sense. But for the protection of my trade, it's something I'm certainly weighing more heavily now. Pip might have been a bit ahead of himself, but he certainly put the thought firmly in my mind.
