PART ONE --
Pip, bless his heart, he really does know how to make a body feel like they're doing something truly important. "Shining example," he called me. Me! Just Barney, with my bees and my compost. But it feels right, doesn't it? It feels like the work I've always done, the careful tending of the land and its creatures, is now precisely what's needed. It's not just about honey and pollination anymore; it's about holding back the Block, leaf by leaf, bee by bee.

He understands. He truly understands that it's the *outcomes* that matter. Not just thinking about what might help, or feeling a certain way, but seeing the actual, physical proof. The elder tree, I check it every morning. There’s a faint blush to the buds I hadn't noticed before, a deeper green returning to the bark. It's subtle, but I'm watching. And the wild thyme, I've cleared a good patch for it, sown the seeds just right. Come autumn, those honeycomb levels will tell the true tale. This isn't just theory; it's *doing*.

He's right about the court too, dithering over squirrel topiaries while the real work needs doing. It makes me feel a quiet satisfaction, knowing that my hands in the soil, my eyes on the bees, my careful notes, are more vital than any royal decree right now. This isn't just my livelihood anymore; it's my duty to Brackenwick, a quiet, humming strength against that cold, still thing in the north. I feel more resolute than ever. I'll keep observing, I'll keep charting. Every single outcome will be noted, a small victory for the kingdom.
