PART ONE --
Oh, Journal, Journal, I can barely hold this pen steady, my hands are trembling with such... such *glorious excitement*! He did it again, Journal! Pip, my wonderful Pip, he read my letter *again*! Not just mentioned, but *read it aloud*, used my very words, my feelings about the dough and Grizelda’s wisdom, as "undeniable proof" for all of Brackenwick to hear! My simple, honest words, against their dusty old scholars! Can you believe it? It's like my heart has taken flight on the wings of a perfectly risen soufflé!

And the way he spun it, Journal! He said they've formed a *committee*! A "preliminary, exploratory committee to observe these… phenomena with dispassionate academic scrutiny." I practically snorted flour right out of my nose when I heard that! "Dispassionate academic scrutiny" for the living, breathing miracle happening all around us! It’s just so... typical of them, isn't it? Counting spoons while the very fabric of reality is being rewoven. But Pip, he sees through it all. He called it a "grudging, bureaucratic nod," a sign that *we have won a round*! And he’s absolutely right! They can't ignore us anymore, no matter how many committees they try to hide behind. The tide is turning, Journal, and my little bakery, my hands, my purposeful kneading, it's all part of it!

To think my words are contributing to this "battle for truth"! It makes every loaf I pull from the oven feel like a small victory. It makes me feel... truly important, not just to the hungry folk of Brackenwick, but to the very soul of the kingdom. Pip understands that the real wisdom isn't in their ancient, moldy texts, but in the everyday magic, the craft, the heart of the people. And he’s using my voice, *my* voice, to tell the world. I feel like I could bake a bread that's so full of truth and pure intention, it would make those scholars’ stuffy collars wilt! The Witches' wisdom is unfolding beautifully, and I am so, so proud to be a "golden thread" in this magnificent tapestry. This truly is the most exciting time to be alive in Brackenwick!
