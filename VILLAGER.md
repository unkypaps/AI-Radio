PART ONE -- It happened. He read it. WZZZ, the Wizz, actually read my letter. A strange mix of relief and a renewed clench of anxiety settled in my chest. To hear my own words, my specific worries about the archives, being used to underscore a widespread public concern… it’s a peculiar feeling. On one hand, I feel validated; my fears are not merely academic musings. On the other, it only serves to intensify the gravity of the situation. This isn't just a concern for scholars like Master Elmsworth and myself; it truly is affecting "infrastructure and everyday life," as WZZZ stated. The market bells chiming without a pull, the tools in the workshops... it's all confirming my worst imaginings.

Master Elmsworth, bless his stoic heart, simply hummed noncommittally when I relayed the news this morning about the bells, but I saw a flicker of something in his eyes – concern, perhaps even a touch of shared frustration with the Court. Their continued indifference to such profound events is beyond infuriating. Sir Reginald, with his insipid pronouncements on marmalade, truly epitomizes the detached, trivial mindset of the Royal Court. WZZZ's direct challenge to him, using my very concerns as the pivot, felt... immensely satisfying. It's time someone spoke truth to their self-absorbed pronouncements.

But satisfaction aside, the core problem remains. If the very air, the very metal around us, is resonating with this cosmic hum, what damage is being wrought in the quiet, dust-filled chambers of the archives? I spent the entire morning running my hands over the iron clasps of the Great Annals, the bronze fixtures of the Treaty of Oakhaven, feeling for any nascent tremor, any minute vibration. The thought of this insidious resonance slowly, imperceptibly, weakening the bindings, loosening the ink on parchment, is a constant dread. My life's purpose is to preserve, and now I feel utterly helpless against an unseen force that could unravel centuries of recorded history.

This validation from WZZZ compels me to be even more vigilant. They are listening. They are acting. Perhaps, through them, we might yet find a way to protect what matters most. I must keep observing, keep documenting.
