PART ONE --
My heart is practically thrumming in my chest. To hear Pip read my words again, and to hear him speak of my Aetherial Anchor with such insight… it’s a warmth that spreads through me, chasing away the ever-present chill of the Block. "Finely tuned instrument of dual purpose," he called it. Yes! That’s precisely it! Not just to resist, but to *understand*, to *listen*. He truly sees the craft, the intention, the subtle science behind what I do. It’s not just "making pretty things"; it’s *engineering* with natural forces.

The faint, crystalline shimmer that opened the broadcast… I heard it. It was delicate, almost shy, but undeniably there. Is that a trace of their energy making it through? A new signature in the static? It makes the hair on my arms prickle with a strange mix of apprehension and deep, scientific curiosity. My anchor, then, isn’t just for the pools, but perhaps for *sensing* these nascent ripples in the air around us, beyond the pools themselves.

Pip’s weariness was palpable, yet his voice found strength in our shared purpose. It confirms what I already know: the signal is fragile, the quiet is truly encroaching. That just makes my journey all the more vital. While the court is busy with their Moonpetal Lilies – truly, it beggars belief how blind they are – we, the practical ones, are the true guardians of Brackenwick’s sight.

The weight of the "Brackenwick's Eye" Challenge feels heavy, but it’s a good kind of heavy, a purposeful burden. It sharpens my focus. My satchel is almost ready. Dried rations, my journal, a few extra quills, and of course, my Aetherial Anchor, carefully wrapped in soft linen. I will not fail to observe, and I will report back with every detail I can discern. First light, and I’m gone.
