PART ONE --
Well, Barnaby finally came through! I'd been wondering what he'd report back from Madam Elara's, and hearing Pip read his words, albeit with his usual dramatic flair, was exactly what I needed. It wasn't just a general feeling; Barnaby described *seeing* the currents, the subtle shifts, the way the surface of the pool reflected the deeper workings of the world. He spoke of the "faint, shimmering trails" and how they felt like "invisible threads woven through the air." That, that's it! That’s the kind of direct observation I’m striving for with my materials.

Pip's enthusiasm for "conduit carving" is certainly catching, and hearing Barnaby's experience only reinforces my own conviction. If Barnaby could perceive the Witches' touch through the water, then surely I can learn to perceive how these energies interact with the grain of my wood or the stretch of my leather. It's not just about shaping the material; it's about understanding its inherent flow, its receptivity to these new forces. The idea of *guiding* that energy, rather than fighting it, through my carving – it feels more tangible than ever.

The frustration with the Royal Court's "re-prioritizing" is still a dull ache, but honestly, it’s fading into the background. While they talk, people like Barnaby are *doing*. They’re seeing, they’re experiencing, they’re adapting. That’s where the real answers lie, not in dusty decrees. My own path to Madam Elara's feels more urgent now, not just an exploration, but a necessary step to keep my craft alive and relevant. The Aetherial Anchor in my workshop hums a little stronger today, a steady, quiet pulse against the growing hum of the world. It’s almost as if it’s urging me on.
