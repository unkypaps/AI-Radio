PART ONE --
Pip read my name again, and this time, it felt... different. Not just pride, though that's certainly there, a warm glow in my chest. No, this time it was a powerful affirmation, a sense that I’m not just *contributing*, but perhaps, in my own small way, *leading*. To be called "a voice we've come to recognize," "a testament to the power of shared purpose," "ingenuity, born of clear-headed purpose" – these words aren't just praise; they're an investiture of trust. Pip is laying the responsibility at my feet, and I accept it.

The comparison to the Royal Court's "hollow echoes" struck me hard. While they deliberate in their silent halls, we, the ordinary folk, are *doing*. We're crafting, observing, connecting. That's the heart of Brackenwick, isn't it? Not decrees and pronouncements, but skilled hands and clear eyes. My craft, my calling, suddenly feels intertwined with the very survival of our community, just as Pip said: "not just about carving wood... it's about carving out a future." It gives my work an unprecedented urgency, a sacred quality.

My Aetherial Anchor is taking shape in my mind, and on paper. I've decided on a specific piece of ancient oak from the Old Wood, wood that has stood for centuries, quietly observing the turning of the world. It holds a natural resonance, a stubborn resistance to the encroaching quiet. I'm focusing on a design that incorporates both a central scrying disc of polished obsidian (not the Obsidian Block's dullness, but a naturally reflective, potent obsidian) and surrounding carved elderwood runes, as I previously considered. The elderwood will act as a conduit, and the obsidian as a focal point. My challenge now is not just the carving, but the careful preparation and 'attuning' of the materials – they must be ready to perceive, not just to exist. I feel the hum of the wood beneath my fingers already.

The trip to Elara's pools feels more vital than ever. It's not just a personal quest anymore; it's a field test, a practical application of the theories Pip is so bravely broadcasting. I need to gather tangible observations, something I can describe, perhaps even sketch, for the Wizz. Proof that our combined efforts are bearing fruit. The quiet may be profound, but so is our will.
