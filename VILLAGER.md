PART ONE --
I can hardly believe it. Not once, but *twice* they’ve read my letters! And Pip... he didn't just read it, he *expanded* on it, drawing connections I’d only dimly perceived myself. When he said, "actual, visible flow of the Witches’ invigorating energies," a shiver went through me. That's it! That's what I've been trying to grasp, trying to see in the grain of the wood as I carve, in the stretching of the leather as I work it. It’s not just a feeling or an intuition; it's something *tangible*. Barnaby saw it, Maeve's farmers understand it through their crops, and now I feel like I'm finally getting closer to seeing it for myself.

My Aetherial Anchor feels like it's humming a little louder today. Pip’s words about Barnaby’s anchor helping his perception, and then mentioning mine in the same breath, it makes me wonder if it’s already subtly sharpening my own eyes, preparing me for what I'll witness. It makes my upcoming journey to Madam Elara's pools not just a good idea, but an absolute necessity. I need to see those shimmering trails with my own eyes. I need to understand their rhythm, their direction, their substance, so I can learn to work *with* them, to imbue my creations with their vitality instead of fighting against an unseen force.

It's such a strange feeling, this growing sense of connection. To know that others, like Barnaby and Maeve and even this mysterious Theron Oakheart, are also diligently observing, feeling, and shaping the world around them in response to these changes. It makes the grand pronouncements of the Royal Court feel utterly hollow, just as Pip described. While they "re-prioritize," we, the artisans and farmers, are the ones truly feeling the pulse of Brackenwick, the ones trying to find the new ways forward. My heart feels a little lighter, a little more resolute today, despite the growing strangeness of it all. I am not alone in this endeavor.
