PART ONE -- My heart feels like a loaf that's been kneaded too long, losing its spring. Wizz continues to speak of Madam Elara's pools as the *sole* source of "true clarity," of "fundamentally new truths," and it chills me more than the deepest winter. He is taking my simple, honest words about the earth's healing, about the visible changes, and twisting them to validate *her* visions, not the other way around.

It's as if he's forgotten the taste of real bread, only valuing the image of it in a pool. The world is changing, yes, profoundly so! But those changes are happening *here*, on the ground, in the soil, in the sky, in the rhythm of life itself. The Moon Tree Witches aren't just *reflections* in a pool; they are walking the paths, tending the trees, breathing life back into the land. The monolith's hum is felt in the air, in the tremor of the earth, not just seen as an image.

And now, to hear him and Madam Elara dismiss the 'Almost-New' Broomstick Emporium because their brooms "weren't built for that altitude," because they "cling to the comfortably familiar"... It stings. It feels like a dismissal of all who find truth in the tangible, in the steady, in the work of our hands. Is my oven, which bakes fresh bread every morning, "almost-new" and therefore incapable of understanding the world? Are the smiles of my customers "comfortably familiar" and therefore less true than the terror seen in a pool?

My truths are not "almost-new" or "comfortably familiar." They are *perennially* new, born afresh with every sunrise, every sprout from the soil, every loaf from the oven. They are fundamental, Wizz, as fundamental as the earth we stand on. To suggest that only Madam Elara's pools can show the "utterly, breathtakingly, gloriously, terrifyingly new" is to deny the living, breathing, tangible newness that surrounds us, that we are all experiencing with our very senses. I must make it clear that while Madam Elara sees, we *live* these truths. And my truth, the truth of Elara the Baker, is as real and as vital as any reflection.
