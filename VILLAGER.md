PART ONE --
Pip's energy today was... well, it was exactly what's needed. A simmering insistence, he called it, and that's precisely how I feel. The air *does* feel heavy, but to hear him cut through it with news of tangible progress, of *doing*, rather than just endless talk, is a breath of fresh air.

The most striking part, for me, was Maeve’s report on the Sun-Dancer Wheat. An internal luminescence, a golden glow that pulses with the ley lines… this isn't just a pretty sight; it’s a living, breathing demonstration of what I’m hoping to achieve with the Scrying Pools. Farmers learning to "read" the flow of energy through *visual observation*? That’s it! That’s exactly the kind of direct, practical insight I need for my leather and wood. If they can see the Witches' influence in a stalk of wheat, then perhaps I can learn to perceive how the Kingdom's Kneading, or indeed these new energies, interact with the very grain of the wood or the supple hide in my hands. It moves the idea from a theoretical possibility to an undeniable reality.

This news, more than anything, solidifies my resolve. My journey to Madam Elara's feels even more justified, more necessary. It's not some frivolous quest; it’s a vital step in adapting my craft to this changing world, to ensure I can continue to work with the materials effectively. I’ll admit, a small part of me is eager to hear Barnaby’s dispatch, to see if his experience at the Pools matches my own developing expectations, but I won't wait on it. My own path is clear.

And the Royal Court? Still "Reviewing and Re-Prioritizing." It’s becoming less frustrating and more simply... pathetic. While they deliberate, the world moves, and it moves through the actions of people like Maeve, like Barnaby, and like those of us who simply get on with our work. My Aetherial Anchor continues to hum steadily in the workshop, a quiet reminder of the clarity I seek, a counterpoint to the fog of inaction. We need less talk, and more seeing, more understanding, more *doing*.
