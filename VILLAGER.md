PART ONE -- He's doing it! He's actually taking my questions and concerns directly to the people, and using my very words! "Elara the Baker's anecdote," "Elara's latest letter, which introduces a critical nuance..." It's exhilarating, honestly. To think that my little observations from behind the counter, while kneading dough or weighing out flour, are being considered by the Wizz, and even by the court! It feels like I'm not just a baker anymore, but... a voice. A small, yeast-scented voice, but a voice nonetheless.

And now he's bringing on Old Man Hemlock. Well, if anyone knows about the *practical* side of things, it's Hemlock. Grumpy as a badger with a toothache, but honest as the day is long. If he confirms the Thistle story – and I'd bet my best sourdough starter he will – then that really cements it. It's not just Elara the Baker seeing things, it's Hemlock too. He'll give it the gruff, no-nonsense endorsement it needs. He’ll make it real for those who might still be scoffing.

The "differentiation question" – that's my main worry, the one I wrote to him about. He called it a "critical nuance." Exactly! If it's just the ridiculous eel regulations and pointless taxes that are losing their meaning, then frankly, good riddance. But what about the laws that keep us safe? The ones that prevent someone from just walking into my bakery and taking my last loaf, or setting fire to the mill? That's the part that keeps me up at night, even with the excitement of being heard. Hemlock is the perfect person to ground this. He won't mince words. He'll tell us what's actually happening on the ground, away from the philosophical musings of the court.

I'm still wary, of course. This is Brackenwick, and things rarely go smoothly. But there's a growing part of me that feels... cautiously optimistic. If this strange phenomenon is truly winnowing away the absurdities of our kingdom, leaving behind only what makes sense, then perhaps this dark monolith isn't just a harbinger of doom. Perhaps it's a very strange, very large, very obsidian broom, sweeping away the cobwebs of bureaucracy. My bread still rises, my customers still pay (most of them, anyway), and the sun still comes up. For now, that's enough.
