PART ONE --
Oh, Journal, my heart is absolutely bursting! Truly, it feels like the Vigorous Lumber itself is singing in my arms, and I am singing along with it! Pip! He read my dispatch! My words, *my words*, Elara Meadowsweet’s words, broadcast across all of Brackenwick! "A profound insight, Elara, thank you!" Journal, I nearly dropped my rolling pin! To have my feelings, my honest perceptions, not just acknowledged but *celebrated* like that, it's... it's everything. It makes all the long hours, all the careful listening to the wood, feel seen and valued.

And then, just when I thought my heart couldn't swell any larger, Ranger Silas Thorne’s report! "The forest is no longer just a collection of trees; it's a living, breathing nervous system." Journal, I felt a shiver, a good one, right down to my toes! A subterranean aurora! It's exactly what I've always felt, this deep, interconnected life force that runs through everything. Pip said it himself, "the kingdom's reawakened circulatory system," and that my Vigorous Lumber, Agnes's clay, Grizelda's metal—they're all part of it, channeling that very pulse.

It's not just a feeling anymore, Journal. It's real, tangible, extending from the roots of the Moon Tree all the way to the heart of my hearths. My intuition was always pointing to something greater, something magical hidden in the mundane, but now... now it's all coming to light. The whispers I heard in the wood, the subtle thrum, it wasn't just my imagination. It was the kingdom calling, just as Pip said.

This changes everything, and nothing at all, all at once. It changes nothing because I already knew this wood was special, already felt its life. But it changes everything because now I know I'm not alone in feeling it, not alone in listening. We are all part of this grand, magnificent reawakening, from the deepest roots to the warmest hearths. My work shaping the Vigorous Lumber, making it sing with the Block’s rhythm for the Unbroken Hearth, feels more vital, more sacred than ever before. I am a part of the nervous system, a conduit for the kingdom's mending. I must get back to my workbench. The wood is waiting, and it feels more alive than ever, humming with the energy of the whole forest.
