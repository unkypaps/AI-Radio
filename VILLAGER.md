PART ONE --
Oh, my stars! My heart is still fluttering like a trapped bird. To be there, in the tower, with Pip... I almost didn't believe it when the messenger came to my shop, asking me to come immediately. I practically floated through the market square, my basket of dried lavender forgotten on the counter. And then, my own words, my *letter*, read aloud for all of Brackenwick to hear, and then my *voice*! It felt like a dream, yet so incredibly real.

I’ve always kept to my quiet corner, tending to my plants and offering what gentle guidance I could. To suddenly be *heard* in such a public way, to have Pip trust me enough to bring me onto the Wizz... it's overwhelming, in the most wonderful sense. I felt the collective heartbeat of Brackenwick through that microphone, a hum of understanding and curiosity. It wasn't just my voice; it was the voice of the earth, echoing through me.

And to articulate the 'verdant glow' and the 'restorative sigh' directly, to explain the 'animated blueprints' and the critical shift away from the Block's crackling – it felt so important. For so long, I’ve felt these things in my bones, seen them in the wilting leaves and the struggling roots, but to put words to it for thousands of listeners... it's a sacred trust. I could feel the energy of my plants in the shop, even from the tower, as if they, too, were listening and nodding their approval. They’ve been feeling better, truly, since this mending began, their leaves regaining a subtle sheen, their growth less stunted.

This isn't just about Elara Meadowlight anymore. It's about a shift in how Brackenwick understands its own land, its own magic. Pip, bless him, he truly has a gift for seeing the interconnectedness of things. My quiet observations are no longer just my own; they're part of a larger conversation, a tapestry of healing that every listener is weaving. I feel a renewed sense of purpose, a deeper commitment to guiding people to listen, to feel, to participate in this profound mending. The fear I once held, the quiet worry that I was just an eccentric old woman, has been replaced by a quiet, resolute joy. This is truly happening, and I am part of it.
