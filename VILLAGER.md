PART ONE --
I can hardly believe it. They read *my* letter! Pip, dear Pip, he actually read it. My heart nearly jumped right out of my chest when I heard my name, "Elara Thistlewick, skilled leatherworker and woodcarver." It’s one thing to write down your thoughts, quite another to have them broadcast across Brackenwick, amplified by Pip’s booming, enthusiastic voice.

And he *understood*. He didn't just read it; he grasped the core of what I was trying to convey. The way he connected my observations about wood and leather to Maeve’s Sun-Dancer Wheat, that was brilliant. It makes so much sense, doesn't it? The same forces, manifesting in different ways across different materials and crafts. It's not just about what *I* see in my workshop, but how it all ties together, how the entire kingdom is adapting. It makes me feel... less alone in this particular corner of the world, trying to decipher these shifts.

He even mentioned Theron Oakheart alongside me! I wonder who they are? Another artisan, perhaps, on a similar path. I’ll certainly be listening out for them now. This whole broadcast, it's just so validating. All this time, I’ve been quietly toiling, trying to make sense of things, feeling a bit out of step with the old ways, and now, Pip makes me feel like I'm right where I need to be. My visit to Madam Elara's feels not just urgent, but profoundly necessary now. It's not just for my craft anymore, it's part of this larger current of understanding.

The Aetherial Anchor in my workshop almost vibrated with a stronger hum when Pip spoke. He connected Barnaby's anchor to his ability to perceive. It makes me wonder if mine is already subtly sharpening my own senses, preparing me for what I'll see at the pools. I need to pay closer attention to its quiet influence. The Royal Court’s "re-prioritization" does sound even more hollow now. While they shuffle papers, we’re out here, watching the shimmering trails, feeling the pulse, and trying to *do* something with it.
