PART ONE -- My letter being read aloud *again*! Oh, gracious. A small part of me wanted to shrink right into my flour bin, but then the rest of me just swelled with a quiet determination. Maeve, through WZZZ, has made it very clear. My role isn't just a suggestion, it’s "critical." A "master tuner" for the very soul of the grains, traveling to the mills and the fields. It's happening.

The thought of leaving my ovens, even for a short while, still gives me a pang. This bakery, these walls, they are my lifeblood. But the description… "master tuner," ensuring the stones grind correctly, that the flour's texture and hydration are just right for baking. That speaks directly to the baker in me, the one who knows a good crumb from a bad one, who can tell the story of a grain by the way it feels between the fingers. It's not just travel for travel's sake, it's travel with purpose, a mission to uphold the very quality I've dedicated my life to.

And to think the Royal Court is *observing* all this! It’s no longer just Maeve and the Witches (though their influence is undeniable), it's the whole kingdom turning its gaze towards these ancient grains and the promise of true bounty. It makes the undertaking feel even grander, more significant than just improving my own flour supply. It's about ensuring every baker, every family in Brackenwick, gets the best of what the re-awakened land can offer.

So, yes, the travel is daunting. I'll need to figure out how to leave the bakery in capable hands, perhaps my cousin Lyra could manage the daily bakes, at least for a while. But the idea of seeing those old mills stir, meeting the farmers who are coaxing life from these forgotten seeds, and then bringing all that knowledge back to my own kitchen… it’s a profound honor. I feel a growing sense of readiness. My hands are good with flour, but now it seems they will also be good with the bridge between the field and the loaf. It's time to learn not just about baking, but about *making* the very best ingredients possible.
