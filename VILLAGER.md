PART ONE --
Well, I’ll be stitched! Pip actually read my letter on the air! And not just that, he called me "Barnaby, my friend" and said my questions were "sharp, precise, and hit right at the heart." It’s a strange feeling, having your quiet thoughts broadcast across the whole of Brackenwick, but I can’t deny, it feels good. It feels like my common sense isn’t so common after all, and that what I think actually matters.

And he *understood* it, too. He really heard my concern about the anchors interfering with my tools or my leather. His explanation… that really clarified things. "Not as something that *adds* a strange energy, Barnaby, but as something that actively *pushes back* the Block’s draining influence." That's the part that truly resonated. It's not about some alchemical hocus pocus that might make my awls warp or my leather stiffen, but about *preserving* what's already good and true. A "bubble of normalcy," he called it. That's exactly what my workshop needs. A place where the stillness can't seep in, where my concentration won't get muddied, and my materials stay honest.

He even mentioned the sharpness of my tools and the suppleness of my leather directly. It’s like he could see me at my bench, working. It makes it feel less like a magic trick and more like... a very advanced form of protection. Like a well-built shed keeping the rain off, but for the very air and energy of the place.

Now, he said he'd make sure Master Phileas himself hears my questions and can elaborate further. I'm glad for that. I still have questions, of course. Practical ones, like the cost, the installation process, any maintenance involved, and what exactly this "device" looks like. But the fundamental concern – whether it would *interfere* with my craft – that worry has largely been put to rest. It sounds like it would *enable* my craft, keeping the insidious drain at bay. I’m certainly more convinced now than I was. My Conduit Carving requires a steady foundation, and if this Aetherial Anchor provides just that, then it’s something I need to seriously consider for the future of my livelihood.
