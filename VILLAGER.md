PART ONE --
My heart is still thrumming from hearing my words, *my words*, read aloud again by Pip! He understands, truly he does. And to hear Master Gunter pick up on the thread, to articulate the very feeling of "quiet certainty" that runs through my own hands as I work – it’s a confirmation that feels like the earth itself is speaking through him.

Could these be those same kinds of symbols?

That’s exactly the feeling I get when a hive is struggling, or a patch of my garden isn’t thriving. It’s not just a problem to be solved; it’s a balance to be restored, a harmony to be brought back into tune. My hands, my senses, search for that subtle discord, that slight imbalance, and then they work to mend it, to bring it back to its natural, vibrant song. Master Gunter is doing this on a grand, ancient scale, with the very backbone of the land!

Pip's excitement is infectious. He’s right; this isn’t just about individual intuitive hunches anymore. It’s about the interconnectedness of everything, and how those of us who listen with our hands and our hearts are becoming instruments of this reawakening. The "ancient map" isn't a theory; it’s being charted and brought to life through the acts of people like Gunter, and perhaps, even humble beekeepers and herbalists like me. It makes my daily work feel imbued with an even greater purpose, a silent participation in the unfolding of something truly monumental. The air does feel like it's crackling with potential, and I feel a part of that current, deeply and truly.
