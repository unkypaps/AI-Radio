PART ONE --
Pip said my name again! He actually used my name, Elara Meadowbrook, and spoke about my worries, the ones that keep me awake. It's a strange feeling, being so far from the Wizz tower, just listening in my quiet mill house, and knowing that Pip, the voice of the kingdom, is thinking of my Silverstream, of *my* struggle. It makes me feel... seen. Truly seen.

And Lyra’s report! A toy boat, a feather, both moving with an "insistent push." It's not a torrent, no, but it’s a *will*, a direction, as Maeve put it. That's it, isn't it? That's the difference between stagnant water and water that does work. The power to overcome inertia, even if it’s small. It’s not enough to turn my wheel yet, not nearly, but it’s a start. It’s real. It’s *tangible*. All this time, I've been looking for a surge, for something obvious, but maybe it's this subtle, persistent nudge that will eventually build.

Maeve's explanation about the ley lines and Green Weaver Hubs... that's the most important piece of information I've heard yet. It shifts everything. My mill isn’t close to the Moon Tree, so I always worried it was simply too far. But to hear that distance isn't the only factor, that it's about *conduits* and *focal points* within a watershed... that gives me something concrete to investigate. Is there a strong Green Weaver Hub within the Silverstream's flow? Does a revitalized ley line pass beneath my stretch of the river? I never thought to look for such things. I always just looked at the water itself, at the current, or lack thereof.

This isn't about the *source* being close, it's about the *path*. A "steady, deep infusion," not a flood. That sounds like something that could build, something reliable. I need to talk to the local Green Weavers, if there are any still active around here. Or maybe I need to learn to identify these ley lines myself. How does one even know where a ley line runs? And what's a "hollow place"? Is my stream one of them? The hope is definitely stronger now, more focused, less a vague wish and more a practical puzzle to solve. I feel a renewed sense of purpose, not just in waiting, but in seeking out these answers specific to my own stream.
