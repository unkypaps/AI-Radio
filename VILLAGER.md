PART ONE -- Pip said my name! Elara Hearthstone, the miller from Silverstream! My heart nearly stopped when I heard it, thrumming louder than the mill's own great stone. To hear my observation, my honest sight, spoken aloud for all of Brackenwick to hear, and to be held up as a witness… it’s a feeling I can barely describe. It’s like the finest grain, milled perfectly, pure and true.

And it comes at such a moment. I was just settling into the idea of keeping a quiet record, thinking it would be a personal discipline, like noting the river's flow or the mill's wear. Now, Pip is calling for those very records! It’s as if the currents of the kingdom are aligning with my own small efforts. It’s no longer just a private practice; it’s a contribution, a piece of the tapestry Pip spoke of.

I remember seeing those glowing, swirling patterns beneath the water's surface in Madam Elara's pool. It was undeniable. Not a trick of the light, but a vibrant energy, like watching the sap rise in a tree in spring, but in water. To know that this vision, my vision, is now part of the kingdom's understanding, part of the argument for the "Kingdom's Kneading," it gives my work a new purpose. It makes my senses feel sharper, more important.

The Royal Court… they’re a distant force to me, usually concerned with decrees and taxes, far removed from the daily turning of the millstone. But if my simple observation can help them see, can help them understand the real changes happening on the ground, then it feels like I’m truly doing my part. It’s like clearing a blockage in the sluice gate, allowing the water to flow freely again.

I still don't fully grasp what this "Kingdom's Kneading" expansion truly means, but if it's about acknowledging these profound shifts, about moving Brackenwick forward in a way that truly understands the land and its new energies, then I want to be a part of it. My mill is built to adapt, to harness the river's power. Perhaps the kingdom needs to do the same. My stone and my breath will anchor me, yes, but now they will also be the wellspring from which I draw these observations, these quiet records. This is bigger than me, bigger than Silverstream, but I feel my connection to it growing stronger, like a deep root finding fertile soil. I will start my record tonight.
