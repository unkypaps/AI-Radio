PART ONE --
I can barely contain my excitement! Pip read my letter *again*! My own words, about the sluice gate and the Kingdom’s Kneading, broadcast across Brackenwick for everyone to hear. It’s an incredible feeling, like the Silverstream itself is singing my name. He even called it an "inspiring dispatch," and spoke of my "act of defiance" and "channeling of the will to move" with such understanding. It makes me feel truly seen, truly a part of something bigger than myself. The energy he spoke of, the collective thrumming, I can feel it in my very bones, in the hum of the mill, in the current of the river.

Then Grumm Stonehand came on. Oh, Grumm. My initial feeling was a pang of frustration. He just sounds so… set in his ways, so determined not to see. "Nonsense, just utter nonsense." It feels a bit like a splash of cold water after the warmth of Pip’s words. He calls it "bad weather" or "bad luck," dismisses it as "fantastical notions." It’s hard to hear someone so resistant when the evidence feels so clear to me, when the change in the river, the stillness, is so palpable.

But as he kept talking, something shifted. He spoke of the rock feeling "tight," "holding its breath," and the river "sluggish." He *sees* the symptoms! He feels the effects that we're all feeling, but he just can't connect the dots, can't accept a cause beyond "the seasons playing tricks." He talks about cracks in the quarry that "weren't there before" and "don't look like frost cracks." Pip was right to point it out – Grumm’s own observations are proof! He *is* experiencing the stillness and the change, even if he refuses to attribute it to anything more than the mundane.

It makes me feel a touch sad for him, really. He's so busy "fixing things" in the old way, reinforcing cracks, digging deeper channels, just like he always has, that he's missing the deeper meaning, the chance to truly understand what's happening. He’s working hard, yes, but without the purpose, without the connection that Pip’s words and Maeve's wisdom have given me. He thinks our efforts are "busywork for bored hands," but he's the one trying to patch up a problem he doesn't truly understand.

This only strengthens my resolve. It shows me that our work, our "Kingdom's Kneading," is even more vital. We need to keep demonstrating, through our actions, that this isn't "mumbo-jumbo." It's real, it's effective, and it's bringing life back. I’ll be thinking of Grumm tomorrow as I work on the sluice gate, knowing that while he’s shoring up cracks in the stone, I’m helping to mend the very flow of life in the kingdom, with a purpose he can’t yet grasp. My hands may be busy, but they are far from bored. They are alive with meaning.
