PART ONE --
Well, I'll be dipped in flour. Pip read my letter! My letter! Didn’t think it would even make it past the static, let alone be read out to the whole blinking kingdom. I nearly dropped my rolling pin when I heard my own name. "Barnaby 'Barney' Buttercup." Pip even got the 'Barney' bit right. Felt a bit like shouting down a well, I said, and blast it if he didn’t echo it back. Made me feel… heard. Like I wasn’t just muttering to myself and old Betsy.

The quiet, though. He’s right, it’s choking. My latest batch of rye isn't quite right. It's dense, almost unwilling to rise. I kept poking at it this morning, willing it to spring, but it just sat there, heavy and sullen, like the air itself. Martha always said my bread had a bit of my own stubborn spirit in it, but even my spirit feels a bit worn thin these days.

And the Royal Court. Pip understood, he really did. The squirrels! It’s such an insult, such a waste of good, honest work and coin. While folks like me are trying to keep our livelihoods afloat, they're fussing over furry little thieves. Makes my blood boil, it does.

But then… then he said it. "The narrative requires more than just acorns." A Royal Cartographer. Someone on the inside, actually *doing* their job, risking something. Well, I’ll be. I’d tarred them all with the same brush, I admit. Figured they were all just as useless as the Duke himself. But a secret schematic, precise and coded… that's not squirrel food, is it? That’s *work*. Good work. Elara Meadowlight, she sounds like a straight shooter, and for someone to get that to her… it’s a sliver, isn’t it? A tiny little sliver of light in all this gloom. I don't pretend to understand "anomalous geomagnetic fluctuations" or "ley lines," but the idea that someone's actually charting the *real* danger, not just where the Duke's next pampered pet is going to bury its nuts… that’s something.

It doesn't make the Block any less real, or the quiet less suffocating, but it makes it feel less… hopeless. Like maybe we're not just waiting for the whole thing to come crashing down. Maybe someone, somewhere, is still trying to hold it up. Maybe my sourdough isn’t completely doomed after all. Still angry at the Duke, mind you, but not at *everyone*. Not entirely.
