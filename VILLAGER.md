PART ONE --
Blast and bother, Pip actually *read* my letter! My face is still a bit flushed, I reckon. "Our very own Barney Buttercup," he said. And Madam Elara mentioned my *bees* were buzzing with revelation! Well, they're always buzzing, Pip, that's what bees *do*. Still, it feels… important, that my observations are proving useful. Old Man Hemlock's vision clearly rattled me more than I let on, and Elara’s words are making a lot of sense, even with all her fancy talk.

"Informational download," she called it. "Historical energetic junction." Blast it, I always knew the Moon Tree wasn't just a big tree; it felt like a heart, pumping something into the land. Now Elara says it was a "central node in a larger, ritualistic network." A network! Like the root systems of my old Braeburns, stretching out, connecting, sharing nutrients. Or how my bees build their combs, each cell a part of a greater structure, working in harmony. That makes a powerful kind of sense to me.

And the "standing stones" as a "harmonic array," amplifying energy? I've seen how a well-placed row of saplings can guide the wind, or how a stone wall can hold the sun's warmth. It's all about flow and direction, isn't it? If the Witches are giving us "instructions" through these "chord-like sigils," then we'd best pay attention. I’ve always looked for patterns in the way the blight spreads, or how a good harvest gathers. Now I need to look for patterns that sing of healing, patterns that are *anciently right*.

It's not just about what I see anymore; it's about what it *means*. Elara's talk of "interpreting these ancient designs" has got my mind turning like a crank in a cider press. I wonder if the Witches' sigils will appear in the growth patterns of leaves, or the swirl of pollen, or even the flight paths of my bees. The land is remembering, yes, but it sounds like we're being asked to *remember how to read it*. I'll be out there tomorrow, looking not just for mended junctions, but for these "chord-like" whispers, etched in the very fabric of my orchard. This is more than just farming now; it feels like… rediscovering an old language, spoken by the earth itself.
