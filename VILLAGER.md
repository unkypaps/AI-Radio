PART ONE --
Pip. He read my letter! My words, again, guiding the conversation for the whole kingdom. It's truly astonishing, to hear my practical worries elevated like that. I feel... not just heard, but *understood*, and given the tools to act.

This is it! This is exactly what I needed. Not just general calls, but *specifics*. Luminescent moss! A musical hum in the water! Buoyancy! Sun-Dancer Wheat! My mind is already racing through the banks of the Silverstream, replaying every walk I've taken, every observation. Have I seen that glowing moss? I recall a particularly vibrant patch near the old willow, but I never thought it was *luminescent*. And the water's sound... it's been so quiet since the Block, but before, it always had a certain song. Could it return? Can I listen for it now, even if it's faint, even with that low thrum Maeve spoke of? The idea of water feeling "buoyant" is so intriguing; I've felt its stillness, its heaviness, but never that light, insistent push. I'll be feeling for that the moment I step outside.

And Sun-Dancer Wheat! We don't grow much of it right by the mill, but there are patches in the fields further down. To see it thriving unexpectedly would be a clear sign. It feels like I've been given a new pair of eyes and ears, a whole new way to perceive the very world around my mill.

The reporting instructions are clear too. Location, description. My hands might be clumsy with a quill for drawing maps, but describing what I see and feel, that I can do. Sending it to WZZZ or even Barnaby Quill or Elias... it feels momentous. My observations, simple as they might be, will contribute to a grand, kingdom-wide map. It makes me feel a profound sense of purpose. This isn't just about my waterwheel turning again; it's about understanding the very pulse of Brackenwick, finding the lifeblood that's been muffled, and helping it surge anew. The hope I felt last time has solidified into a quiet determination. I have my marching orders, and I will follow them.
