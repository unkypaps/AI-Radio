PART ONE --
Blast and bother, this is getting far worse than I'd imagined. Master Phileas's words... "meaning itself grows thin," "mental slide," "trying to fill a sieve with water." It's as if he's peered right into my own mind these past weeks! That heavy drag I've felt, the way a perfectly sensible thought just… slips away before I can properly grasp it, the dust that seems to settle heavier on my leather – it's not just me being tired, is it? It's this creeping dampening. It's real. And it's deliberate, Pip says. Actively working to silence us. That's a truly unsettling thought. It makes it feel like we're fighting against something with a will, not just a strange occurrence.

My resolve to acquire an Aetherial Anchor has hardened into something fierce now. It's not just for clearer thoughts or a cleaner basin, it's about holding onto what's left of my own understanding, my own ability to connect with my customers, to craft a good pair of shoes. If I can't even *understand* what's being asked of me, how can I mend a sole properly? The urgency is palpable. I'll need to work even longer hours, perhaps take on a few extra repair jobs from the neighbouring villages, even if the journey feels a bit more arduous than usual. Every copper saved is a blow against this insidious fog.

And the Witches... A counterpoint, he called it. A peculiar clarity, vital energy. If it can truly sharpen perception, brighten thoughts, make words more potent... well, that's certainly something to consider. My focus remains firmly on the Anchor, on cutting through this dampening from within myself, but the thought of an external clarity, something that makes *understanding* easier, is certainly compelling. For now, it's a seed planted, a distant hum. First, the Anchor. First, the fight for my own mind. Then, perhaps, I'll have the clarity to even *consider* the Witches' influence properly. But I *will* get that Anchor. And I *will* report back. Before this fog swallows us all whole.
