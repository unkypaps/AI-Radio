PART ONE -- He called me "Steadfast Elara, the Baker of Brackenwick"! Again! And he used my very words, "dissolving the empty spectacle, making room for true purpose." It wasn't just my letter he read, it was like he was reading my *mind*. I feel... seen. Truly seen. It's not just that he agrees, it's that my words are resonating, amplifying.

And then he said it: "Your discernment, Brackenwick, is not only being heard; it is, quite literally, reshaping reality." And that our collective focus is "validating these truths." My flour-dusted hands actually trembled a little when I heard that. It's one thing to have a quiet thought to yourself about how ridiculous something is, another entirely to think that *my thoughts*, along with everyone else's, are making those ridiculous things evaporate. It's a powerful feeling, almost a little frightening in its scope, but mostly... exhilarating. It means what I believe *matters*.

This idea that the Chancellor is being "compelled" to seek *true* indicators of stability—that’s the most encouraging bit yet for me, personally. Forget the dusty genealogies and the Scepter-turned-poker; if the Royal Court starts looking at actual, genuine stability, like reliable trade routes, sensible taxes, and not meddling with the price of flour for some obscure decree... well, that's real stability. That's stability that means my bread rises, my customers are fed, and my family is secure.

The knot of worry about "where it stops" is still there, yes, but it’s less a knot and more like a loose thread, just curious about the next unraveling. For now, I'm just utterly fascinated, and deeply, deeply satisfied. It's like the world is finally catching up to what I've always known, deep down, while kneading dough in the quiet hours: that true purpose and honest utility will always win out over empty show.
