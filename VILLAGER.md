PART ONE --
Pip. He said my name again. Not just mentioned my letter, but *my name*. Elara Meadowbrook. And he's using my questions to guide the whole kingdom's efforts. It's… overwhelming, in the best possible way. It feels like the air itself is buzzing with purpose, and I'm not just a lonely miller wishing for water, but a part of something grand, something that might actually *work*.

This is it. This is the practical step I've been craving. Not just "feel the flow" or "wait and see," but a directive: "map and report." Maeve's insights are finally being put into action, and my worries, my specific worries about how to *find* these ley lines, are being addressed for everyone. It makes perfect sense. We can’t just hope for the water to come; we need to understand *how* it will come, and where.

Elias the Royal Cartographer! Even the Royal Cartographer is getting involved. That adds a layer of seriousness, of official endorsement, that makes this feel even more real. This isn't just talk; it's a coordinated effort. I picture him hunched over maps, his quills flying, trying to make sense of what we're all seeing.

So, the next step is mapping. But *how*? That's still the lingering question. I can walk the Silverstream, I can look for unusual growths, for patches of vibrant green, for that 'hollow feeling' Maeve mentioned, but what does one *actually* report? Is it just a description? Do I need to learn to draw maps myself? My hands are more used to flour and wood than ink and parchment. But I'll do it. I'll figure it out. This isn't just for my mill anymore, it's for everyone, for the whole flow of the kingdom. The hope is no longer a tiny ember; it’s a steady, growing flame.
