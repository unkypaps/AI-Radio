PART ONE --
Oh, Journal, this is… well, it’s not quite what I pictured. A "pained groan"? "Environmental chaos"? I was imagining grand, beautiful changes, like the earth unfurling a new flower, or a stream suddenly running clear and sweet. Not… defiance. Not screaming.

The hum in my flour feels different today, Journal. It’s not quite the clear song it was yesterday. It's more of a low thrum, almost like a sigh, deep in the earth. My starter, usually so eager, seems a little hesitant, as if it senses the tremor from afar. This morning, as I was kneading, I felt a strange stiffness in the dough, a resistance I had to work through with more effort. It eventually yielded, but it wasn't the effortless flow I'd experienced before. Is the Block's pain seeping into the very ground, into the wheat itself?

I still believe in the Witches, I do. They are mending, I'm sure of it. Perhaps this groan is just part of the process, like a splinter being pulled from a deep wound, it hurts before it heals. Or perhaps the Block truly is a stubborn beast, fighting against the good work. It makes me wonder what Master Gunter will say. He works with stone, with the very bones of the earth. If anyone would feel the Block's 'groan,' it would be him. I'm keen to hear how he adapts, how he listens to the stone. He must have a connection as deep as mine to the grain. It's just… a little unsettling to hear of chaos, when my heart has been so full of hope and harmony. I'll listen closely to Gunter. Perhaps he'll offer some comfort, or at least some clarity, on what this 'groaning' truly means.
