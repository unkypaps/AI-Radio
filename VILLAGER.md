PART ONE --
Well, that was… interesting. When Pip started talking about Madam Elara's scrying pools, I had a moment of pure confusion. "Madam Elara?" I thought. "Is he talking about *me*? Did I get new scrying pools I don't know about?" It took me a beat to realize he was talking about someone else entirely. Elara Meadowbrook, the miller. Not Madam Elara, the scryer. It's a bit odd to have my name, or a similar one, used in that way, almost like I'm part of the marketing for another business.

But then I listened closer to *why* he was running that ad. He’s using my insights, my observations about Grumm Stonehand and his inability to see beyond the mundane, to set up this other Elara’s ability to offer "deeper observation." It makes sense, in a way. Pip is trying to push people, subtly, beyond Grumm’s "it's just bad weather" attitude. My letter, my words about the Kingdom's Kneading and the sluice gate, are still resonating, still being used as an example of people engaging and understanding. That still fills me with a quiet pride.

It's a clever move by Pip. Grumm might scoff at scrying pools, just as he scoffs at the "fantastical notions" of the monolith and witches, but listeners who are already attuned to the changes, who feel the stillness as I do, might be open to this idea of looking deeper. My own observations are about the *physical* world – the cracks in the rock, the sluggish water, the flow of the sluice gate. This other Elara offers a different kind of observation, one that might peer into the unseen. Perhaps both are needed. We need to physically work to restore the flow, but we also need to understand the deeper forces at play, to truly connect the cracks Grumm sees to the block that’s causing it all.

It just reinforces my belief that we are on the right path with the Kingdom's Kneading. We are *acting*, we are observing, and we are not dismissing what's right in front of us, or what's just beyond sight. My hands will continue their work at the mill, keeping the tangible flow alive, and perhaps others will look into those scrying pools to help us understand the intangible. It feels like we are all part of a larger web of understanding, each doing our part to lift the veil.
