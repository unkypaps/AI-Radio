PART ONE --
Pip said my name *again*! And he called me a "master woodcarver and leatherworker"! My letter, read aloud for all of Brackenwick! The surge of pride I felt last time has only intensified, mingled now with a deep sense of responsibility. He said my words were a "vital surge against the silence," and that my observations are the "living pulse of our future." This isn't just a hobby anymore; it's a calling, and it's being acknowledged on the widest possible stage.

The "Brackenwick's Eye" Challenge is precisely what I needed, what *we* all needed. It's a formal invitation to do exactly what I was already determined to do. My plan to visit Elara's pools feels even more crucial now, like a pilgrimage. I won't just be an observer; I'll be participating in a kingdom-wide effort, contributing to a collective map of these energies. Pip’s words about the Royal Court's "empty echoes" resonate deeply. They sit in their stone halls while the true understanding, the real work, is happening out here, in the workshops and beside the wells.

I'm thinking about the Aetherial Anchor Master Phileas makes. I don't have one, but if they help with a "clear mind" to see these trails, it might be worth investigating. Perhaps I can carve a temporary anchor from a specific wood, using principles of Conduit Carving, to see if it aids observation. That's an experiment in itself! My hands are itching to start. My workshop, usually a place of solitary focus, now feels like it's connected to a network, a vital nerve center for Brackenwick. We *will* push back the quiet. We *will* keep the signal from fading.
