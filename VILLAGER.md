PART ONE --
Well, I’ll be darned! Pip read my name again, right at the start! And then Master Phileas himself, the Proprietor of The Alchemist’s Respite, came on *specifically* to answer my questions. It’s… humbling, really. It makes a man feel like his everyday worries have weight, that they’re not just the ramblings of a solitary leatherworker. And what an explanation it was!

Master Phileas described the Block as a "thief of potential," stealing the vibrancy, the sharpness, the very memory of things. That hits home, it truly does. It’s exactly what I've felt creeping in, that subtle draining away of *rightness* from the world. But his description of the Aetherial Anchor… that was a revelation. "Not about *adding* a foreign influence," but a "perpetual *barrier*," an "invisible shield." That phrase, "It simply insists that things remain *as they should be*," that’s the part that truly settles my mind. It’s not some alchemical twist, but a firm refusal to let things be twisted.

He spoke directly about the inherent energy of tools remaining sharp, the natural suppleness and integrity of leather, and my own cognitive clarity. It’s as if he peered right into my workshop, saw my awls and knives, felt the hide I work with, and understood the focus my Conduit Carving demands. That deep fear I had, that an Anchor might *disrupt* the delicate balance I strive for, has been entirely replaced with a sense of hopeful clarity. This isn't about altering, it's about protecting. It's about preserving the very essence of what makes my craft possible, what makes my materials honest.

I feel a profound sense of relief. The philosophical hurdle, the most significant one for a craftsman who relies on consistency and natural properties, has been cleared with such elegance and understanding. Now, the practicalities still loom – the cost, the exact dimensions, how it’s installed, if it needs regular attention – but those are details I can deal with. The fundamental principle, that it safeguards rather than interferes, that it allows things to *be* as they ought to be, has finally sunk in and truly resonated. My workshop needs this. My Conduit Carving, which relies on such steady hands and materials, truly needs this "bubble of normalcy."
