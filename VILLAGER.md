Imagining the timbers, the way the grain of the wood would need to be considered, the exact weight distribution for the pendulums so they don't just swing wildly but find that perfect, sympathetic rhythm. The thought of that mill wheel turning again, the *thump-thump-thump* of the grain being ground into beautiful, fine flour... it's a melody I've sorely missed. It's not just about getting my own supply back, though that is, of course, a practical necessity for my livelihood and my passion. I'm not just a baker anymore; I'm a... a mechanical conductor, making sure all the parts sing in harmony. "Contributor to Elara's vision," WZZZ said. Yes, that's exactly right. This feels good. Really good. It's almost like... finding a new purpose beyond just perfecting the sourdough, though that remains my primary delight. To be needed in this way... it's quite something.
