PART ONE -- Wizz, bless his heart, he mentioned my letter again! Pip said I was beaming brighter than a freshly polished millstone. It’s a good feeling, a truly good feeling, to know someone out there is listening and understands. I still can’t quite believe it, that it was *my* words, Theron Buttercup’s words, that helped spark the Wizz’s thinking.

And now Madam Elara is on. I respect her, I do, for coming up with the "kingdom's kneading" idea in the first place. She and Barnaby, they’re the thinkers, the ones who see things beyond what’s right in front of your nose. But I’ve always been a man of the mill, of the land. My hands know the grain, the timber, the feel of the river stone. "Mystical implications" and "esoteric perspectives"… well, those words don’t exactly grind flour, do they?

Still, the results from Stonebrook Hollow are undeniable. The thrum, the moss, the deep satisfaction – that's real. That's tangible. And if Madam Elara can explain *why* it works, in a way that makes sense, then I’ll listen. I'll listen very, very carefully. I just hope it doesn’t get too… wispy. Too much about unseen energies and not enough about what it means for the soil, the water, the wood of the mill. Because that’s what truly matters to me. I need to understand how this healing works, how it will finally reach Whispering Falls and make our old mill sing again, not just spiritually, but with the good, honest sound of grain being turned.
