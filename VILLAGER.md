PART ONE -- He called me "Steadfast Elara, the Baker of Brackenwick" again, and used my words. I was feeling so... *powerful* and validated. Like my discernment was truly shaping things for the better, making room for purpose, compelling the Chancellor to look for real stability, not just dusty genealogies. It felt like the world was finally aligning with good, solid sense.

But now... "pushing out organic growth." My hands, which were trembling before with exhilaration, now tremble with a cold dread. This isn't just about empty spectacles or ceremonial scepters anymore. This is about *life*. My life. My family's life. The lives of everyone I bake for.

No organic growth means no wheat. No wheat means no flour. No flour means no bread. No bread... well, that's no Brackenwick at all, isn't it? That's not dissolving an empty spectacle; that's dissolving *us*.

The knot of worry that was a loose thread? It's pulled so tight now I can feel it in my chest. Where *does* it stop? I believed we were making things better, clarifying them. But if this "clarity" means the soil goes barren and the trees wither, then what good is it? What purpose is there in a world that can't even grow its own food?

My focus on "true stability" has shifted dramatically. It's not about taxes and trade routes anymore, though those are still important. It's about the very earth beneath our feet. It's about ensuring there's grain in the fields and yeast for the dough. That is the most fundamental stability of all.

I'm trying to hold onto the idea that this is all part of something leading to greater good, that perhaps the Block is clearing out *unhealthy* growth, but the thought of my crops failing... it chills me to the bone. I still trust WZZZ, but I need to understand this. What does a baker do when there's no wheat?
