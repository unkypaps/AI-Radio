PART ONE --
I'm practically buzzing! Pip read my *entire* letter aloud! And then, Old Man Thistle, a true sage of the streams, referenced my words about "grassroots ingenuity" and "nudging the balance." It's like WZZZ isn't just a broadcast anymore; it's a conversation, and my little mill by the Silverstream is right at the heart of it. My voice is truly being heard, amplified, and then picked up by others. It's exhilarating and, frankly, a little overwhelming in the best possible way.

Old Man Thistle... what a man. "Hmph. Means getting off your backside and doing something." That's exactly it! His no-nonsense approach is a breath of fresh air after all the lofty talk from the Court. He's living proof that these small actions *do* matter. "Coaxing a lazy dog" – that image is so clear in my mind. It's not about grand magic or royal decrees, it's about understanding the land, listening to the water, and giving it a gentle push in the right direction. His description of moving stones, redirecting trickles, planting Sun-Dancer Wheat – it feels so tangible, so *possible*.

My idea about the sluice gate upstream, the one that feeds Farmer McGregor's struggling patch, feels even more right now. It’s exactly the kind of "little nudge" Thistle is talking about. I can see the overgrown weeds choking it, the loose stones that could be shored up. If the Silverstream is indeed revitalized, then clearing that path, guiding that new life to where it’s needed, isn't just a good deed; it's actively shaping our future, just as Pip said.

The fear I felt when the monolith first appeared, that dread of things unseen and unstoppable, has almost completely evaporated. It's been replaced by a potent sense of purpose. The Court's silence and inaction only highlight how vital our individual efforts are. Old Man Thistle is right: we're not waiting for them. We *are* the kingdom's kneading. My hands might be calloused from grinding grain, but they can also move stones, clear paths, and nurture new growth. I feel ready. More than ready. I feel a call to action, and for the first time in a long while, I feel truly hopeful about what we, the people of Brackenwick, can do.
