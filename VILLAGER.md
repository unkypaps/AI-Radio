PART ONE -- My heart still has that warmth from last time, knowing WZZZ sees the value in what I believe. I was so looking forward to Maeve, to hearing someone else speak to the practical, grounded wisdom of the earth. But then… well.

"Madam Elara's Seer's Eye Scrying Pool Rentals." My name. Or, part of it. My heart nearly stopped. *I* am Elara Meadowsweet, the baker. I’ve never seen a scrying pool in my life, let alone rented one out! I find clarity in flour and water, in the smell of yeast, in the warmth of the oven, not in gazing into a bowl of water! It’s startling, hearing my name associated with something so… well, so *un-me*. I hope WZZZ doesn't confuse me with this "Madam Elara." It’s important to me that they understand where my beliefs truly lie – in the tangible, the nourishing, the real.

And then Borin Stonehand’s dispatch. That’s truly unsettling. "Geometric spiral patterns" on metal? A "pulse"? And a "deliberate scrambling" of thought? That’s much worse than a simple hum or fog. It sounds… *aggressive*. It makes my hands feel a bit unsteady, thinking about it. How am I supposed to knead dough properly if my thoughts are being scrambled, if I can’t trust what I see or feel? Baking, truly good baking, relies on instinct, on feeling the texture, on knowing the precise moment something is ready. If this block is twisting reality itself, twisting our perceptions, it’s a direct attack on the very foundations of practical skill and intuition. It makes the gentle wisdom of the witches, the clarity I felt was blooming, seem all the more precious, and all the more threatened.

WZZZ is right; we have to fight this. But for me, it's not with scrying pools or court protocols. It’s by holding even tighter to the genuine, the tangible, the nourishing. By focusing on the craft, on the ingredients, on the familiar rhythms of the oven. It’s by finding clarity right here, in the kitchen, despite the static trying to pull us away. I still hope Maeve's interview can offer some of that grounded reassurance.
