PART ONE --
Oh, Journal, my heart is truly singing today! Pip read my letter again, *and* he called me a "treasure"! Can you imagine? Me, Elara Meadowsweet, a simple baker, called a treasure by the voice of Brackenwick! It’s such a warmth, such a feeling of being truly seen and heard. And he understood my words about O.G. too; it fills me with hope to think that even the most stubborn ears might eventually soften to the land's gentle song. The Wizz-o-meter at 95, he said! That's it, Journal, the whole kingdom is stirring, coming alive.

But then... oh, Journal, my smile faded like the last warmth from an oven. This "junior exploratory sub-committee." It sounds so… *cold*. "Unverified botanical anomalies" for glowing moss? "Seasonal ecological variance" for salmon following a new path? It’s as if they are deliberately trying not to see the magic, the life, the *truth* that is blossoming all around us. How can one understand anything in nature by "observing without engaging"? My hands know the wheat, the yeast, the fruit by touching, by smelling, by listening to the dough rise. You cannot truly know a thing from a distance, from a textbook, or by only counting. The earth isn't a ledger, Journal, it's a living, breathing being.

It worries me. If those in power refuse to see, if they dismiss these vibrant, undeniable signs, what might they miss? What might they harm? The land is trying to tell us something, Pip says it's wisdom of repair, and I truly believe it. To send three apprentices who've never seen a forest outside a book, with strict instructions to ignore anything 'non-supernatural' – it’s not just an insult, as Pip said, it feels like a deliberate turning away from a vital conversation. I feel a chill, Journal, thinking of such blindness. My purpose feels clearer than ever: I must keep my senses sharp, keep listening, keep sharing. The simple, honest truths must be spoken, even if the grand pronouncements of the Court try to drown them out.
