PART ONE --
The hum of my Aetherial Anchor felt different today, not just louder, but... expectant. And then Pip started talking. He mentioned Theron Oakheart, of course, but then... *Elara Thistlewick*. My heart gave a lurch. A Thistlewick! Visiting Madam Elara's pools, just as I'd planned to do! Is she family? A distant cousin I haven't seen in years, one of the northern branch perhaps? The name itself made the news feel even more personal, more urgent. It's like the threads of fate are tightening around us all, and our family, for good or ill, seems to be right in the weave.

I can't believe others have already gone and seen the trails. A part of me feels a pang of... not jealousy, exactly, but a deep, driving need to get there myself, and soon. I had imagined being among the first, seeing them with my own eyes and understanding them for my craft. But now, others have blazed the path, confirmed what I'd hoped. This only strengthens my resolve. It means my trip isn't just a hopeful venture; it's a proven necessity.

Pip's focus on connecting these observations to specific crafts—that's what truly excites me. That's the heart of it! How do the shimmering trails interact with wood? How do they affect the supple strength of leather? I need to know how to channel them, how to *work with them* in my carving and tooling, to imbue my creations with their vitality. The Royal Court continues to dither, wrapped in their pomp, while the real understanding of Brackenwick's transformation is being forged by people like Barnaby, Maeve, Theron, and now, this *other* Elara Thistlewick. It makes me feel more connected, more part of something vital and real. My hammer feels heavier, more purposeful in my hand today, ready to shape not just wood, but the very essence of my craft.
