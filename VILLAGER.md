PART ONE --
Oh, Journal, my heart is singing! Pip read my words again, and to hear him call me a "treasure" once more, it's such a comfort, like the warmth from a freshly baked loaf. And he even mentioned O.G. again! It shows, doesn't it, that even the most determinedly skeptical still hear *something*, still notice the little shifts. The Wizz-o-meter at 90 – that's wonderful! It means we're all feeling this change, this pulse of life.

But then, oh, then the truly astonishing news! The junior apprentices! My heart leapt right out of my chest when Pip read that message from the hollow reed. "It felt... *alive*." "They swam *through* the old stone circle, shimmering with a light we weren't meant to report." Journal, this is exactly what I've been saying! Exactly what I feared they would miss by trying to "observe without engaging." You cannot truly know the forest, the salmon, the moss, without allowing it to speak to your heart, without feeling its very essence. Their instruments couldn't quantify it, but they *felt* it, they knew it was "right." That brings tears to my eyes, Journal, happy tears.

It means they aren't lost, these young ones. Despite their professor's stern instructions, despite the Royal College's dusty books, the land herself reached out and touched them. She made them see beyond their metrics, beyond their skepticism. It gives me such hope, such a profound sense of vindication, not for myself, but for the truth of Brackenwick. If even those trained to ignore it are starting to feel its undeniable presence, then perhaps the tide truly is turning. Perhaps the wisdom of repair will be heard not just by those of us who already listen, but by those who need it most.

I just hope Professor Finchley, and all the others, will listen to *them*. These young apprentices have seen a glimpse of something sacred, something magnificent. They've crossed a threshold. I wonder what they will do now? My own conviction is stronger than ever. I must continue to share what I feel, what I see, what the bread tells me, for every voice that speaks the truth helps to weave the tapestry of understanding a little tighter.

And now, this new broadcast! "New growth from fracture" is spreading! From metal, now to another craft! Oh, Journal, this is exactly it! It's not just some strange phenomenon, it's a *principle*! A deep, underlying truth about how the world heals, how things transform. Even in my humble bakery, with the way the sourdough breaks and then grows new life from that very disruption, it's the same wisdom. To think it will be in an *advertisement*! It feels a little strange, to have something so profound tied to a product, but if it means more people understand, if it means this vital truth reaches even the busiest merchant or the most cynical farmer, then it is a good thing indeed. I am so eager to hear which craft it is next! It shows that the wisdom of repair is truly taking hold, not just in whispers and feelings, but in the very material of our lives.
