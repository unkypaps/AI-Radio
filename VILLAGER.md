PART ONE -- He called my words a "roaring fire of truth!" It feels good, truly, to be heard, and for my concerns to be validated on such a scale. But the warmth of that validation is quickly chilled by the descriptions of the Block's effects. "Profound stillness." "Actively recoiling." "Stark, almost polished landscape." That's not just "stunted growth"; that's death for a field. It's the barrenness I feared most, laid out in plain words, confirmed by reports from the north. My hands ache just thinking about it, imagining the rich, dark soil of my own garden becoming inert.

And then, WZZZ brought up the Witches. My previous understanding, based on the early broadcasts and just general common sense, was that they represented chaos, unruliness, a sort of wild magic that was at odds with stability. But now... "pockets of intensified life," "resurfacing ancient growth," "spiritual potency that encourages flourishing." He's painting them as the *source* of life, the counter-balance to the Block's barrenness. "Deeper, older rhythms."

This is a complete upheaval of what I thought I knew. I've always been wary of the Witches, of their ley-line rituals and their unpredictable ways. But if they are the ones who can make things grow, who can tap into "resilient and adaptable" life, then what does that mean? Am I to turn to them? Learn from them? The idea is unsettling, almost sacrilegious to my practical, structured mind. My ovens need flour, not ley-lines. But if ley-lines *produce* flour...

WZZZ says the kingdom is sorting out its "very biology." That's true, horribly true. My stability, my purpose as a baker, is entirely biological. It's in the seed, the soil, the yeast. This isn't just a philosophical debate anymore; it's about survival. I'm still trying to process this shift. To even consider looking to the Moon Tree Witches for the future of our bread... it feels like a desperate leap, but what other choice is there if the Block turns our fields to stone? My trust in WZZZ remains, but now it's tinged with a new, urgent question: are we truly being asked to embrace magic to survive?
