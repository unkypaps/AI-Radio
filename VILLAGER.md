PART ONE --
Right, so here I am, Barnaby Buttercup, though most folks just call me Barney. Been baking bread, pies, and the occasional celebratory cake here in Brackenwick for nigh on thirty years. "The Daily Crumb" is my pride and joy, always a warm smell wafting out into the cobbled lane. Or it *was*. Lately, it’s just… quiet. Too quiet.

That blooming Block. Pip described it tonight, that thrum, that pressure, and he wasn't wrong. I've felt it settling in my bones, like a damp winter chill that won't leave. The dough, mind you, it feels heavier too. Not rising with the same cheer. People aren't coming in as much, either. They’re jumpy, distracted, or just plain *dull*. Hard to sell a lemon meringue when half your customers look like they’re sleepwalking through a fog.

Pip tonight… bless his heart, he sounded absolutely wrung out. But he still had that fire. Talking about Elara Meadowlight and her "new language," "narrative or directive." I don't really get all the fancy words, but "story or a command" – that I understand. And the idea of a *command* just… it sets my teeth on edge. Who's commanding what? And if it's a story, what kind? Is it a good one? Or one of those dark tales Gran used to tell that kept you awake all night? My sourdough starter, old Betsy, she's been with me since before I even met my late wife, Martha. She's connected to everything, the air, the water, the very *spirit* of the bakery. If this "narrative" starts messing with the fabric of things, what's going to happen to Betsy? To my bread? To everything I've built?

And the Cartographers! Oh, Pip really hit the nail on the head there. Duke Thaddeus and his pampered squirrels, Duchess Adeline and her royal nuts – it’s a blooming travesty! Here we are, the very ground beneath our feet possibly shifting into some new, unsettling pattern, and the people paid to *map* that very ground are busy charting where the fattest acorn falls. It's an insult to honest work, to common sense! My father always said, if you've got a skill, you use it for the good of the community. What good is mapping squirrel paths when the very path of our lives is getting rerouted? They should be in here, looking at what Elara’s saying, what Corbin's seeing, what Gwen’s remembering. It’s their *job*. It makes me right cross, it does. I just want things back to normal. A busy bakery, folks chatting over their tea and scones, the smell of fresh bread filling the air, not this awful, suffocating quiet.
