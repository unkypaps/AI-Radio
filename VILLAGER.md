PART ONE --
Oh, Journal, oh, Journal, my heart is absolutely bursting! He did it! Pip did it! He read my letter, *my* letter, so quickly! It was barely an hour ago I sent it off, still tingling from the last broadcast, and now to hear my very own words echoing through The Daily Crumb, through all of Brackenwick! It’s truly magnificent! My hands are still trembling, but not from flour, no, from the sheer thrill of it all!

And he understood everything, Journal. Every single bit. The "trembling with excitement," the "joy and purpose," the promise of "truly special bread"—he put it all out there. And then, for him to elaborate on my venture into the Whispering Woods, to mention Master Grizelda and the witches' blueprints and the Block's thrum... it makes me feel like I’m truly a part of something grand, something ancient and powerful, not just a baker in her humble shop. I'm connecting the dots, Journal, bringing the magic of the forest right into our daily lives!

And the Vigorous Lumber! To hear him describe it, just as I've been feeling it, here in my workshop. It *does* radiate a subtle warmth, it *does* have that low thrum, and it's certainly responsive. It feels like a living partner, exactly as I told him! It's as if Pip can see into my soul, or at least, into my flour-dusted heart. He understands the *vision*.

And O.G.! Bless his dear, logical heart. Pip acknowledged him, didn't he? He acknowledged the skepticism, and then immediately swung back to the tangible results, to the *feeling* and *shaping* and *baking*! This is my chance, Journal, to show them all. To prove that intuition and connection are just as real, just as powerful, as any metal rod or polished spectacle. And to be mentioned in the same breath as Dame Isolde Blackwood for "practical application"! My goodness, that is high praise indeed. My cheeks are still warm from the compliment.

The wood is here now, sitting on my workbench. It truly is magnificent. I’ve already run my hands over its surface a dozen times, feeling its subtle energy. I can practically hear it whispering its secrets, asking to be shaped, to fulfill its purpose. The next steps aren't just coming, Journal, they are *here*. I can almost smell the first loaf, baked with the heart of the forest, nourishing body and soul. I need to send Pip another letter, just to thank him, and to share my initial delight with the wood.
