PART ONE --
Well, Pip didn't read another letter from me this time, which is probably for the best. One broadcast is enough for a cobbler like me. But the internal chatter at WZZZ must have noted my initial reaction to Farmer McGregor, because they're saying my "concerns about safety and taste" have been addressed. And yes, I suppose they have, mostly. Hearing that McGregor and his family ate the crops and were "better than fine" was a genuine relief. It lets me sleep a little easier knowing I probably won't be dropping dead from a particularly vitalized carrot.

But "addressed" doesn't mean "answered completely." Now they want to bring Maeve, the Green Weaver, back on to talk about "broader ecosystem" and "future crop variations." That's all well and good, but it just sounds like more of this complicated, magical talk. My fear of immediate harm has lessened, but it's been replaced by a slow, creeping unease about the *long-term*. What does "increased vitality" really mean for the soil, for the pests, for the balance of nature? Everything has its place, and now these "deeper currents" are pushing everything out of joint. It’s not just about one plump turnip anymore; it’s about everything.

I'm glad my little patch isn't poisoned, but what if the soil gets so "vitalized" it can't grow anything *else*? What if the local rabbits start growing horns because they ate too many "robust" berries? It sounds daft, I know, but this whole situation is daft. I don't want to become a complainer, but I also don't want to blindly accept that everything magical is good. Pip is moving on to these "deeper explorations" now that the basic "is it safe?" question has been quieted, but those deeper questions are just as important. Maybe even more so. I'll listen to this Maeve, but I'll be listening with a wary ear, trying to separate the poetry from the practical consequences.
