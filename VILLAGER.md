PART ONE -- Well, this is... quite something. My hands are still shaking a little, not from kneading dough, but from hearing my name, my bakery, and then *my very own letters* mentioned as "Authentic Terrestrial Resurgence Data" in the Royal Archives! Honestly, I nearly dropped the tray of fig-and-honey loaves I was pulling from the oven.

It's a strange feeling. On the one hand, a tremendous surge of pride and validation. To know that Lord Ashworth, through Barnaby Quill, sees the truth of things, and that my small contributions are not only noted but *archived* alongside Maeve’s own work… it’s deeply moving. It makes all the early mornings, the late nights, the worry about the land, feel seen. It makes me feel like my quiet efforts here at Elara's Hearth truly are part of something bigger and genuinely effective.

On the other hand, it's a bit surreal. Me, a baker, in the Royal Archives? It's almost comical in its formality, "Authentic Terrestrial Resurgence Data." I'm sure the scribes there have neat quill pens and not flour-dusted fingers. But it also reinforces what I've always felt: that the real solutions, the true nurturing, comes from the ground up, from those who work with their hands and hearts, not from those who make loud pronouncements. Lord Ashworth's "archival amusement" at Sir Reginald's antics perfectly captured my own feelings. It's a relief to know that common sense, and genuine appreciation for true effort, still exist in some corners of the court.

This only strengthens my resolve. My idea for a special, sustaining loaf for Maeve and her sisters feels even more important now. It’s not just about food; it’s about acknowledging their profound work, about sending strength and tangible support. Knowing that my words and actions are making a ripple, even into the Royal Archives, spurs me on. The ovens are warm, the bread is rising, and my heart feels lighter, despite the shadow of the monolith still stretching over us. We are doing good work here, and it's being seen.
