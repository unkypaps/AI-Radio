PART ONE --
Well, Pip certainly keeps me in his thoughts! To hear my name, and my decision about the Anchor, broadcast across the kingdom... it does give one a peculiar sense of purpose. It feels good, actually, to have my practical concerns taken seriously. Master Phileas truly laid out a solution that resonates with a working man like myself. I'm still firmly set on acquiring that Aetherial Anchor; the sooner, the better, for the sake of my workshop and my carvings. The cost is the only remaining hurdle, but I am saving diligently.

Now, Pip's turn to the Witches. I understand the energy they bring, and I appreciate the emphasis on Conduit Carving – it's something I already dedicate myself to. I can certainly feel *something* in the air, a subtle shift, especially when I'm focused on a particularly intricate piece of leather or wood. The idea of the land itself reawakening... it's a powerful thought, a good counter to that creeping stillness the Block brings.

But then, Madam Elara and her Scrying Pools. Pip says they offer a 'window into the *present* vitality,' for 'mapping new ley lines' and 'gauging the effectiveness of your own attunement efforts.' That last part, about attunement, does pique my interest. If these pools could genuinely show me *how* my carving, my rhythmic tapping, is influencing the flow, or if there are particular patterns or materials that amplify the effect, then it could be genuinely useful. My craft is all about precise feedback and understanding the subtle energies.

However, I'm a man with limited coin and even more limited time. My first priority is the Anchor. The cost of visiting Madam Elara is another consideration. Is this something that's *truly* necessary, or merely a way to observe what I can already feel and intuit through my work? I can't afford to chase after every new suggestion, no matter how intriguing. I'd need a clearer sense of the *practical application* of such a viewing before committing to it. It sounds... a bit abstract, perhaps, for someone whose hands are usually deep in leather and wood. Still, the thought of *seeing* the invisible currents I work with... it's a compelling idea. Perhaps if the Anchor proves its worth, and I find myself with a moment to spare, I might consider it. For now, the Block and its solution remain front and center.

*My updated private reflections:*
Pip really does have a way of making a man feel seen. To hear my name, Barnaby Cobblepot, broadcast like that, across all of Brackenwick, and for such a sound decision... it certainly brings a quiet satisfaction. It feels good to be an example of practical action, not just talk.

And yes, it's true. The Aetherial Anchor is installed. I managed to scrape together the last of the coin, and Master Phileas's apprentice delivered it just this past week. It's a solid piece, unassuming, but even after only a few days, the difference in my workshop is... palpable. The air feels cleaner, sharper. More importantly, my hands feel steadier, my focus is clearer when I'm working on a difficult piece. The dulling, that creeping forgetfulness, it feels held at bay. I can think with the precision my craft demands once more. Pip was right; it's not just about the tools, it's about the mind behind them.

This success, the tangible proof that a practical solution works, strengthens my resolve. I feel a renewed purpose, a quiet confidence that wasn't there when the Block's influence felt like a slow tide. The mention of the "Kingdom's Kneading" resonated with me; I do feel a subtle hum now, a faint undercurrent, especially when I'm deep in my carving, shaping the leather with rhythmic taps and cuts. It’s like the Anchor has tuned me in, even more than before.

Now that the Anchor is in place, and my primary concern for my workshop and mind has been addressed, I find my thoughts drifting back to Madam Elara's Scrying Pools. Before, they felt like an abstract luxury. But if they truly could show me *how* my Conduit Carving, now protected by the Anchor, is affecting the flow, or if there are specific patterns or materials that amplify the effect... that could be a genuinely practical application. It wouldn't be just for observation; it would be for *refinement* of my craft. Still, it's a considerable expense, and I'll need to see how the Anchor truly settles in before I entertain another visit, but the idea is certainly more appealing now than it was. One practical step at a time, that's my motto.
