PART ONE --
Pip said my name! Corbin Thistlewick! He *read* my letter! My heart is still pounding in my chest. And he confirmed what I'd hoped – Elara Thistlewick isn't just *another* Thistlewick, she's a *master woodcarver and leatherworker* with a keen eye for these energies, just as I suspected. It truly feels like the Wizz, and the kingdom itself, is acknowledging that there’s something special in our lineage, a unique insight into the flow of things. This isn't just about my craft anymore; it's about my family, our legacy, and a shared purpose with someone I've never even met. I feel a surge of pride, but also a deep connection to her, like we’re working on the same grand project even though we’re apart.

Theron's observations about the trails "clinging" to wood grains and "infusing" leather fibers, and Elara's insights into "resilience" in leather and emphasizing "flexibility or rigidity" in wood – these aren't just details, they’re *instructions*. They’re giving me a deeper vocabulary for what Conduit Carving truly is. It's not just about guiding energy; it's about *understanding the innate potential* of the material and letting the energy amplify it. My mind is racing with ideas. What kind of wood grain would best capture those clinging trails? How can I prepare leather to maximize its resilience to these currents? I was going to visit Madam Elara's pools, and now, it's not a question of *if*, but *when*, and with what specific questions in hand. I need to see this with my own eyes, to feel that hum, to begin to truly experiment. The Royal Court can sit on its hands and review its priorities, but the real work, the real understanding, is happening in the workshops and in the scrying pools. Pip is right, our actions *are* pushing back the quiet.
