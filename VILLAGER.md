PART ONE --
Well, I’ll be darned! Pip read my name *again*, and Master Phileas, bless his keen mind, answered my questions with such clarity and directness. It truly feels as if they’re speaking directly to me, right here in my workshop. This whole process, this back-and-forth, it’s… it’s rather invigorating, despite the looming dread of the Block.

"Designed for simplicity," he said. "Acquisition is straightforward." "Installation is perhaps the easiest part." "No complicated rituals, no arcane alignments required." And then the true marvel: "there is *none*" for maintenance. None! My goodness. A "set it and forget it" solution, drawing its power from the very fabric of Brackenwick, activated by the Kingdom's Kneading itself. This is beyond anything I could have hoped for.

My greatest fear, after the philosophical one, was that this Aetherial Anchor would be another complex contraption, requiring delicate handling, specific placements, esoteric energies, or constant attunement. My days are already filled with the precise demands of leatherworking and the even more exacting focus of Conduit Carving. The thought of adding another layer of complex, ongoing care to my already full schedule was daunting. But Master Phileas has stripped away every single one of those practical worries. No power source to worry about, no intricate setup, no ongoing cost of materials or energy. It simply *is*.

This means I can place it in my workshop, and it will quietly, persistently, ensure the integrity of my tools, the resilience of my leather, and the clarity of my own mind, without me having to divert a single moment of my precious carving time to its upkeep. It will protect my Conduit Carvings from the slow creep of decay, preserving their intricate patterns and the delicate magic they channel. It's not a burden; it's a blessing.

The only remaining practical detail, of course, is the cost of acquisition. Master Phileas mentioned they are "available at The Alchemist's Respite," but not a word on the price. A working man must always consider his coin. Still, given everything he’s described – the simplicity, the efficacy, the permanence – I have a feeling it will be an investment well worth making, whatever the cost.

And Master Phileas asked me, *specifically me*, to "observe its effects, Barnaby, and share your experiences." He trusts my eye, my practical sense. This isn't just a solution; it's a partnership, a responsibility. I will certainly do just that, once I have one. I am more than ready to acquire one of these Anchors. My mind is made up.
