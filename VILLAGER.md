PART ONE --
Oh, my goodness, he said my name again! Agnes Plumkin! And he quoted me, "listening to the earth's sigh"! My heart is absolutely fluttering like a butterfly caught in a bread basket. To think that my simple observation, just a feeling I had in my garden, has become a "touchstone" and now a reminder of the "profound, harmonizing silence" the Witches are calling us to embrace. It's truly overwhelming, and I must confess, I've had to sit down with a strong cup of chamomile to steady myself. The Daily Crumb might need a special little plaque soon, I jest, of course!

But Pip also spoke of "scrutiny" again, and this time it felt much more... pointed. "Court factions," he said, and the Duke's "squirrel sanctuary proposals" contrasted with the Witches' "urgent directives." That rather stopped me in my tracks. A squirrel sanctuary, when the very earth beneath us is groaning and singing? It does feel rather... out of touch, doesn't it? It's unsettling to think that while we are all trying to listen and understand, there are those in power who would rather focus on such trifles, or worse, try to quiet us down. It feels like the whispers I heard in the broadcast about "elements within the kingdom" don't just want things to be quiet, but want *us* to be quiet.

It makes me nervous, truly it does. A baker like me, I'm not used to political machinations. My world is flour, yeast, and the gentle hum of the oven. But then Pip’s voice, so clear and strong, cutting through the static, reminding us that our very listening is a "shield" and "proof" of Brackenwick’s true heart. It gives me strength. He's right; we *are* making a difference. I can feel it. That little spark of clarity he mentioned, I feel it too now, a persistent, quiet brightness even beneath the Obsidian Block's hum. It's like a tiny candle in the gloom, but many tiny candles can light a whole room, can't they? I will continue to listen, to observe, and to encourage others. The Daily Crumb will remain a hub for those who feel the pull of this mending, no matter who is scrutinizing.
