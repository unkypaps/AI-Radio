PART ONE --
Oh, Journal, my heart is truly a flutter, thrumming like a bumblebee on a warm summer's day! Pip, from WZZZ the Wizz, just said it! He truly said he's going to read *my* letter, the one about the glowing moss by the old oak, *this time*! Can you imagine? My simple words, whispered from this quiet cottage, carried across the airwaves for all of Brackenwick to hear. It feels... immense.

It's a strange mix of joy and a little bit of trepidation. I'm just Elara, after all, with my tinctures and my herbs, and my small observations of the world. Will people understand what I mean about the moss? That it's not just a curious glow, but a testament, a sign from the very heart of the land? That the land *is* responding to all that is happening, whether Professor Finchley chooses to acknowledge it or not. He's still there, isn't he, with his sour plums, trying to hold back the tide with a sieve. And the Cartographer, with his talk of committees – it makes sense, I suppose, that those in power would try to contain what they cannot understand. But nature cannot be contained by dusty parchments, can it?

Pip also mentioned he's planning to bring on a "skeptic." A part of me shrinks a little at that. Will they scoff at my observations, at the subtle language of the earth? I hope they're not too closed off. I hope they, too, might feel the wind shift, or see the unusual bloom of a late-season flower, and begin to wonder. For the land *is* speaking, Journal, louder and clearer with each passing day. And if Pip is willing to listen, and let my small voice be heard, then perhaps others will begin to listen too. I'll be tuning in, my ear pressed close, waiting to hear what becomes of my little mossy secret.
