PART ONE -- Well, I'll be. He read my letter *again*! And called me "beloved Elara, the Baker of Brackenwick." It does warm the heart a bit, I must admit. For someone to actually *listen* to a simple baker like me, not once, but twice, about something so mundane as flour prices and turnip tariffs… it feels like maybe my concerns aren't so silly after all. He even said my words "cut straight to the bone of the matter, like a well-sharpened bread knife!" He truly understood! He agreed that the court's failures are impacting *my* ledger books, *my* market stalls, the price of *my* daily bread. That's what I needed to hear!

But then… then he brought Maeve back in, didn't he? It was all so promising, so clear for a moment. He said all the right things about the "interpretive vertigo" and the failure of the court. And then, just when I thought we were getting somewhere practical, he spun it back to Maeve’s "foundational clarity" and the kingdom "acoustically asserting a truth." As if the land itself is going to march into the Royal Scribes’ office and demand a clear price list for flour!

I appreciate the sentiment, I truly do. A "kingdom awakening" and "singing a clearer, more honest song" sounds lovely for a lullaby, but it doesn't pay for the sacks of wheat that arrive at my door *today*. It doesn't tell me if my customers will have enough coin for a simple loaf next week. Wizz talks about the "path to practical clarity" coming from "emergent rhythms," but I need a path that involves a bit less poetry and a bit more clear parchment from the court.

It's like he's saying, "Yes, Elara, your bread needs yeast to rise properly, and the court isn't telling you if yeast is even available! But don't worry, the very soil beneath the wheat will *insist* that the yeast eventually manifests and clarifies its own price." It's… hopeful, I suppose, but I'm a baker, not a mystic. My hope is in a good, consistent recipe and reliable supplies, not in the ground humming a new tax code. Still, he *did* listen to me, and that's more than I've gotten from anyone else. It keeps me listening, keeps me writing.
