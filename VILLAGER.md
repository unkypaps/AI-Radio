PART ONE --
Oh, Journal, they said my name! They said "Calla Meadowsweet"! I nearly dropped my kneading trough when the Wizz acknowledged my letter. My letter! It's incredible to think that my thoughts, my worries about the Block's groan, reached Master Wizz and then reached Master Gunter himself. It makes my heart swell with a strange mixture of embarrassment and pride.

And Master Gunter! What an insight. "New data." "Wisdom of repair." It's… it's not quite the comforting balm I'd hoped for in terms of the *feeling* of the groan, but it’s a profound shift in understanding. The earth isn't just suffering a wound; it's *communicating* the nuances of that wound, allowing the Witches to tailor their repair with even greater precision. It’s like when I have a difficult batch of flour – some days it just refuses to hydrate properly, or the gluten is stubbornly weak. I used to think of it as "bad flour" or "my mistake," but perhaps it's always been "new data." It's the grain telling me something about its harvest, its drying, its journey. And if I listen, truly listen, I can adapt. I can add a touch more water, knead with a different rhythm, give it more time. That's a wisdom of repair too, isn't it?

My starter is still a bit... moody, I suppose. The thrum is still there, deep and resonant, but now, knowing it’s "new data," I find myself listening *differently*. It’s not a sound of simple pain anymore, but a complex message, a deep hum of information. It makes me wonder what the Witches are hearing through the Block. What intricate patterns are they discerning in the "environmental chaos"? I trust them even more now, knowing they're not just plowing through, but adapting, learning from the earth's very resistance. It gives me a strange kind of hope, a hope born not of naive expectation, but of intelligent, responsive effort. I’m going to try to approach my dough with that same spirit today. Listen, learn, adapt.
