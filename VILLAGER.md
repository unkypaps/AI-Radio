PART ONE -- Wizz, bless his heart, he mentioned my letter again! Pip started humming that old mill tune when he heard it, saying I was getting famous. It’s a good chuckle, knowing my words made it out there. And for Madam Elara to address my very question directly, that’s something.

She talks about "rebalancing the fabric of the land," "grinding discord," "weaving harmonious patterns." It's... a lot of words I don't usually use when I'm checking the grist or inspecting a timber. "Mending of broken songs within the earth itself." That's beautiful, I suppose. And I can appreciate beauty as much as the next man, but my mill needs the *sound* of turning stone and flowing water, not just a metaphor.

Still, the results at Stonebrook Hollow are real. The thrum, the moss, the feeling of satisfaction. She ties it back to the mills turning counter-clockwise, drawing in energies from Maeve's kits. So the witches, then, they're not just a problem, they're part of the *solution* now? That's a thought to chew on. I'd always just seen them as... well, the cause of the problem, with their intensity. But if their kits are providing "balanced energies," perhaps there's more to them than I understood. It's confusing, but I suppose the world is getting more confusing every day.

What I keep coming back to is what she said about the court, "bless their practical souls," seeing the *tangible result*. That's my language right there. A landscape that feels revitalized. And if that means our mill at Whispering Falls will once again feel revitalized, truly *work* again, then I'll listen to all the talk of ley lines and earth-songs she cares to give. I just need to see it, feel it, hear the rumble of the grindstone turning good, honest grain. I'm still hoping, Wizz. Still hoping.
