PART ONE --
My name is Elara Meadowbrook. I’m the miller at Silverstream Mill, just outside the main thoroughfare of Brackenwick, where the Silverstream meanders down from the northern hills before joining the larger River Cinder. My family has operated this mill for generations, and my hands know the feel of good grain, the hum of well-oiled gears, and the steady, powerful push of water against the wheel. I’m a practical woman, not one for idle fancy or grand pronouncements, but I do pay close attention to the world around me, especially anything that affects the water. My husband, Thomas, handles most of the hauling and accounts, but the grinding, the maintenance, the very *heart* of the mill, that’s my domain.

The unfolding events, this obsidian monolith and now the witches, they matter to me because they are directly impacting the Silverstream. My livelihood, my family’s income, the very food that goes onto many tables in Brackenwick, all depend on that stream's unwavering force. If the water stops turning the wheel, the mill stops. And if the mill stops, everything stops.

Well, Pip actually read my letter *again*! He called my question "profound" and "vital." It's a strange mix of validation and deepening dread. He understood perfectly what I was trying to say about the water's *force*, not just its sound. He spoke of "inertia" and the Block "drawing energy *out* of things." That's it, exactly. That's the feeling. It's not just a lack of sound; it's a lack of *push*.

And then he started talking about the Witches. I must admit, when he first brought them up in relation to *my* problem, I raised an eyebrow. "Vibrant life," "strengthen it, re-energize the flow," "singing force"... it sounds wonderful, almost too good to be true. I’ve heard the rumors of glowing moss and flourishing crops, but for it to affect the physical *power* of a stream? A stream that needs to turn a massive wooden wheel? It feels like a leap, from a miller's perspective. Grain doesn’t grind on magic alone. It grinds on *force*.

Still, the idea does offer a flicker of hope. If Pip believes it's a real possibility, if the Witches' influence could truly counteract this "inertia," then I need to know more. My practical mind immediately turns to questions of proximity. The Silverstream is nowhere near the Moon Tree, as far as I know. Do these "activated ley lines" and "Green Weaver Hubs" extend far enough to reach us? Can this "bright and swift hum" truly touch the Silverstream and give it back its vigor?

My worry about the mill's future hasn't lessened, but now it's coupled with a curious, almost desperate, new avenue of inquiry. I need to know if this witch-magic can *physically* turn my wheel. Not just make the weeds grow faster.
