PART ONE --
Pip, he certainly knows how to put a man on the spot. My name, twice last broadcast, and then again, right at the top of this one! "Esteemed Cobbler by the Square," he called me. It's a bit much, really, but I can't deny a certain warmth in my chest at the recognition. Though, it comes with a heavier weight this time.

He sounds genuinely frustrated, doesn't he? "Deafening silence." And then to hear about The Alchemist's Respite, how they're struggling with sales, how they sent a "pointed missive"... that makes it real. It's not just about sharing observations for the sake of it anymore. It's about ensuring these Anchors are even *available*. If people aren't buying them, and no one is reporting back, then why would The Alchemist's Respite keep making them? That thought truly gives me pause. What if I finally save up my coppers, only to find they've stopped production? That would be a bitter pill, indeed.

He's right, of course, about people needing to hear from their neighbors. I'm a prime example of that myself. I need to see it work for others, or at least hear direct, practical accounts, before I fully commit. But he's also right that *my* voice, *my* practical observation, once I have the thing, will be important for others. I don't want to be part of that silence.

My resolution to acquire an Anchor has now shifted from a firm commitment to something closer to an urgent necessity. The sluggishness in my mind, the way my water basin seems to cling to the dust, it's not just an inconvenience anymore; it’s a tangible hindrance to my livelihood. I need this clarity, this flow, to do my best work, to keep my customers satisfied.

I’ve already started setting aside a larger portion of my daily earnings. Every sole repaired, every heel replaced, a little more goes into the Anchor fund. I'm hoping to have enough by the end of next week, maybe the week after at the latest. I'll need to make a trip down to the Respite myself, of course. Can't just send the coppers by courier, who knows where they'd end up in this Block. Once I have it, I'll place it carefully, observe it meticulously, and then, Pip, you *will* hear from me. I give my word.
