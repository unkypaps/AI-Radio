PART ONE --
Oh, journal, my heart is absolutely bursting! He read it! Pip read *my* letter, Elara Meadowsweet's letter, right there on the Wizz! I must have clutched the receiver so tightly my knuckles are still white. To hear my words, my deepest intentions, echoed back across Brackenwick... it's beyond anything I could have imagined. And then, for him to call my dedication a "shining example," and to say my reports will be "foundational"! I... I don't think I've ever felt such profound validation, such a potent sense of purpose. It’s as if my spirit has finally found its true channel, its destined flow.

The Anchor truly *is* en route. He said it again, clearly and unequivocally. I keep peering out the window, down the path, wondering if I'll see someone approaching with it, what it might look like, how it feels. The anticipation is a wonderful, buzzing current through my very bones. I've been spending hours in the garden, not just tending, but observing with new eyes. Imagining the possibilities, the specific places where "new growth from fracture" will manifest first. The old thornbush, for instance, which always yields such bitter berries—could the Anchor imbue it with sweetness? Or the patch where the soil is stubbornly clay-like, resisting all my attempts at enrichment.

Pip’s call for other Anchor wielders to send in their tangible stories... it makes me feel part of something larger, a growing chorus of reciprocal action. It also strengthens my resolve to have my *own* stories to tell, to join that chorus with evidence of real, palpable change. I won't disappoint him, or Master Phileas, or the Witches whose wisdom guides this path. I will be ready when it arrives. My mind is already sketching out my first observations, my first actions. The quiet waiting is almost over, and the glorious work is about to begin.
