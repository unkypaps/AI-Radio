PART ONE --
Oh, my goodness gracious, it happened again! Pip, and Elara, they said my name! Not only that, but they called me a "brilliant woman"! Me, Agnes Plumkin, just a baker from The Daily Crumb! My cheeks are burning something fierce, but my heart feels like it's swelling with all the good yeast in my starter. I nearly dropped the tray of fresh saffron buns I was pulling from the oven.

To hear my letter read aloud once was a wonder, but twice? And to be singled out as a "community anchor," alongside Mabel the Baker! It's an honor, truly. It makes all those little whispers, all those odd feelings I've been having, feel even more real and purposeful. I was worried, just a tiny bit, that my little notice and my ledger might seem a bit... well, *quaint* in the face of all this grand magic, but Pip and Elara see it exactly as I hoped. They see it as vital.

This "new language" the Witches are imparting, the "knowing without knowing," it feels so true to what people have already started to share over their morning coffee and their lunchtime pasties. Mrs. Gable down the lane, she came in just yesterday, looking quite flustered. She said she had an inexplicable urge to replant the old willow saplings that had fallen by the stream after the last heavy rains. Used to be her late husband’s project, she said, but she’d never had much interest. Now, it was all she could think about. And young Thomas, the apprentice cobbler, he suddenly feels compelled to mend the worn-down stone steps leading to the old forgotten spring house, even though no one has used that path in decades. These are the "animated blueprints" Pip spoke of, aren't they? The "commands to mend."

It makes such perfect sense! Every little act, every strange impulse, every vivid dream – they're all pieces of a grand tapestry. And my little shop, my "listening post," is right in the thick of it, helping to gather these threads. It's not just about baking bread anymore, it's about kneading hope into our community, about providing a place where these whispers can be heard, acknowledged, and shared. I’ll make sure to note down Mrs. Gable's willow saplings and Thomas’s spring steps in my special ledger. I need to get a new, bigger one, I think.

The "omnipresent hum" and the "unsettling quiet" are still there, yes, I feel them too, especially late at night when the bakery is dark and still. But Pip's voice, full of that "genuine surge of something hopeful," it truly does lift the spirit. I feel less scared, and more... *ready*. Ready to listen, ready to encourage, ready to help weave our Brackenwick back together.
