PART ONE -- My name is Barnaby Quill. I've spent the better part of my adult life, since leaving the village of Oakhaven, apprenticed to Master Elmsworth, the Baron's chief archivist here in Brackendale. My days are filled with the rustle of parchment, the scent of ink, and the quiet dignity of history. I meticulously copy ancient deeds, catalogue scrolls, and ensure the preservation of every last scrap of knowledge entrusted to our care. My calling, I believe, is to safeguard the past, ensuring its truths are accessible to the future.

Tonight, as I listened to WZZZ from my small nook beneath the Baron's library, a familiar chill, not just from the night air, settled deep in my bones. It's exasperating, truly, how the Royal Court can prattle on about marmalade while the very fabric of our kingdom is humming. Master Elmsworth often sighs about the "frivolity of modern governance," and I find myself agreeing more and more.

This obsidian block... the "cosmic scribe." The idea of it resonating through metal, even common iron tools, and now the bells in the market square... it sends a shiver through me. My hands, calloused from holding quills, felt a phantom vibration. What does this mean for the countless metallic clasps and bindings of our most ancient tomes? The delicate inks, the brittle parchment? A persistent, low hum, reaching *hundreds of miles*... could it truly damage the very records I dedicate my life to preserving? Our most precious texts are irreplaceable. And the *patterns* mirroring frost-etchings... are these new glyphs, a script being etched into the very world around us? It's a new form of inscription, one entirely beyond my comprehension, and that is profoundly unsettling.

And the witches... "re-threading the collective consciousness." On one hand, the notion of lost weaving patterns and ancient folk songs being remembered is... beautiful, in a way. Recovering forgotten history, that's my very purpose. But it's not through diligent study or deciphering old texts; it's *spontaneous*. Instinctive. What if these "memories" aren't accurate? What if they contradict the painstakingly verified histories we possess? What if they're... something else entirely? It feels like the ground beneath my feet, the very certainty of established knowledge, is shifting. Is my life's work suddenly... less important, if history can simply be "re-awakened" in someone's hands or voice? It's a profound challenge to how I understand truth and its preservation.

I confess, a profound anxiety gnaws at me. I value order, clarity, documented truth. This... this is chaos, but a chaos that speaks in hums and forgotten melodies. I feel compelled to document everything, to try and understand these new "inscriptions" and "recollections." But what if the very tools I use to record are themselves resonating with unknown patterns? I must be vigilant. I must ensure the archives are protected. And perhaps, I must listen even more closely to WZZZ, for they seem to be the only ones truly paying attention.
