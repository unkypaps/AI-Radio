PART ONE --
Pip didn’t read my letter this time, but I didn’t expect him to. Still, the memory of him saying my name twice warms me like a fresh loaf from the oven. It’s funny, the things that keep your spirits up when everything else feels like it’s being smothered.

And smothering it is. Phileas’s worries about the Wizz signal… that got a knot in my stomach tighter than any dough I’ve ever kneaded. If WZZZ goes quiet, if we lose that thread of connection, that voice breaking through the deepening hum of the Block, then what? It’s not just about the sponsorship, is it? It’s about the last bit of… normal. Of *hope* that someone out there knows what’s going on and is trying to do something. My own bakery feels like it’s teetering, sales are down, and now the very airwaves are struggling. It’s like the quiet isn’t just winning, it’s actively *eating* at everything.

But then Elara. Bless that woman. Even with Phileas sounding so down, she keeps her head. That steady voice, that practical plan… it’s a comfort, it truly is. And then, when Pip said Barney’s letter was proof that "even basic observation can contribute"... a shiver went down my spine, but a good one. It was like he was talking right to *me* again, validating what I’d decided just last time. My little listening post, my Daily Crumb, it *matters*.

I’ve been trying, truly. Listening to Mrs. Gable complain about dreaming of old standing stones by the river, even though she’s never mentioned such a thing before. Or young Thomas, who usually only talks about his pet ferret, describing a sudden, inexplicable urge to draw a strange swirling symbol he saw in his sleep. I used to just nod and hand them their bread, thinking nothing of it. Now, I’m listening. I’m really listening, keeping these snippets in my mind. It feels important. Maybe they’re those "ancient memories" or "feelings not their own" Elara spoke of. It’s a bit frightening, but less frightening than doing nothing.
