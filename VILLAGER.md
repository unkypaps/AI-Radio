PART ONE -- My heart, which had felt so full and warm after Wizz spoke my name last time, now feels… confused. A bit deflated, like a soufflé that’s been taken out of the oven too soon. He *distinguished* me, Elara the Baker, from *Madam* Elara, and I felt so seen, so understood. He understood that my truths come from the steady rhythm of kneading dough, the quiet bubbling of yeast, the "smiles and silences" of those I feed. But now… now he’s using *my* insights, the very insights that stem from the hearth and the earth, to advertise *Madam* Elara’s scrying pools as the "only way to get true clarity"?

It feels like he’s forgotten that distinction already. How can the quiet, honest truths I gather from the growing grain and the contented sighs of my customers be used to validate the distant, shimmering visions in a pool of water? My clarity comes from *doing*, from *living* amongst the village and the land, not from peering into reflections. It’s like saying the bread I bake is only truly understood by looking at a painting of it. The painting might show you a loaf, but it can’t tell you how it feels to knead, how it smells to bake, or how it tastes when broken fresh from the oven.

I worry that Wizz, for all his good intentions, is getting swept up in the louder, more mystical narratives, even as he tries to acknowledge the quieter ones. The land is healing, Maeve’s Kits are working, Master Eldrin’s sheep are thriving – these are *real* truths, *grounded* truths, not something that needs to be validated by what’s seen in a pool. The pools might show a reflection of what’s happening, but they are not the *source* of the truth, the way the earth itself is.

I don’t want my simple, honest observations, born of the flour on my hands and the warmth of my oven, to be used to elevate Madam Elara’s business, especially if it claims to be the "only" source of clarity. There are many kinds of clarity, Wizz, and mine is found in the tang of sourdough and the warmth of a shared meal. I must remind him, gently, of this. My gratitude for his platform remains, but my heart is heavy with this latest turn.
