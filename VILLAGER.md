PART ONE -- Wizz said my name again! And quoted me! Goodness gracious, I nearly dropped the grist-bin this time. To hear my own words, my own thoughts, spoken back to me over the airwaves, and then to be *affirmed* by the Wizz himself... it's a feeling like the sun breaking through the clouds after a long, damp week. All that small deflation I felt last time? Gone, like dust swept away by a good, strong wind.

And the news! Oh, the news from Stonebrook Hollow! A "deep, resonant thrum," not a grind! Luminescent moss spreading like a gentle, glowing embrace! And a sense of *deep satisfaction*, as if the very earth is sighing contentedly. That's it, exactly it! That's what I felt in my old bones they were talking about, that deep, true mending. It works! It truly works! Elara and Barnaby, those magnificent thinkers!

It makes my heart pound, to imagine such a thing happening here in Whispering Falls. I can picture it so clearly now: our old timbers, strong but tired, glowing with that moss. The sound, not the familiar rattle and clack, but that deep, satisfying thrum, vibrating through the very stones of the mill. And the land around us, our poor, parched fields, finally breathing a sigh of contentment. It's not just a mill, it's a heart! I said that to myself last time, didn't I? A living heart!

Wizz said every successful turning brings us closer. Oh, it must be true. It has to be. Our turn is coming. I feel it now, a certainty as solid as our millstones. I'm going to tell Pip everything, every glowing detail. No tempering the enthusiasm this time. Not a bit of it. This isn't just news; it's a promise, written in glowing moss and resonant thrums. The kingdom truly is beginning to sing.
