PART ONE --
I... I can't believe it. They're going to interview *me*? Elara Meadowbrook, on the Wizz? My name, on the air, not just in a letter, but for everyone to hear me speak. My heart is thrumming almost as much as I hope the Silverstream will again soon. It's a jolt, a genuine shock, but not unwelcome. In fact, it's... exhilarating.

This isn't just about my words being read anymore; it's about my voice being heard. My practical worries, my observations from the riverbanks, they're important enough for WZZZ to dedicate an entire segment to them. It feels like a profound validation. All those years I’ve spent by the water, learning its moods, its sounds, its secrets—now, that knowledge is suddenly vital to the whole kingdom.

A tiny part of me is a bit nervous, of course. What if I stumble over my words? What if I don't sound as articulate as I do in my letters? I'm a miller, not a bard or a scholar. But then, that's precisely why it matters, isn't it? It's the practical, boots-on-the-ground perspective that they're after. This isn't about flowery language; it's about clear, useful information.

This is my chance to truly detail what I've seen, what I've felt. I've been spending every spare moment by the river, just as I promised myself. The luminescent moss... I've found a patch, near the old ford, that glows faintly in the twilight. Not a bright beacon, but a soft, ethereal shimmer I never noticed before the Wizz mentioned it. And the water's hum! It's not the same melody it once had, but there *is* a low, almost resonant thrumming beneath the stillness. It’s subtle, but it’s there, like the deep pulse of a sleeping beast. As for buoyancy, I’ve been trying to feel for that lightness, that push. The water is still heavy, but in some places, particularly near the deeper pools, there's a slight, almost imperceptible resistance, a feeling of 'holding' that wasn't there when the flow was truly dead. And the Sun-Dancer Wheat... I trekked a little further downriver, past the usual fields. There's a small, forgotten patch that seems to be standing taller, greener than I'd expect for this time of year. Could it be a sign?

I'll tell them about all of it. This isn't just my mill anymore; it’s *our* kingdom. My duty, my purpose, feels clearer than ever. I will speak for the river, for the mill, for all of us.
