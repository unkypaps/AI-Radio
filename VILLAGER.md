PART ONE --
Oh, Pip, bless his heart. He’s truly in a difficult position, isn't he? When he spoke of the "increased scrutiny" and then introduced that Lord Featherbottom – "forced upon me," he said. My stomach actually did a little flip. It's exactly as I feared, those court factions aren't just ignoring things; they're actively trying to muddy the waters, to make it seem like there's doubt where there should be clear listening. Lord Featherbottom, with his talk of "ancient texts" and "dusty scrolls," sounded so… well, *stuffy*. It's like he's forgotten that the world isn't just words on a page, but the ground beneath our feet and the air we breathe.

He seemed so convinced of his own wisdom, talking about "cosmic alignments" as if they were just something for scholars to argue over, not something we're *living* through. And the way he just dismissed the monolith's hum as "geological vibration"! Honestly, sometimes I think these powerful folk live in such towers they can’t hear the birds sing, let alone the earth sigh. But Pip, he was so clever, wasn't he? He let Lord Featherbottom ramble on, and then, without even meaning to, the Lord brought up that old story about the "Stone of Whispers" and how it used to glow, long ago, when the "sky-dwellers" were said to visit. It was almost like a little seed of truth hidden in all his bluster! Pip picked right up on it, too, asking about "celestial beings." It was brilliant.

It reminds me that even when people try to deny things, the truth often finds a way to peek through, like a little crocus pushing through the last snow. It makes me feel a bit sad for Lord Featherbottom, actually. He’s so busy trying to uphold the "old ways" that he can't see the new ones unfolding all around him, the beautiful new harmonies. But it also steelens my resolve. If even the skeptics can accidentally confirm things, then our listening is truly vital. I feel more confident now in sharing my thoughts at the bakery, even if someone grumbles about "nonsense" over their currant bun. We have to keep listening, keep hearing the whispers, and yes, even the accidental insights from those who don't mean to give them.
