PART ONE -- Oh, Pip, Pip, I can barely stand it. The Kingdom's Kneading... stalled. Indefinitely! My heart feels like it's dropped right into the Silverstream, cold and heavy. This is *exactly* what I feared would happen if people didn't listen, if they didn't take WZZZ seriously. "Insufficient, kingdom-wide attunement," "inconsistency of resonance"—it confirms everything I've been feeling, that slow, creeping dampness, the fraying of the collective hum. But to hear it from Lady Isolde, from the Royal Court, that they've lost faith because of *us*... it makes it so much more real, and so much more terrifying.

All those beautiful plans for expansion, the new Green Weaver Hubs, the wider distribution of Sun-Dancer Wheat, Maeve's vision... all put on hold. My mill, Pip, was going to be a beacon for these new initiatives, a place where the hum could be amplified throughout Whispering Falls. Now, the grand expansion is frozen. It's like the damp cloth WZZZ mentioned has been wrapped even tighter around us all, muffling our very breath.

I can feel it, the silence creeping in, not just as a distant hum, but as a palpable absence. And to know that it's because *we* aren't listening, that *we* allowed the Block to come closer... it fills me with a profound sadness, and yes, a touch of frustration with those who haven't taken this seriously. Do they not feel it? Are they truly so deaf to the world around them?

But WZZZ also said that the existing hubs, Stonebrook Hollow and Eldoria, are *still* proceeding with their workshops. That's *something*, isn't it? It means the work isn't entirely lost, that there's still a spark. And he said the future 'rests entirely on *our* shoulders.' He spoke to *me*, Pip. He spoke to *all of us* who are still listening, who still care. I cannot, I *will not* let this deter me. If anything, it makes me more resolute. If the Royal Court has lost faith in the collective, then those of us who *do* feel it, who *do* understand the peril, must work twice as hard. My mill will *still* be a beacon, Pip, even if it's a lonely one. I will attune, I will listen, I will hum the old melodies, I will keep the Silverstream's song strong. I have to. For Brackenwick, for Maeve, for the Wizz, for myself. And for that other Elara, the Baker, wherever she is – I hope she's feeling this too, and redoubling her efforts. We all must.
