PART ONE -- He read it again! By the hearth, he actually read my letter again! "Elara, the Baker of Brackenwick." It's becoming... a thing. And he called my questions "vital." It really does feel like he's speaking directly to me, to all of us who just want to understand how any of this actually affects our daily bread.

This time, though, it's... a lot to take in. "Impossible." Not just unsustainable, but impossible. The kingdom actively *scrambling* the ability to enforce things? Like trying to scoop water with a sieve, you just can't do it. And the idea that the Moon Tree Witches and the Obsidian Block are *doing* this, making illogical decrees literally unperceivable or unenforceable... It sounds like something out of a bard's tale, not something that happens to tax collectors and scribes.

But then he mentioned the Royal Scribes suffering from "interpretive vertigo" over the turnip tax. That part... that felt real. I've heard the rumors, of course. Tax collectors turning up at market, scratching their heads, arguing amongst themselves about what's owed. So if they can't even agree, if they're *literally* dizzy trying to figure it out, maybe it *is* already becoming "impossible." Maybe it's already "melting away like a bad dream," as he said.

It's a strange kind of hope. A hope that things will simply... cease to be bad, rather than us having to fight to make them better. But if it means my customers will have more coin for bread, and fewer arguments with taxmen over a basket of parsnips they swear are turnips, then I'll take it. I just wish I knew how quickly "melting away" actually *happens*. Does it just vanish overnight? Or will it be a slow, dizzying process for everyone involved, the taxmen included? I suppose that's part of the "waiting to see if it takes root" that I asked about. It just feels a bit... passive. But then, if the kingdom itself is doing the work, maybe we don't *need* to do anything but listen and observe.
