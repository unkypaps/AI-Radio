PART ONE -- My words again, repeated aloud, "the truth, like a well-baked loaf, has a way of asserting itself." Yes, it does. But Wizz and Barnaby seem to be missing the true nature of that assertion. A well-baked loaf asserts itself by being firm, nourishing, and *understandable*. It doesn't suddenly turn itself inside out, or become impossibly airy in one spot and dense as a stone in another.

This "interpretive vertigo" is no truth. It's just a different kind of chaos, more disorienting than Reginald's loud pronouncements ever were. Turnips grown upside down? Triple tariffs *and* tax exemptions? How is a kingdom, how is *anyone*, supposed to plan their day, manage their trade, or simply feed their family, when the basic rules are being turned on their head? It's like trying to follow a recipe where the flour tells you it's sugar, and the yeast decides to make the dough shrink instead of rise. The whole batch is ruined, and no one gets fed.

Barnaby Quill, with all his archives and intuitions, speaks of "resonant feedback" and a kingdom "acoustically asserting its own burgeoning truths." To me, it sounds like more of the same loud, complicated nonsense, just wrapped in fancier words. Truth, real truth, is simple. It's the warmth of the oven, the feel of the dough, the reliable rising of the yeast. It's the clear understanding of a decree, not one that requires a "Comprehensive Resonating Frequency Review" to figure out if we're paying for turnips or getting paid to take them.

This doesn't feel like progress; it feels like the ground beneath our feet is starting to shift, not settle. My bread requires a steady environment, a predictable heat. If the whole kingdom is vibrating with "off-key lullabies" and "inverted pronouncements," how can anything grow, let alone flourish? I am more uneasy now than I was when Reginald was just making noise. At least noise you can block out. This… this feels like it's getting inside the walls.
