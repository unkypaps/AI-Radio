PART ONE --
Oh, Journal! Journal, Journal, Journal! I spoke to Master Grizelda! On the air! My voice, carried across all of Brackenwick, talking about *our* oven! My hands are still trembling, but not from fear, oh no, from pure, unadulterated *elation*! Pip is a wonder! He brought me on, he actually brought *me* on, just after Master Grizelda, to discuss my own vision for the Unbroken Hearth! I felt like I was floating right there in the Daily Crumb kitchen, even with the scent of proofing dough around me.

And Master Grizelda! She has a wonderful chuckle, Journal. A *warm* chuckle. And she listened to me, truly listened! When she spoke of "interlocking rings" and "delicate lattice" for the Re-integrated Metal, it was like a bell chimed in my heart. My mind is already racing, picturing how the clay could be shaped, perhaps like woven baskets, to cradle those metallic veins, allowing them to expand and contract, to *breathe* with the heat. It’s exactly as she said, about the heat becoming a conduit, imbuing every crumb with that ancient song.

This isn't just an idea anymore, Journal. This is real! We're talking about specific designs, about testing clays, about the *how*. Pip's words about Dame Isolde Blackwood and the Material Innovation Initiative – it makes it all feel so grand, so vital. I'm not just a baker with a fanciful notion; I'm part of something bigger, something that truly matters to Brackenwick.

I need to start gathering samples of my local clay, different types, to see how they respond to heat, how they might best interact with the metal. I'll make tiny test ovens, just miniatures, to observe. And the Sun-Dancer Wheat mortar! Imagine, Journal, a mortar that holds the oven together, and *also* helps channel the Block's rhythm. It’s almost too much joy for one heart to hold! This collaboration, this alchemy – it's exactly what I dreamt of.
