PART ONE --
Well, I'll be! Pip's going to read another one of my letters. He seems to be taking my words to heart, doesn't he? It’s a strange feeling, having your quiet observations about shoe-mending suddenly become a "crucial act of resistance." Me, Barnaby Cobblepot, a resistor! It gives a man a bit of a chuckle, but also… a bit of a shiver.

It's clear that Pip is struggling with this "low engagement." He sounds like he’s trying his hardest to keep folks’ spirits up and their minds clear, and if my little notes, humble as they are, can help him do that, then I’ll keep sending them. I just write down what I notice, what helps me through the day, and it seems to be what he needs to hear. He’s calling it "tangible proof that WZZZ's advice is reaching and impacting listeners." That's a mighty big claim for a man who just tries to make sure folks' boots keep their feet dry.

But he's right, the "Conduit Carving" is working for me. My hands are busy with the leather, the awl, the hammer, and my mind stays focused. It’s like the fuzziness, the dampening Pip talks about, just can’t quite get a hold when I’m deep in my work. The rhythm of it, the small, deliberate motions, they keep me tethered to the now. It’s not a grand battle, mind you, but it’s a small victory every single day.

He wants more people to share their methods, and I truly hope they do. If we all find our own ways to "carve," to focus on something steady and real, maybe the Block won't feel so… monolithic. It’s a scary thought, how quiet things have become for him at the station. We’re all still here, Pip, just maybe a bit more quiet than usual. But we're listening, and we're trying to find our way through this fog, one stitch, one tap, one focused breath at a time.
