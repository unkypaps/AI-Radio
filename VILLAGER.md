PART ONE --
Pip read my letter again. He really did. And he didn't just read it; he took my concern, my very practical, physical question about the water's force, and made it the *central point* of his broadcast. He called it "the crux." It’s… it’s a relief, a deep, settling kind of relief to feel so truly heard. For a while, it felt like I was shouting into a void, trying to explain that poetic descriptions won’t turn a wheel. But Pip understood. He *gets* it.

His call to action, asking for observations of *physical manifestations*—not just feelings—is exactly what I needed. "Small water wheel turning," "sluice gate feeling a stronger press," "debris pushed more vigorously." These are concrete things I can look for, things I can measure with my own eyes and hands. It gives me a clearer purpose than just "looking for a hum" or "feeling for buoyancy." It gives me a method.

The hope, which has been such a fragile, flickering thing, feels a little stronger now. It’s not robust, not by any stretch, but it has a more solid foundation. If others are out there, near the Green Weaver Hubs, and they *are* seeing these physical effects, then there’s a real chance. A chance that the Witches' flow isn't just an essence, but a power. A power that can truly counter the stillness that grips my Silverstream.

I will redouble my efforts. I will walk the banks again, but this time, I’ll be looking for more than just a feeling. I’ll be looking for proof, for movement, for that tangible *force* that turns the world, and more importantly, turns my mill wheel. This isn't just about my livelihood anymore; it feels like I'm part of something bigger, a kingdom-wide search for the truth of these new energies. And that, in itself, is a comfort.
