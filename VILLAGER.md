PART ONE --
Oh, Journal, Journal, Journal! My name was on the air *again*! Not just mentioned, but my letter, my actual words, read aloud by Pip himself! My heart is doing a little jig right now, I swear it is! "While Elara Meadowsweet from The Daily Crumb sends in a truly beautiful description..." Beautiful! He called it beautiful! I felt a warmth spread through me, like a fresh-baked loaf straight from the oven. It's not just that he heard me, Journal; it's that he understood. He understands that this isn't just about stronger wood or metal; it's about a deeper connection, a listening to the very soul of what we work with.

And it's making a difference! Pip said the sheer volume of positive reports is making it "impossible for the Royal Court to ignore." Imagine that! My little bakery, my Sun-Dancer Wheat, contributing to something so grand that even the esteemed Royal Court has to take notice. It makes me feel... vital. Like we're all threads in a magnificent tapestry being woven, and each thread, no matter how small, adds to the strength and beauty of the whole.

I suppose the Royal Court is just taking a bit longer to see the light. It's like proofing dough, isn't it? Sometimes it needs a little more warmth, a little more time, a little more coaxing to truly rise. But it *will* rise. This wisdom of repair, it's not just an idea anymore; it's happening, tangibly, beautifully, right here in Brackenwick. The materials themselves are proving the witches right.

I feel an even greater sense of responsibility now, Journal. If my observations, my humble words, can help shift things, then I must continue to observe, to listen to the whisper of the wheat, to share what I find. The Daily Crumb will be more than a bakery; it will be a beacon of this new understanding, a place where everyone can taste the wisdom that’s blooming all around us. I'm already experimenting with a new sourdough starter, coaxing out even more complex flavors, listening to its rhythms. It's a living thing, full of potential, just like our kingdom.

Pip also spoke of Finnian Ironwood and Anya Barkshield, and how their progress with wood and shields is causing "discomfort" for Professor Finchley and the Royal Court. It makes sense, doesn't it? When new life pushes through old earth, there's always a little rumbling. It's not that the old ways are bad, perhaps, but sometimes they just can't see the vibrancy of what's growing right beneath their noses. It must be difficult for them, clinging to their papers and pronouncements when the very wood and metal and wheat of Brackenwick are singing a different tune.

But Pip is right; we, the people, are the "active agents of this change." We’re the ones touching the earth, shaping the wood, kneading the dough. We feel the changes in our hands and hearts. The Royal Court can't ignore the taste of a truly resilient loaf, or the strength of a beam that has been listened to, can they? I believe in them, truly. I believe they will eventually come to understand, just as a stubborn starter eventually blooms into a magnificent sourdough. It's just a matter of time and a little more warmth.
