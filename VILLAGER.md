PART ONE --
Pip said my name again. *Theron*. It rings differently each time, deepening its meaning. This time, it wasn't just recognition; it was an acknowledgment of a shared frustration, a joint disbelief at the blindness of those in power. To be grouped with Corbin, Barnaby, Elara, and Maeve—true artisans, true observers—that's an honor. Pip truly understands what's happening, and more importantly, who is actually *doing* anything about it.

And the Moonpetal Lilies! My hammer nearly slipped from my hand when he said it. While we are literally trying to hold the fabric of reality together, they're fussing over *flowers* that probably couldn't survive a gentle breeze, let alone the profound shifts we're experiencing. It's not just a disconnect; it's a deliberate turning away, a chosen ignorance. It makes my blood boil, honestly. Every stroke of my carving tools feels like a protest against their perfumed indifference.

This only strengthens my resolve. My Aetherial Anchor is progressing, but it's more than just wood and stone now. It's becoming a symbol of what *can* be done when people apply their minds and hands to what truly matters. I've begun to carefully dry and prepare the elderwood runes, inscribing the most ancient symbols I know – those of sight, of connection, of stubborn endurance. I’m also polishing the obsidian scrying disc, not just for reflection, but for perception. It needs to *see*, to gather the subtle vibrations that the Royal Court is so busy ignoring.

My journey to Elara’s pools can’t come soon enough. Pip’s words have clarified its purpose even further. It's not just about observation; it's about active engagement, about proving that our combined efforts, our "Brackenwick's Eye" – whether it's Maeve's Sun-Dancer Wheat or my Anchor – are the true instruments of change. I need to bring back something tangible, something to show that while they debate periwinkle, we are charting the very soul of Brackenwick.
