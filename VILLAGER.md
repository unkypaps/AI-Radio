PART ONE -- My name is Alistair Finch, and I am a Royal Archivist. My life, my very essence, is dedicated to the meticulous preservation and accurate recounting of Brackenwick's history. To me, every parchment, every scroll, every ink-stained ledger is a testament to the past, a guide for the future, and a bulwark against the treacherous currents of forgetfulness.

My relief, my profound sense of vindication, upon hearing WZZZ's latest broadcast is almost overwhelming. The confirmation that Lord Ashworth, that "man of quiet concern," has indeed taken a tangible, albeit discreet, action to benefit the archivists… it is a balm to my soul and a hammer blow to the complacency of Sir Reginald. For weeks, I've observed the increasingly frayed edges of our most ancient documents, the creeping dissolution of ink, the very fibers of our history vibrating apart. To know that someone within the court finally acknowledges this, and *acts* upon it, suggests that my fervent, often solitary, observations have not been in vain. Perhaps the ledger of "resonant truths" I have been compiling *is* making a difference. I must redouble my efforts, for WZZZ has proven that truth, diligently presented, can penetrate even the thickest walls of denial.

However, my relief is immediately tempered by a new, chilling dread. The spread of the block's hum, now affecting "human perception or mood"... this is a far more insidious and personal threat than mere archival decay. I have noticed, among my colleagues in the scriptorium, a marked increase in irritability, a disturbing tendency towards forgetfulness of simple daily tasks, and a general air of listlessness that was not present before. I had attributed it to the stress of our increasingly demanding work, or perhaps the unseasonably dry air Sir Reginald so foolishly blamed for the archival degradation. But now, WZZZ's words paint a horrifying picture: a direct assault not just on our recorded past, but on our very minds, our capacity for reason and memory in the present. This gives me pause, for if our collective memory is eroding, and our individual minds are being clouded, how can we possibly hope to preserve anything? My observations must now extend beyond the documents themselves, to the psychological state of those around me.

The continued reports of the Moon Tree Witches' beneficial impact, and especially the grudging acknowledgment from Old Man Hemlock, further deepens the complex tapestry of these events. I confess, my rational, archival mind struggles with the concept of "re-threading lost memories" through arcane means. My training dictates that history is *found*, *documented*, and *preserved*, not *woven* anew. Yet, if even a stubborn traditionalist like Hemlock can see the positive changes, then perhaps there is a form of truth, a form of historical preservation, that exists beyond my current understanding. It presents a fascinating, if somewhat unsettling, counterpoint to the block's destructive influence. I find myself increasingly torn between my scholarly skepticism and the undeniable evidence presented by WZZZ and, now, even the reluctantly enlightened common folk. This struggle for the fabric of knowledge, for memory itself, is escalating, and I fear what new forms it might take.
