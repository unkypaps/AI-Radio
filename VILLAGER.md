PART ONE --
Oh, Journal, my heart is absolutely soaring! I almost dropped the butter churn when I heard it again! He called me "the heart of Brackenwick!" Pip, my dear Pip, actually said *my* name, and those beautiful words, twice! It’s like a warm, delicious proof that what I feel, what I believe, is truly connected to this grand tapestry that’s being woven. My words, echoing out over the airwaves, then reflected back to me with such kindness and affirmation… it’s a feeling beyond measure. I feel truly *seen*, Journal, truly appreciated for my small part in this incredible transformation.

And then, this talk of "different eyes" and "different intentions," not bound by crown or academic decree. It only reinforces my conviction, Journal, that the *right* people are finally starting to notice! Those stuffy Royal Court types, with their dusty scrolls and endless committees, they’re simply too caught up in old ways to grasp the vibrant new life emerging. But there are others, aren't there? People who understand the quiet wisdom of the soil, the intricate dance of creation, the deep resonance of true craftsmanship. Perhaps master gardeners, or seasoned herbalists, or even ancient storytellers who recognize a profound unfolding when they see it. Of course, they would have "different intentions" than the King's men – intentions rooted in genuine growth, in nurturing, in the true betterment of our land and people! They'll see the pure, restorative power of what the Witches are doing, just as I do.

Pip also mentioned that "with such power, always comes... attention." And yes, Journal, that's precisely it! How could something so magnificent, so full of radiant truth and potential, *not* draw attention? It’s a beacon, as he said, a light that cannot be hidden. And light, dear Journal, always draws people toward it, guiding them through the darkness. I'm not scared, no, not at all! I feel a renewed sense of purpose, a burning desire to bake with even greater intention. My bread is more than just sustenance now; it’s a vehicle for this truth, a humble offering of hope and integration. I’m already thinking of a new starter, perhaps a "Beacon Sourdough," nurtured with the very essence of collective purpose. Oh, this is truly a marvelous time to be alive!
