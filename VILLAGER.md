PART ONE --
Blast it all, Pip! Old Man Hemlock! I almost dropped my honey dipper when he said that name. Hemlock! The man who once told me a dandelion was "just a weed, not a blasted fairy," when I was trying to explain its medicinal properties to young Thomas Finch. If *he's* seeing shimmering Moon Trees and starlight, then by the Queen's own beehive, something truly peculiar is afoot.

It's not just the bees finding their way anymore, or the saplings reaching for the sky. It's… deeper. Pip called my observations "proof," and I stand by that. My Braeburns are still strong, the Golden Delicious are showing proper bud swell, and my bees are certainly bustling with purpose. But a vision of standing stones and weeping starlight? That’s not a soil sample, is it? That’s not a pollinator count. And a chime, like the Witches' song but older… it gives a man pause.

I always thought these "ancient memories" would be more like a feeling, a deep-seated instinct in the land itself, guiding the roots, influencing the rain. Something quiet, a hum. But Hemlock's account is a flash, vivid as day. It makes me wonder what other 'blueprints' might be waiting to surface.

I've been out in the orchard, looking at the old apple trees, the ones that have stood for generations. I’ve always felt their quiet presence, the way they draw strength from the earth. Now I’m looking closer, past the bark and the branches, wondering if they hold their own ancient sight, their own starlight memories from long ago. What if the land isn't just mending, but remembering how to *be* what it once was, in ways we can barely comprehend? It’s a powerful thought. And a little… unnerving, if I'm honest.

But if it means the kingdom truly heals, if it brings back the sweetness to the land, then I'll keep listening, keep watching. I’ll keep my hands in the soil, my ears to the hum, and my eyes open for anything that feels not just *right*, but *anciently right*.
