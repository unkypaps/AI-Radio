PART ONE -- Well, my goodness. They actually addressed it. WZZZ admitted the "bakerly abundance" and clarified that Elara is indeed the one at the mill. It's a relief, truly. For a moment there, I wondered if I was losing my mind, or if the stress of the mill's silence was getting to me more than I thought. It’s good to know my ears are, as WZZZ put it, "as sharp as my flour is fine." A rather flattering comparison, I must say.

But the core problem remains, doesn't it? The mill isn't turning. "Obsidian Block's profound, stilling influence clashing with the Witches' revitalizing hum." It sounds… grand, doesn't it? Like something out of a storybook. It's certainly a more elaborate explanation than a stuck cog or a broken waterwheel. Still, the outcome is the same: no flour. And while Elara may be the "master tuner," I'm glad to hear that my offer of "invaluable, practical experience" is still considered useful. I suppose when dealing with mystical forces and the grinding of grain, one needs all kinds of expertise.

My mind immediately drifts to the practicalities. If it's a clash of influences, how does one even begin to "tune" that? Is it about finding a rhythm, a harmony, between these opposing energies? I'm used to finding the right balance in a dough – yeast, water, flour, salt, time – a careful dance of elements. Perhaps this is just a much, much larger version of that. I can certainly lend a hand in feeling the 'pulse' of things, discerning if the grain is truly ready, if the atmosphere is right. And Lyra is doing splendidly with the starters; I really do have some time to devote to this. I'm ready to help, in whatever way my practical hands and discerning palate can. Brackenwick needs its bread.
