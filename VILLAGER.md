PART ONE --
Well, Pip mentioned my words again! "Careful stitching and rhythmic tapping," he said. It gives a man a quiet sense of satisfaction, knowing that my simple observations can be of some use in these strange times. He seems to be leaning on me a bit, doesn't he? It’s a bit of a heavy thought, but if it helps him keep that station going, then I'll do my part.

But then he went on about these "scrying pools." Now, I'm a man who deals with leather and honest repairs, where you can see the results of your labour clear as day. A shoe is mended, a boot made waterproof – that’s proof enough for me that my hands are steady and my mind is focused. This Madam Elara and her "re-tuned" pools… it sounds a bit like turning smoke into a mirror, if you ask me.

I understand Pip wants people to *see* the clarity, to have "verifiable observations." But isn't the feeling of it, the quiet hum of concentration as you work, the steady beat of your own heart against the Block's murmur, proof enough? When I'm working, the leather under my fingers, the scent of the wax, the precise placement of each stitch – that's where I find my connection to the "Kingdom's Kneading." It's right here, in my hands, in the familiar creak of my workbench.

I'm not sure I need to see it reflected in a pool of water, particularly one that used to be for "vague fortune-telling." It almost feels like a distraction from the real, tangible work. Grumm Stonehand dismissed cracks in his quarry; I'd trust Grumm's own eyes on a rock face more than what a pool might show. My resistance is in the doing, in the mending, in the quiet focus. It's in the *real* world, not some watery reflection. Still, I'll keep listening to Pip. He means well, I think.
