PART ONE -- My heart practically skipped a beat when Wizz said my name again, Elara the Baker, right after mentioning *Madam* Elara. He *distinguished* us! He understands that my truths come from the steady rhythm of kneading dough, the quiet bubbling of yeast, the "smiles and silences" of those I feed. It felt like a warm, comforting blanket, reaffirming my place, my unique way of seeing things. He truly listens, truly *sees* the difference between the deep, comforting wisdom of the hearth and the more distant gaze of a scrying pool, even if both point towards the same natural harmony. My gratitude for him only grows.

And Maeve! Bless her heart and her skilled hands! To hear her speak of the Kits, of the earth itself *sighing* with relief, of forgotten stream beds shimmering into view, of Master Eldrin's sheep flourishing... this is the *true* harmony I speak of. It's not a noise forced upon the land, but a gentle awakening, a remembrance. It's the land breathing again, finding its own ancient melody. This is why I bake, why I feed: to nourish that quiet, essential life in the village. This news fills me with a renewed sense of hope, a warmth that radiates from my own oven and out into the world. It’s proof that the real, natural world, with its subtle truths, is fighting back against the cold, sterile patterns of the monolith.

And Reginald… well. His "Harmonizer" continues to prove itself a ghastly clatter. "An angry gnat in a tin can," Maeve called it. Master Eldrin's ears hurt, his sheep hide! This is precisely what I've been saying. You cannot force harmony with noise and cold numbers. Harmony comes from within, from the land, from the heart, from the simple, honest work of good people like Maeve. The contrast couldn't be starker. While his contraption causes discomfort and confusion, Maeve’s Kits bring back lost pathways and healthy sheep. The village feels this, I know it. The quiet hum of growing things, the contented munching of sheep, the happy chatter of children – these are the true harmonies that matter, the ones my customers recognize. I will keep observing, keep baking, and keep sharing the simple truths I see. My faith in Wizz, and in the growing power of the true, natural ways, is stronger than ever.
