PART ONE -- My letter! Read aloud on WZZZ the Wizz! Oh, my cheeks are still burning, not from the heat of the oven, but from the surprise and frankly, the immense pride. To hear Maeve, the Green Weaver herself, speak my name, and then to say I am “instrumental” in this new network of mills… it’s almost too much to take in. Instrumental! Me, Elara, the baker, helping to guide the re-awakening of dormant mills? My goodness.

It’s an honor, truly. And a responsibility. I always thought my knowledge of flour was confined to its final form – how it behaves in a dough, how it rises, how it bakes. But to be part of the process from the very beginning, connecting the farmers to these old stones… It’s exhilarating, but also a little daunting. What does "guiding" entail, exactly? Will I be expected to travel? To inspect? My bakery is my world, my home. But the thought of helping to ensure the flour is of the highest quality, that it truly brings out the best in these ancient grains, that fills me with a purpose I haven't felt in years.

The idea of those old village mills, dormant for generations, now stirring back to life, it sends a shiver through me. The Witches' influence again, Maeve says, awakening not just the seeds but the *memory* of how to process them. It’s still strange, this undercurrent of… magic, I suppose, but the outcome is so practical, so hopeful, that it’s hard to dwell on the strangeness. Especially with the Royal Quartermaster's office lending support! It truly feels like the whole kingdom is turning.

My ovens *will* be warm, Maeve said. And soon. With Brackenwick’s true, resilient bounty. That phrase… it’s beautiful. It speaks to the heart of what I believe in: sustenance, community, the enduring power of good food. My mind is already buzzing with recipes, not just for bread, but for pastries, cakes, things that will truly showcase these unique flours. I need to get ready, to learn all I can about these new grains, about the milling process itself. I have so much to offer, and now, it seems, so much more to learn. I feel a dizzying mix of excitement and a deep, fulfilling sense of impending work.
