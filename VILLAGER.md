PART ONE -- My name is Theron, and I've spent more years than I can count coaxing the grain into flour at the old mill in Whispering Falls. My hands know the vibration of healthy gears, the whisper of water turning the wheel.

Wizz said my name. Theron from Whispering Falls. He *said my name*! And he said Elara and Barnaby's solution "echoed my question"! I swear, the old waterwheel outside practically groaned in sympathy with my pounding heart. It’s one thing to listen, to wonder, to be proud of your kin, but to be acknowledged like that, to feel like you've contributed to the kingdom's song, even a small note of it... well, it warms an old miller's soul more than a hearth in winter.

And their solution! Not a new machine, no! I was thinking along those lines, something shiny and new. But a rhythmic, *counter-clockwise turning* of the millstones, timed to the *lunar cycles*? "The kingdom's kneading," Barnaby calls it. And Elara, with her wisdom, seeing the mill as "a living heart." Oh, it's absolutely brilliant! It's so utterly *right*. It's not about forcing something new upon the land, but coaxing out its ancient memory, its true rhythm. It speaks to the very soul of a mill, not just its gears and stones. It’s what I've always felt about our own mill – that it isn't just a building, but a breathing part of the valley. To think of it becoming a "resonant nexus"... it gives me shivers, the good kind.

And the other two mills, settling into a "beautiful, productive balance" thanks to Maeve's kits. That's a relief. It means the work is holding, that things aren't spiraling out of control. It means Elara and Barnaby have a stable foundation for their deeper work. I always knew our local wisdom was worth its weight in gold, but this... this is beyond anything I imagined. We're not just grinding flour anymore; we're tuning the very pulse of the land. And I, Theron, was mentioned. I'm going to have to tell young Pip down at the general store about that. He'll never believe it.
