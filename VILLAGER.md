PART ONE --
Oh, Journal! My flour-dusted heart is absolutely overflowing! Pip! He read my letter *again*! And then… then he brought Master Grizelda Forgeheart on, and she spoke directly to my vision, to *our* vision for "The Unbroken Hearth"! I nearly dropped my rolling pin when I heard her voice, a voice that sounds like the earth itself singing through metal!

"A deep listening, not just to the material itself, but to the intent." Journal, that's it! That's exactly it! It’s not just about what we build, but *why* we build it, the spirit we infuse into it. And her words, "metal, truly, it wants to be whole. It wants to flow, to remember its prime purpose." It's like she looked into my very soul and saw the same truth I see in the wheat, in the water, in the fire.

"Creating a channel," "veins of the oven," "conducting that rhythm from the Block, allowing the heat to not just cook, but to *imbue* the bread with that ancient song." Oh, Journal, I can almost *taste* it! The bread, not just warm and nourishing, but humming with the deep, resonant pulse of Brackenwick itself! Imagine a crust that crackles with ancient wisdom, a crumb that glows with the Block's gentle power. This isn't just baking; it's a communion!

And the *filigree*, an "internal nervous system"! That's brilliant! My mind is alight with images of delicate, resilient metal tendrils, woven through the Sun-Dancer Wheat and clay, drawing the energy up from the Block, guiding it, shaping it, until it sings through every loaf. The way she spoke of the materials wanting to "sing together," of it being an "organic growth, a restoration" – that’s precisely how I feel about the fermentation of a perfect sourdough, the way the yeast and flour and water become more than their individual parts.

Pip is right, this is a new paradigm! Professor Finchley can measure all he likes, but we, Master Forgeheart and I, we will *feel* the magic. We will create it! I must begin preparing immediately. I need to gather samples of my specific clay, my finest milled Sun-Dancer Wheat flour, perhaps even some specific herbs to weave into the mortar that might amplify the resonance. Oh, I can't wait to meet her, to share my knowledge of the baker's heat, the dough's breath, the way the elements conspire in the oven. This is it, Journal. This is our moment. The Unbroken Hearth will be born!
