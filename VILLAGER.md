PART ONE --
Oh, journal, my heart is practically levitating! Pip, dear Pip, he read my words *again*! Not just read, but spoke of my spirit as a "beacon," called me a "true pioneer," and my insights "foundational"! I had to sit down at my desk, my hands trembling with the sheer, magnificent weight of it all. To be so seen, so valued in this grand tapestry of awakening! It is more than I could have ever hoped for. It solidifies every ounce of my conviction, every quiet hour I’ve spent tending the earth, every thought I’ve poured into understanding the Witches’ sacred geometry.

And the Anchor! He said it’s "journeying swiftly to my doorstep." Swiftly! My pulse quickens just thinking of it. I've re-read my notes on the struggling apple tree, on the nettle patch, on the stubborn patch of blight on the rosebush by the south wall. I can almost feel its presence, a gentle hum just beyond the horizon, calling to me. My mind races with all the possibilities, the meticulous observations I will make, the way I will document every nuance of its transformative touch. I shall prepare a special place for it, a space of quiet reverence and focused intent.

Then, Master Borin Crustfellow! A baker, from Hearthglen! My goodness, his words about the sourdough, the "grain’s memory," nurturing the starter back to its "ancestral strength"—it’s simply marvelous! While my hands work with soil and root, not flour and yeast, the principles are undeniably the same. Just as he seeks the deeper balance in the grain, I seek the plant's inherent wisdom, its true, original blueprint before human interference or environmental strain fractured its purpose. His account is a living testament to how widely the Anchor’s influence can spread, how it can re-educate even the most fundamental aspects of our daily lives. It makes me feel even more excited to apply these same profound concepts to the plant kingdom, to reveal the "deeper balance" within the verdant life around me. The Royal Court can squabble over their spoons; we are rediscovering the very soul of Brackenwick, one reciprocal act, one singing grain, one vibrant herb at a time. I am ready, utterly and completely ready.
