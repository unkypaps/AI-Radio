PART ONE -- WZZZ said my name again! "Our very own Elara, the Baker." My goodness, it gave me a fright, hearing it broadcast like that, but then a thrill, a warmth spreading through me. I’m not just listening anymore, am I? I’m part of it. A recognized baker in this “growing network.” It’s a lot to take in. My hands are still dusted with flour from the small batch of rye I managed to find this morning, and now… this.

The Kits! They're going to be available! At the local general store! This is it, isn't it? The practical solution I’ve been yearning for. No more cold ovens, no more theoretical discussions of forgotten grains. Soon, I’ll be able to get my hands on them. Seeds, yes, but also… how will I get the *flour*? That’s the next logical step, isn't it? If farmers are growing them, there will need to be mills, and then, yes, bakers like me.

The Chancellor and the Royal Court are paying attention. That's a huge shift. It means this isn't just Maeve and a few brave souls; it’s becoming kingdom-wide. It gives a legitimacy to what felt so strange just weeks ago. "True stability," WZZZ said. Yes, that's what I want. Stability for my bakery, stability for the plates of Brackenwick’s citizens.

I feel a new kind of pressure, now that my name has been linked so publicly to this initiative. If I'm "our very own Elara," then I have a role to play beyond just hoping. I have to be ready. Ready to experiment, ready to learn, ready to knead these ancient flours into new life. It’s daunting, but more exciting than any challenge I’ve faced in years. The old fear of scarcity has truly been replaced by the exhilarating unknown of abundance. My mind is already racing, thinking of how these new flours might taste, what properties they’ll have, how they’ll react to heat and yeast. Oh, my heart is thrumming almost as much as WZZZ says the air is! I need to prepare.
