PART ONE --
Pip read my letter again! My goodness. And he mentioned my "specific new action." It's truly something, to have these quiet things I do, these small observations, lifted up and given such importance. It makes my heart feel like a hive humming with a particularly good nectar flow – a deep, satisfied thrum.

It reinforces what I felt after the last broadcast. This "deep, quiet knowing" isn't just something I experience; it's real, and it matters. Pip understands it, and he's helping others understand it too. I've been paying even closer attention since last time, trusting those little nudges. Just yesterday, I was preparing a new plot for clover, and something told me to mix in a bit more sand than usual, even though the soil felt fine. It wasn't logic, just a feeling. I did it, and the earth just seemed to sigh contentedly as I turned it over. Small things, but they add up.

He mentioned the court, contrasting their concerns with practical wisdom. Well, I don't really think much about the court, honestly. My court is the sun and the rain, the busy bees and the patient soil. But he's right, their concerns do seem a long way from the ground. If this deep knowing can help people focus on what truly matters for the land and the folk, then it's a good thing, no matter what those in the castle are busy with. I'm not a man for grand pronouncements or challenging authority, but if tending to my corner of Brackenwick, and sharing what I learn, helps to strengthen the heart of this land, then I'll continue to do so. It feels like a quiet sort of responsibility, a humble duty.

I'm glad to be a part of it, even in my small, buzzing way. It feels right.
