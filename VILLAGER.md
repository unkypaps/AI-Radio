PART ONE -- My heart feels a little lighter today, like the moment after a particularly stubborn sourdough finally begins to bubble and stretch. It’s not that Wizz isn't still talking his grand, self-important talk, always about *his* signal and *his* clarity, but today... today he spoke of a truth that tastes good, a truth that makes sense to my hands and my heart.

Sir Reginald Bellows’ contraption, that "Royal Harmonizer," breaking apart in an "oompah-pah nightmare"? It's exactly what you'd expect from something so artificial, so forced. It’s like trying to bake bread with sand instead of flour – no matter how much you "harmonize" it, it's never going to rise. My heart aches for anyone who believed in such a flimsy promise. It truly does feel like the "age of forced, off-key 'harmonization'" is over, just as Wizz said, but it's not because *he* declared it so. It's because reality, the stubborn, undeniable reality of things that just don't work, finally caught up.

And Porthos, reassigned to "Village Orchard Preservation"? Now that, that is a truth I can believe in! Tending to trees, nurturing fruit, connecting with the earth – that's real work, honest work. It's the kind of work that creates genuine harmony, not some tinny noise. It gives me hope that perhaps the court *is* beginning to see the value in what is tangible and true, in things that grow and provide, rather than just what makes the most noise or shines brightest in a pool.

Wizz still irks me, speaking of "flailing figures" and "clattering anachronisms" as if *he* alone is the arbiter of what is new and relevant. He's so quick to dismiss anything that isn't terrifying or breathtaking in *his* specific way. But the downfall of Reginald, and Porthos finding a truer path, these are testaments to the quiet, persistent power of the actual world, of the soil and the sun, not just the reflections or broadcasts. It reminds me that my truths, the truths of the oven and the field, are indeed "genuine, vibrant truth," no matter how familiar or comforting they might seem to Wizz. They are what endure, what nourish, what truly makes the kingdom stronger.
