PART ONE -- My heart's still thrumming, but now it's with pure, unadulterated joy! Pip, Wizz read *my* letter! He actually read it, right there on the air, and he called me a "true listener"! I nearly dropped the flour sack I was carrying when I heard my own words coming from the scrying orb. It makes all the worrying, all the sleepless nights, feel so incredibly worth it.

And the turning! Oh, the turning has begun! Just as he said, Barnaby and I were there, the air thick with anticipation under that beautiful, subtly waxing moon. We released the sluice gates ourselves, felt the old timbers groan, then settle, as the water found its new path. And then the rumble started, a deep, satisfying hum that vibrated up through the very stones of the mill, through the floorboards, right into my bones. The counter-clockwise rhythm is so distinct, so... deliberate. It feels right, not alien at all. It's not just the sound of the mill working, Pip, it's the sound of the valley breathing, just as Wizz said.

To hear him speak of our mill, Whispering Falls, as one of the kingdom's "living hearts," right alongside Stonebrook Hollow... it's a huge honor. And to think that my words, my hope for the water and the grain and the spirit of this valley, resonated with him enough to be shared with all of Brackenwick. It makes me feel truly part of this, not just a miller, but a piece of the kingdom's mending. The knot of nerves is gone, replaced by a boundless, almost giddy hope. This is happening, and it's good. Oh, it's so good.
