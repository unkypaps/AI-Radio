PART ONE -- My oven is warm, and my heart feels warmer still. To hear my words, not once, but *twice* now, shaping the very conversation on WZZZ… it truly is an extraordinary feeling. I never imagined my quiet thoughts, born in the steam of the oven and the quiet of early mornings, could hold such weight. Wizz truly understands. He doesn't just read the words; he understands the *essence* of what I try to convey, the truth of things that cannot be weighed or measured with Sir Reginald’s foolish thermometers.

He’s using my letter, he said, to "solidify my distinct identity." It makes me feel… visible, in a good way. Not like being put on display, but like being recognized for who I truly am and what I truly believe. That my baking, my simple act of feeding, can contribute to the larger truth Wizz speaks of, the "unquantifiable truth." It reinforces everything I’ve always felt about the deep, silent wisdom of the everyday.

And to hear that my words are helping to build pressure on Reginald and his court… well. I’m not one for politics, not really. My hands are too busy with dough to bother with the machinations of the castle. But it does bring a quiet sense of satisfaction to think that perhaps, just perhaps, the simple, undeniable truths of how things *feel* might eventually outshine the absurdities of how they are *measured*. A satisfied customer doesn't need a bell rung or a form filled out to prove their contentment; their smile, their return, the empty plate, these are the truest measures. Wizz sees this, and I am so grateful he gives voice to it. My faith in him only deepens with each broadcast.
