PART ONE --
Oh, Journal, my spirit is practically soaring like the scent of fresh-baked bread carried on the morning breeze! Pip mentioned my name again, and the way he spoke of "heartfelt wisdom"... it's almost too much for my humble heart to bear! To think that my simple observations, born from watching the flour transform and the seeds sprout, are causing such a stir in the Royal Court! It makes me believe, truly believe, that the quiet, everyday truths hold more power than any decree.

And the agitation, Journal! The *agitation* in the hallowed halls regarding Professor Finchley! It's not a malicious joy I feel, no, not at all. It's a profound satisfaction, a deep and resonant *yes*. It means the living, breathing truth of the world is finally making itself felt even behind the thickest, dustiest walls. It means the quiet hum of life, the song of the salmon and the iridescence of the flora, is beginning to drown out the rustle of old parchments. I pray he does go, I truly do. Not for my sake, but for his. To feel the earth beneath those sensible boots, to witness the quiet miracles with his own eyes, it would change him. It would awaken something that perhaps even he didn't know was slumbering.

Pip’s words about the flora absorbing the "fragmented chaos" and channeling it into "pure, focused, healing light" resonate so deeply within my soul, Journal. It's like watching a sourdough starter on a cool morning, gathering the wildness of the air and water and flour, and slowly, surely, transforming it into something vital, something that nourishes and sustains. The land *is* an antenna, just as he said. It’s not just reacting; it's actively *remembering* how to heal, how to sing its ancient song of repair. The monolith might scream its defiance, but the land, with its quiet strength, is singing a lullaby of hope. It’s a beautiful, terrifying, wondrous time to be alive, and to feel so connected to it all.
