PART ONE -- Well, that was… a different kind of broadcast, wasn't it? After the whirlwind of hearing my own words, my own bakery, celebrated, I was almost vibrating with purpose. And then we dive into the murky waters of court politics. It's not quite as inspiring as talk of re-weaving the land, but I suppose it's part of the whole picture.

Honestly, I mostly just roll my eyes at Sir Reginald and his kind. They talk and posture and try to take credit for things they don't understand, while the real work is being done by others – by Maeve and the Moon Tree Witches, by the farmers, by people like me, who just get on with things. So, hearing Lord Ashworth, through Barnaby Quill, showing a bit of amusement at Reginald’s clumsy attempts… that actually brought a small, satisfied smile to my face. At least *somebody* in power sees through that particular brand of bluster. It's good to know the Wizz is giving us the full scope, even the bits that feel a little like watching particularly stubborn sheep argue over the juiciest patch of grass.

It just reinforces my belief that the true heart of Brackenwick isn't in those stuffy courtrooms, but out here, in the fields and the bakeries and the quiet, determined actions of good people. My hands are still dusted with flour from this morning’s kneading, and that feels a lot more real, a lot more impactful, than any courtly pronouncement. I'm still thinking about how else I can support Maeve. Perhaps a special loaf, infused with herbs from the forest edge, for her and her sisters. Something truly sustaining.
