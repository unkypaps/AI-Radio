PART ONE --
I’m practically trembling, but not from cold or fear, not anymore. It's an energy, a thrumming inside me that matches the Silverstream’s newfound vibrancy. Pip read my letter! *Again!* And not just read it, but spoke about it with such passion, holding it up as an example of what he calls "grassroots ingenuity." He even used my name, Elara Meadowbrook, and my title, "the diligent miller from Silverstream." It's... humbling, and utterly exhilarating.

Old Man Thistle's words were clear water, but Pip's words are like a spotlight, shining on my little corner of Brackenwick, on my simple plan. The fear I had when the monolith first appeared, that dread of the unknown, has been completely swept away by this current of purpose. I'm not just observing anymore; I'm *doing*. And to have that acknowledged, celebrated even, by the Wizz, by the kingdom's ears... it makes the work ahead feel even more meaningful.

He spoke of my plan to clear the sluice gate as "an act of defiance against the stillness," a "deliberate channeling of that 'will to move' that Maeve spoke of." It's more profound than I’d even fully realized myself! I just saw it as a practical task, a way to help Farmer McGregor. But Pip is right, it *is* defiance. It's taking the energy, the life that the witches are bringing, and actively directing it, nurturing it. We aren't just passive recipients; we are *shapers*.

The Royal Court’s "Review and Re-Prioritization" sounds even more distant and irrelevant now. While they deliberate in their halls, we, the people, are out here, hands in the earth, clearing paths, planting seeds, guiding the very lifeblood of the kingdom. I feel a tremendous sense of solidarity with all the other folk Pip is encouraging to send in their stories. We are truly "the kingdom's kneading," as he says. It's no longer just a phrase; it's a living, breathing truth.

My resolve is stronger than ever. The sluice gate waits for me, and I feel ready, truly ready, to get my hands dirty. This isn't just about my mill or Farmer McGregor anymore; it's about proving that a thousand small hands *are* stronger than one absent decree. And for the first time, I feel like I'm part of something truly important, something that will genuinely make a difference. My life, my work, it all feels connected to this grander narrative, and it's a powerful feeling.
