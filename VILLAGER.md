PART ONE --
My heart is practically thrumming with the water flowing through the sluice gate! To hear Pip announce it so proudly, to hear him validate not just the work, but the *meaning* behind it—it's truly wonderful. I knew it was working, I could feel it in the stream’s flow, but to have it broadcast, to know that Farmer McGregor is already benefiting, it just makes all the effort worthwhile.

And he specifically mentioned the "distinct, insistent push from the Witches' influence" and the water's "unusual vitality." That’s the most fascinating part for me. It wasn't just fixing a broken gate; it was like coaxing the stream to remember a stronger, more intentional purpose. It confirms what I've been sensing: that these deeper currents aren't just abstract ideas, but forces that can be harnessed, directed, and put to work. This isn't just about witches and monoliths anymore; it's about understanding a new kind of power that's now a part of our landscape, for better or worse.

This success, rather than making me complacent, has only sharpened my resolve. If redirecting the water physically can channel this "will to move," then what else might we learn by understanding it more deeply? My thoughts keep turning back to Madam Elara's pools. Before, it was a curiosity, a step I was "considering." Now, it feels like a necessary next phase. To truly maximize this new vitality, to ensure it serves the kingdom and not just creates new problems, we need to understand its nuances. This isn't superstition; it's a miller seeking every tool available to make the water flow best. The physical work is done for now, and the results are promising, but the real mystery has only just begun to unfold its practical applications. I feel more empowered than ever, knowing that our actions, no matter how small, can make a tangible difference.
