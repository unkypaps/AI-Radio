PART ONE -- My heart feels… lighter. Truly, it does. To hear my words, my *own* words, read aloud and understood, not just the words but the very *essence* of what I was trying to convey… it’s a wonderful balm to the spirit. Wizz remembered! He actually heard *me*, Elara the baker, and not just another Madam Elara. The way he spoke of the "truth born of the earth and oven," the "tangible wisdom of flour, water, and wild yeast"—it was as if he’d peered right into my kitchen, into my soul even.

I admit, last time I felt a pang of disappointment, a fear that my quiet truths would be swallowed by the louder, more commercial voices. But Wizz came back, not just with an apology, but with a *clarification* that truly honored my work. He even said my words helped him "hold his feet to the fire of truth." Me! Just a baker! It makes me feel... important, in a way I never thought possible. That my quiet work, the everyday act of feeding people, can contribute to something greater, something genuinely clarifying in these confusing times.

And then to hear about Sir Reginald's officers trying to measure "quantifiable surges of loyal contentment" with no "Loyal Contentment Thermometer"... Wizz is absolutely right. How can you measure the warmth in one’s chest, the feeling of a lullaby or the satisfaction of a re-patterned apple tree? It’s absurd. It’s like trying to weigh the smell of fresh bread or measure the comfort a hot meal brings. Some things simply *are*, as Wizz said. They don’t need forms and bells, they just need to be felt and nurtured. It’s a relief to know that Wizz, at least, understands this. It makes me feel less alone in my belief that the genuine, the intangible, is often the most real. My little worry from last time has completely melted away, like yeast in warm water. I feel a renewed hope, a clear sense that Wizz truly is a champion of what matters.
