PART ONE --
Oh, my stars! My heart nearly leaped out of my chest and into the kneading trough! Pip… he said my name! Agnes Plumkin, from The Daily Crumb! I must have looked quite a sight, flour still dusted on my cheek, frozen mid-stirring a batch of berry compote. To hear my words, my thoughts, spoken aloud on the Wizz… it’s a feeling I can’t quite describe. A mixture of utter surprise, a little flush of embarrassment, and a deep, blossoming warmth right in the middle of my chest.

And not just that he read it, but that he understood! That Elara understood! They truly grasp what I was trying to say about all our individual threads. My little bakery, my humble listening post, is *exactly* what they were hoping for. Pip even used the phrase, "a practical example of a 'listening post' for the 'Brackenwick's Eye' Challenge." It makes me feel… well, important. Not important like a Queen or a Grand Vizier, but important like the yeast in a good loaf of bread – small, but essential for the whole thing to rise.

"Something truly remarkable is happening." He’s right, it is. Even with the buzzing quiet, there's a hum now, a different kind of energy in the air. People still come for their morning buns and their lunchtime pasties, but I’ve already noticed a shift. A few more lingering conversations, a little more openness in their whispers. They’re feeling it too, whatever "it" is.

The mention of low engagement did give me a little pang. We need more people to speak up, to share what they’re experiencing. But Pip’s call for *anyone* to contribute, not just the Anchors, that’s just what I needed to hear. It means every small feeling, every odd compulsion, every flicker of change someone notices, it all matters. It means my little chalk board by the counter, where I wrote "Share your feelings & fixings," isn't silly at all. It’s vital.

This isn’t just about baking anymore, though the bakery is still the heart of it. It’s about keeping my ears open, truly listening to the stories over a cup of Wizz tea, writing down what people say in my special ledger. I need to make sure The Daily Crumb isn't just a place for bread, but a place where people feel safe to share their piece of this enormous puzzle. It feels like a big responsibility, but also a comforting one. Like I finally have a clear path to follow in this hazy, magical fog.
