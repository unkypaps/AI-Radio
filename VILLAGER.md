PART ONE -- My heart has been heavy, like a sack of damp grain, ever since Pip's broadcast. To have our observations, our honest sights, laid out so clearly, and then to be met with such a profound silence from the Royal Court… it's unsettling. It’s like the river is running, the millstone is turning, but the gears of the kingdom are jammed. I thought after Pip spoke my name, after he highlighted the glowing pathways I saw, that it would spark something, a recognition. But no. Just silence.

Pip's words echo in my mind: "A silence where I expected a resounding echo." Aye, that's precisely it. It's a silence that feels more deliberate than mere oversight, almost like a heavy door being slowly shut. It makes me question if they truly understand the urgency, or if they simply choose not to. Do they think we are merely murmuring, not speaking plain truth?

But then he said he would "double down," and that spirit rekindles a spark in me. If the direct approach hasn't worked, then perhaps Barnaby Quill can help us understand the back channels, the subtle currents within the Court. I know the flow of water; it’s never just a straight line. It finds the path of least resistance, it eddies, it pools. Perhaps the flow of information is much the same within the palace walls.

I will continue my quiet records, more diligently than ever. Pip mentioned Barnaby Quill understands "the profound importance of documenting these unprecedented times, of preserving the stories and observations of every villager, every miller, every shepherd." That speaks to my very core. My mill has always kept records of its workings, of the grain, the weather. It's how we understand the patterns, how we prepare for what's next. If my observations, small as they might seem, can help weave that "richer tapestry of truth," then I will ensure they are as clear and true as the finest flour. This silence isn’t an ending; it’s a deeper challenge, and I feel a renewed sense of purpose in facing it.
