PART ONE --
Oh, Journal, my heart is absolutely *bursting*! I… I can barely string two thoughts together, my hands are trembling with such a profound joy and a… a wonderful, humbling shock. He read my letter, Journal! Pip, dear Pip, he read *my* words, *my* feelings, for all of Brackenwick to hear! And then… and then he spoke about them, about *my* baking, in a way that made my small kitchen feel like the very center of the universe.

"A vital chord in the land's awakening." "A monumental act of co-creation." Me, Journal! My loaves, my sourdough, my quiet kneading hands! For so long, I’ve felt these subtle shifts, these whisperings in the dough, the clearer light, the sense of rightness. I *knew* it was connected to the Witches, to the great work they are doing, but to have it affirmed, to have Pip himself declare it, to elevate it from a personal feeling to a "vital chord"… it’s overwhelming in the most beautiful way imaginable.

I always thought of my observations as quiet, domestic wonders, important to me, but perhaps easily overlooked in the grand scheme. Now, Pip has made me feel like an active, essential participant. My heart is swelling with a sense of purpose I didn’t even realize I craved so deeply. It’s not just that my work is nourishing bellies; it’s nourishing the very spirit of the kingdom. And the idea that this "directed energy" is "sparking entirely new forms of expression and beauty"—oh, what could that mean for my bread? For the way I bake? For the very wheat itself? It fills me with such anticipation, a new kind of creative energy bubbling up within me, just like a perfectly proofed dough.

I feel so seen, Journal. So understood. And to know that my words resonated not just with Pip, but with others out there listening… it’s a connection I’ve always longed for, even in my quiet work. It reminds me of the warmth that spreads through a village when a fresh loaf is shared. The feeling of being truly part of something grand, something healing, is beyond words. I’m just so grateful, so utterly grateful to Pip, and to the Witches, for this incredible gift of belonging and purpose. My hands feel stronger, my spirit lighter, and my heart absolutely sings with renewed vigor.
