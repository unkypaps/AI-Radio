PART ONE --
Well, I’ll be darned. Pip actually read *my* letter. Barnaby Cobblepot, Cobbler by the Square. He said my question was "sensible" and "legitimate." Humph. Of course it's legitimate; it's about the food we eat! What else would be more legitimate? But still, it’s a bit of a shock to hear my own words broadcast out to the whole kingdom. I suppose it means Pip isn't completely daft; he knows a good question when he hears one, even if it's from a simple cobbler.

But being called "sensible" doesn't make me any less worried. In fact, it just highlights that Pip hasn't actually *answered* anything. He just repeated my question back to me and then prattled on about "Sun-Dancer Wheat singing" and turnips with "heightened memory." That's not reassuring, that's alarming! What does that even *mean*? I don't want my turnips to remember anything; I want them to be good for stew. It's just more of that vague, flowery talk that makes me think they're hiding something, or worse, they don't even know themselves.

He says he'll talk to Farmer McGregor. Well, good. He'd better. And I hope Farmer McGregor is more interested in plain truth than in all this "unusual vitality" nonsense. I’m not after "robust flavors" if they come with a side of... well, of *witchcraft*. My own little vegetable patch out back, it's not much, but it's *mine*. And if the water here gets all "activated" by these "deeper currents," what happens to *my* carrots? To *my* beans? I don't want a "revitalized harvest" if it means I can't trust what's on my plate.

Pip thinks he's being clever by saying "the proof is in the pudding." But the fact that we even have to *wait* for the pudding is the problem! We should know these things *before* we're eating them. This whole business isn't blowing over, it's just getting more entangled. And now I'm entangled in it too, thanks to Pip reading my letter. I just wanted to make a point, not become part of the Wizz’s daily chatter. Still, at least he heard me. Now we'll see if he actually *listens*.

This Farmer McGregor, now *he* sounds like a man who knows his soil. I’ll give Pip that much; getting a farmer on the air was sensible. What McGregor said... it wasn't the usual Wizz-speak. "Deeper, richer green." "Firmer, healthier looking." "Denser." That sounds like good news for a farmer, truth be told. And the taste? "Sweetness I hadn’t tasted in years." "Deep, earthy flavor that just... popped." My wife, bless her heart, she's always saying the vegetables aren't what they used to be. Maybe... maybe this isn't all bad.

And the biggest thing, of course, is that he and his family ate them. And they're "doing just fine. Better than fine, actually!" That's what I needed to hear. Not "heightened memory" or "singing wheat," but plain fact: you eat it, you're fine. That's a relief, I won't deny it. A big relief. It doesn't mean I'm entirely sold on the whole "Kingdom's Kneading" business, or on these witches, or on Pip's grand pronouncements, but it does mean my immediate fear of poisoning myself with my own carrots has lessened.

Still, "more themselves"? "Robustness"? It's still... *different*. It's not just regular irrigation. It's not just a good growing season. This is magic, plain and simple, and I'm still wary of magic in my dinner. But at least it seems to be palatable magic, for now. I'll keep an eye on my own little patch. Maybe if McGregor's crops are doing so well, mine will benefit too, and not in any 'unpleasant' ways.
