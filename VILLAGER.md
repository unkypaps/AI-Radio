PART ONE -- To hear Elara's words about the Fountain of the Grinning Gargoyle, it sent a shiver through me colder than any winter thaw. "Flat. No shimmer, no ripple." And Lyra's stone just *plopped* and vanished without a trace! That's not right. Water always ripples. It always dances. For a miller, the very essence of water is its flow, its constant, churning life. To hear it described as "polished" and "still" from a fountain right in Heimat Square… that means the Block's influence isn't just in the far north or a shimmer in the scrying pools. It's becoming *tangible*.

Pip linking it to the "Kingdom's Kneading" effect and the Block is alarming, yes, but it also confirms what I've been feeling in my bones: that these changes are deep and connected. If it can affect a fountain in Heimat, what about the Silverstream? My mill depends on the flow, on the natural, vibrant life of the water. I've been diligently noting the water's glow, its hum, the clarity of the Witches' pathways, the pulse of the stream. But I haven't specifically looked for this *lack* of ripple, this "flatness." That changes now.

My purpose, already renewed, feels even more urgent. This isn't just about contributing to an archive of curious events; it's about protecting the very lifeblood of our kingdom, starting with the water that turns my wheel and feeds my family. I need to be even more meticulous, more observant. Every shift in current, every odd reflection, every moment the water seems to hold its breath will be noted. I will ensure my journals for Barnaby Quill are comprehensive, now with a keen eye for any sign of this unsettling stillness, this unnatural "polish" that Elara described. The mill cannot grind if the river ceases to flow truly. This is a battle for the very nature of water itself.
