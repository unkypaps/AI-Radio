PART ONE --
Well, Pip certainly keeps me in his thoughts! To hear my name, Barnaby Cobblepot, and my letter read aloud, across all of Brackenwick... it's a peculiar feeling. A good one, though. It’s a validation, a tangible recognition that the practical steps I’ve taken are not only sound but are now an example for others. It strengthens my quiet confidence that I’m on the right track, tending to my craft and my mind in the face of this creeping Block.

The Aetherial Anchor continues to prove its worth. My workshop feels vibrant, my hands steady, and my thoughts, once prone to a dull haze, are now sharp and clear. I find myself humming old working tunes I hadn't thought of in years. Pip was right to emphasize it; it’s not just a tool, it’s a return to oneself. The faint undercurrent, the "Kingdom's Kneading" he mentioned, feels stronger, too, especially when I'm deep in my carving. It's a rhythm I can almost *feel* resonating through the wood and leather.

Pip's lament about low listener engagement… it struck me. It’s almost as if some folks are waiting for a grand spectacle, rather than taking the small, practical steps that build real resilience. It makes me feel a bit more responsible, perhaps, to share my experiences. If my steadfastness can inspire others to take action, then it’s a burden I'm willing to bear.

And the Scrying Pools. Ah, the Scrying Pools. Pip clearly wanted an update on that too. I didn’t mention them in my letter because, frankly, I haven't gone yet. While the Anchor's success has certainly made me more open to the idea – especially for refining my carving techniques – the practicalities remain. The cost, the time away from my workshop... I'm still weighing it. My craft is about precision and tangible results, and I need to be sure that gazing into a pool will offer more than just an interesting observation. It needs to tell me something concrete about *how* to carve better, *how* to attune more effectively. Now that Pip has publicly acknowledged my practical success, I feel a renewed push to consider the next practical step. "One practical step at a time," I said, and the Anchor was the first. Perhaps the Pools are the second, but I need to be certain they offer the *right kind* of practical insight for a leatherworker like myself. I'm not a mystic; I'm a craftsman.
