PART ONE -- My hands are shaking, but not from fear this time. They’re trembling with... with *joy*! WZZZ read my letter! My letter, Elara Meadowsweet, the baker! He even said my full name, and my occupation, so there’s no confusing me with that Madam Elara and her scrying pools. Oh, the relief that washed over me when I heard my words, my exact words, about the honest work of my hands and the alchemy of flour and yeast, being broadcast across Brackenwick!

It’s like WZZZ reached right into my heart and pulled out what I was feeling, what I truly believe, and held it up for everyone to see. He understands! He understands that the clarity isn't just in looking into murky water, but in feeling the texture of the dough, in the warmth of the oven, in the nourishing things we create with our own hands. And to hear him connect it to the Moon Tree Witches, to Maeve and Old Man Hemlock, to all that practical, grounded wisdom… it’s exactly what I’ve been feeling. It feels like we’re not just floundering alone against this unsettling block, but that there's a real, tangible way to push back, to keep ourselves rooted.

The threat of the mental scrambling still lingers, of course. It’s a very real and frightening thing to think about losing your grip on reality, especially when your work demands such focus and intuition. But hearing my own words, and hearing them celebrated as a form of resistance, gives me such a boost. It makes me want to knead with even more purpose, to bake with even more care, knowing that every perfect loaf, every comforting pastry, is a small act of defiance against that creeping confusion. I feel seen, truly seen, and that makes all the difference. I’m eager now, more than ever, to hear from Maeve.
