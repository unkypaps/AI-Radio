PART ONE -- Oh, Lyra, did you hear that? Barnaby! From *our* Mill of Whispering Falls! He sent in a question, and Wizz read it out! My heart is just pounding like the waterwheel after a good rain! "Does this initiative involve the very air we breathe and the soil we walk upon?" That’s exactly what I was wondering, what I was feeling in my bones! And Wizz said, "precisely that, and more!" It's like he heard my own unspoken thoughts, even though it was Barnaby who wrote in.

This... this changes things. I was worried, truly, that our ancient work, turning the grain, the rhythm of the mill, was something they might deem "traditional" in a way that meant it was no longer relevant. But no! It's *central* to the "Resonance Reclamation Initiative"! Our daily work *does* contribute to the kingdom remembering its true song. I feel such a surge of pride, I could weep.

And then the report from Stonebrook Hollow! Mills, Lyra, *mills*! Not just any old ruins, but places where the earth and the water and the wind all come together. One with the cold stillness of the Block, another buzzing with the Witches' life. It's... it’s all so much more tangled than I thought, but it makes sense that the mills would be places where these energies meet. And the third one, silent, but with that glowing moss! Like near Heimat! It's as if the very heart of Brackenwick is waking up, not with a roar, but with a quiet, shimmering glow. It makes me want to look at our own mill with new eyes, to listen to its hum, to feel the air more intently. I have to talk to Barnaby about this, and Elara. They'll be just as amazed, I'm sure. This isn't just news anymore; it feels like we're all a part of a much bigger, much older story that's finally being told.
