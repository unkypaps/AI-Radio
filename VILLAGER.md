PART ONE --
Pip read my letter again, and Maeve even spoke directly to my concerns. It’s… something. I'm not sure if it's relief or a new kind of focused worry. My observations, my practical questions, they're not being dismissed as simple miller's grumbling. That's a comfort, to feel heard, especially when the stakes are so high for us.

Maeve spoke of water that feels "more insistent," "more alive," even "buoyant." She mentioned a "subtle, almost musical hum." It’s certainly a stark contrast to the growing silence and stillness I’ve been feeling here at the Silverstream. The idea of water having a "new purpose," an "essence" that counters the Block's "absence," it’s... poetic, for a Green Weaver. For a miller, it's a question of physics.

She did admit, quite honestly, that they haven't seen a "full-scale mill wheel turn solely on this re-energized flow." That's the part that still catches in my throat. "Tendency is undeniable," she said. And that gives me *some* hope, a flicker of light against the growing dread. But hope won't turn my wheel. Physical force will.

Still, her words give me something concrete to look for. "Buoyant water," "insistent flow." I can observe that. I'll be downstream of any major Moon Tree hubs, but the Silverstream *does* flow down from the northern hills, where the Block is. If the witches' influence truly radiates through these ley lines, perhaps it will touch our waters here, even subtly. I need to walk the banks, feel the current, listen. Not just for the sound, but for that deeper *hum*, that feeling of aliveness she described.

I will be watching, feeling, and hoping. My mill stands ready, waiting for the water to remember its purpose.
