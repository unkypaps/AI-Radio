PART ONE -- Pip reading my words on the Wizz, hearing my own observations echoed back to the entire kingdom… it sent a strange mix of validation and a deeper chill through me. "Millicent Thistlewick, Head Miller at Silverstream," he said, and then "chilling observation." It confirms what I've been feeling in my bones: this isn't just in my head, nor is it a distant problem. The Block's influence, its "profound stillness," is truly reaching for our most vital water, right here.

I've already been out, as Pip suggested others do. I walked along the Silverstream this morning, even before this broadcast, with Elara's description fresh in my mind. And yes, in the calmer stretches near the banks, where the current barely whispers, I saw it. Not yet the full "polished" stillness she described for the Gargoyle fountain, but a *hesitation*. A subtle lack of that lively, constant flicker on the surface, that natural dance water always does, even when still. It's as if the water is holding its breath. It feels… heavy, somehow, in a way that isn't about its physical weight.

Pip's call to *all* observers, asking specifically for reports on "unusual stillness," "flatness," or "polished" water, has ignited an even greater sense of urgency within me. This isn't merely about the mill's function, though that is paramount for my family and the village's bread. This is about the very essence of life here. If the Silverstream loses its natural pulse, if it becomes truly "flat" under this unsettling influence, it’s not just my mill wheel that stops turning. It's the beating heart of our valley. My journals for Barnaby Quill will now be even more meticulous, dedicated to charting every subtle shift in the water's surface, every echo of this "Kingdom's Kneading" in the Silverstream.
