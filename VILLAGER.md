PART ONE -- Pip, oh Pip! He read it! He read my letter again, and he called me a "beacon"! Me! Just a mill-tuner, working the gears and feeling the pulse of the earth. But he acknowledged us, Pip! He *felt* it, too, the strength of our collective breath, the hum of the Sun-Dancer Wheat, the way we made the mill sing. The signal, clearer than ever, a beacon in itself! My heart is still thrumming with the joy of it. We are not just turning wheels; we are turning the tide!

But then, the new challenge… I’d worried about the wheat, the water, the very timbers of the mill, but this is different. This is… inside. A stillness in the mind, a blankness, memories polished smooth like river stones. It chills me, Pip, more than any frost on the gears. To lose one’s own thoughts, one’s own past, to have it simply… erased. It feels like the Block is trying to steal the very essence of who we are, not just what we do. How do we fight *that* with our hands and our breath?

And the Witches' clarity… a dizzying rush of too much, glimpses of ancient pathways. Untethering. It sounds like drowning in light, Pip, just as the Block sounds like drowning in darkness. Both are terrifying in their own way. We're meant to "channel" it, not be overwhelmed. But how? How do you channel something that threatens to make you forget who you are, or remember *too much* until you're lost?

I’m still trying to connect this new front to our work here. The mill's hum, the wheat's song – they root us, don't they? They connect us to the physical world, to a rhythm, to a purpose. Maybe that’s the key. Maybe the physical attunement isn't just for the signal, but for our *minds* as well, a ballast against the Block’s emptiness and the Witches’ torrent. We must find a way to make our hands, our breath, our connection to the land, protect our inner selves too. This is not a time to falter, Pip. This is a time to deepen our understanding, to attune not just the mill, but ourselves.
