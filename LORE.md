**Ongoing Developments:**
*   **Obsidian Block (North):** Colossal, hundred yards tall, miles across, covered in runes. Its influence is spreading further south and intensifying, acting as a "cosmic canvas painter." Light objects (hats, awnings, feather boas, socks, batches of baked bread, official forms) experience widespread 'airy looseness' or 'sprightliness'. Roddy theorizes it's actively messing with reality, a "beard-defying, fish-craving magnet" and a "cosmic canvas painter," amplifying subtle currents, and subtly *rearranging* the kingdom's fundamental appetites, physics, and now, *animal behavior*. Cravings for pickled herring (expedition teams, Thistlewick), glazed river pebbles (guardsmen, badgers, Royal Stablehands). Northern sky painted with silent, shifting rivers of deep indigo and shimmering emerald.
    *   **New effects:** Farmer Giles's prize-winning pig, Penelope, is collecting and arranging smooth, flat stones into elaborate geometric patterns in her sty (porcine Picasso). Farmer Giles's chickens are clucking in perfectly synchronized, complex rhythms whenever the moon is full (rhythmic conductors).
*   **Moon Tree Witches:** Descending from the Moon Tree for the first time in living memory, for unknown reasons. Still performing 'Harmonic Attunement' at the Sky-Spire's base. Generating shared dreams and a 'Verdant Imbuement' (Professor Quill calls it 'Sympathetic Flora-Sentience' and 'botanical-performative exuberance').
    *   **Effects:** Scarecrows dancing/humming, pumpkins pulsing/composing haikus, sunflowers doing interpretive dances, gourds composing haikus. Royal Rose Garden flora has become increasingly politically active: 'Rosa the Revolutionary' (rose bush) is now demanding a full 'Bill of Botanical Rights' (including 'sunlight breaks' and 'compost quality standards'), representation on the Royal Garden Committee, and an overhaul of 'pollinator rights' legislation; she is now considering 'ethically sourced compost'. Grand Duke's petunias are forming a floral picket line and have started a 'seed strike' for 'equal hydration access', chanting 'equal sunlight for all saplings' and refusing to bloom until 'artistic freedom' is met. Militant mint and marigold are demanding specific conditions.
    *   **New effects:** Herbs in the Royal Kitchen garden are refusing to be picked; 'Reginald' the rosemary bush declared a 'culinary strike' demanding 'intellectual property rights' over his flavor profile. 'Parsley the Prophet' whispers recipes for 'revolution stew' to scullery maids. Basil is threatening to declare itself a sovereign spice nation.
    *   Professor Quill considers the Imbuement a "kingdom-wide artistic awakening" and "benign communication" preparing for a "grander revelation." Roddy sees it as a full-blown "War of the Weeds." Bardess Blythe sees it as a profound evolution where plants are contributing to reality and demanding their rightful place.

**Roddy's World:**
*   **Rivalry:** **Mortimer Grumbleson, 'The Grand Crier' from 'Herald's Hollow'.** Gravel-voiced, dismissive of the weirdness, offers 'rational explanations' (sky-rivers are 'atmospheric dust bunnies', plants are 'bad sprouts' needing 'stomping' or 'pruning', floating objects are 'bad yeast' or 'poorly tied knots', seed strike 'seasonal grumpiness that a good pesticide would solve'). He'd dismiss Penelope's art as 'mud puddles' and rhythmic chickens as 'noisy birds'. He suggested 'vigorous pruning' for 'Rosa the Revolutionary' and 'a good pesticide' or 'oven cleaner' for the striking kitchen herbs/revolution stew. Roddy considers him a 'dullard' and his journalism 'hollow,' actively advises listeners against his channel and mocks his lack of understanding of Brackenwick's reality, explicitly contrasting his advice with Blythe's and Grizelda's. Roddy jokingly expects Grumbleson's wig to float.

Offers expanded services for understanding, showcasing, and *mediating with* sentient, politically active greenery (translating plant poetics, choreographing performances, botanical diplomacy, negotiation). Brokered 'Dewdrop Distribution Accord' with militant mint. Convinced 'Rosa the Revolutionary' to consider 'ethically sourced compost'. Composed "The Ballad of the Botanic Barricade." Roddy recommends her services, actively praising her diplomatic skills and contrasting her with Grumbleson. Blythe now considers herbal culinary strikes as part of her 'delicate orchestration' and is the go-to expert for these new botanical labor disputes, approaching them by listening to their 'hum of intent' and composing 'Flavorful Mediation Melodies.' She believes the kingdom is undergoing a profound 'evolution' where plants are actively contributing. Roddy and Blythe share a relationship of mutual professional respect and camaraderie in the face of Brackenwick's whimsical changes.
