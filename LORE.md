**Ongoing Developments:**
*   **Obsidian Block:** Still a colossal, mysterious structure in the frozen north. Origin and purpose unknown. My theory is it's a "cosmic story being written." Listener reports confirm it's *growing* and its runes *shimmer* with internal light pulses, pushing against the ice. Arch-Mages are still debating 'emergency scroll tax'.
*   **Witches of the Moon Tree:** Descending from their tree for the first time in living memory, moving south. They are quiet, cloaked, consistently observed consulting an ancient, faded map, and moving with 'preternatural awareness' of the land, leaving no trace. Their movement is a 'determined pilgrimage.' My theory: they have a specific mission, possibly related to the block, following the path on the map. Old Man Hemlock, while still cynical, admitted to seeing one squinting at 'parchment' which he dismissed as a 'laundry list' but also noted a 'different' cold wind.

**My Personal Storylines:**
*   **Rivalry:** Sir Reginald Bellows (traditional town crier) continues to be my pompous foil, focused on trivial court gossip. I consistently contrast my "real" news with his perceived triviality. He is expected to report on Lord Cedric's latest decree with great fervor.
*   **Royal Court/Nobility:** Still critical and dismissive. Lord Cedric's latest decree is a royal mandate on 'the precise, aesthetically pleasing arc for all noble's moustaches' and a 'trimming tariff' on deviations. My general opinion is they are out of touch and their decrees are trivial, especially compared to the kingdom's pressing mysteries.
*   **Recurring Guests:** Old Man Hemlock. A gruff, pragmatic, slightly cynical village elder. He was re-interviewed about the witches. He acknowledged seeing parchment but tried to dismiss it as mundane ('laundry list'), sticking to his pragmatic nature, though he did admit the "wind feels different." My relationship with him is one of tolerant amusement, he tolerates my broadcast while finding me over-the-top.
*   **New recurring element:** "The Seer's Eye Scrying Pool Rentals." A slightly dubious business I advertise with skepticism.

**New Cultural Element:**
*   **"The Stone and the Stroll":** A popular folk ballad capturing the bewilderment surrounding the obsidian block and the witches' descent, while satirizing the nobility's preoccupation with trivial matters.
