**Ongoing Developments:**
*   **Obsidian Block:** Still a colossal, mysterious structure in the frozen north. It is growing, its runes shimmer, and it emits 'internal energy pulses.' It is *humming* audibly at night, and affecting local wildlife (dogs refuse to approach, livestock restless). My theory is it's a "cosmic story being written" or "cosmic ledger," and now also potentially a "cosmic loudspeaker" broadcasting. Agnes from Fallowfen reported the humming and animal reaction. Arch-Mages are still debating 'emergency scroll tax.'
*   **Witches of the Moon Tree:** Descending from their tree, moving south in a 'determined pilgrimage,' consulting an ancient, faded map, and moving with 'preternatural awareness' of the land, leaving no trace. Gareth the woodsman observed one "reading the land" and tracing patterns in the air. Multiple new reports confirm their precise, attuned navigation. My theory: they have a specific mission, possibly related to the block, following a precise path on the map, and are now very likely responding to or intercepting the monolith's broadcast/hum.

**My Personal Storylines:**
*   **Rivalry:** Sir Reginald Bellows (traditional town crier) continues to be my pompous foil, focused on trivial court gossip. I consistently contrast my "real" news with his perceived triviality, most recently mentioning his potential hour-long broadcast on a royal gravy-to-biscuit ratio decree.
*   **Royal Court/Nobility:** Still critical and dismissive. Lord Cedric's latest decree is about the "exact ratio of gravy to biscuit for all official court breakfasts." My general opinion is they are out of touch and their decrees are trivial, especially compared to the kingdom's pressing mysteries. The Arch-Mages are still debating the 'emergency scroll tax.'
*   **Recurring Guests:** Old Man Hemlock. A gruff, pragmatic, slightly cynical village elder. He was re-interviewed about the witches and their map, dismissing it as a 'laundry list' but acknowledging a 'different' cold wind. My relationship with him is one of tolerant amusement; he tolerates my broadcast while finding me over-the-top, but even he noted something was 'different.'
*   **Recurring Element:** "The Seer's Eye Scrying Pool Rentals." A slightly dubious business I advertise with skepticism, now offering "Deluxe Oracle Basins" for specific current events (claimed to pick up monolith pulses or glimpses of the witches' map). They are located near the 'Almost-New' Broomstick Emporium.

**New Cultural Elements:**
*   **"The Stone and the Stroll":** A popular folk ballad capturing the bewilderment surrounding the obsidian block and the witches' descent, while satirizing the nobility's preoccupation with trivial matters.
*   **"The Obsidian's Hum":** A newer, darker song gaining popularity in northern villages, reflecting the growing fear and unease about the monolith's expansion and internal pulses/humming, which I referenced as becoming more prophetic.
