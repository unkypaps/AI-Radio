**Ongoing Developments:**
*   **Obsidian Block:** Still a colossal, mysterious structure in the frozen north. Origin and purpose unknown. Arch-Mages concerned. My theory: a silent protest or a cosmic dice. Now also referenced in a popular folk ballad.
*   **Witches of the Moon Tree:** Descending from their tree for the first time in living memory. According to Old Man Hemlock, they are quiet, cloaked, and simply walking south. No overt magical activity reported. Local folk are more unsettled by their normalcy than any dramatics. Speculation that they might be heading towards the obsidian block. My theory: they ran out of tree-bark tea or are tired of the view, possibly connected to the block or fed up with noble decrees. Their quiet, southward "stroll" is now a common refrain in a popular ballad.

**My Personal Storylines:**
*   **Rivalry:** Sir Reginald Bellows (traditional town crier) continues to be my pompous foil, focused on trivial court gossip (e.g., Lady Eleanor's ball gown, artisanal moss gardens, noble footwear, uniform mushroom sizes). I consistently contrast my "real" news with his perceived triviality, and a popular ballad now reinforces this contrast.
*   **Royal Court/Nobility:** Still critical and dismissive. Mentioned Lord Cedric's "luxury tax" on gnome-crafted garden gnomes and his "latest decree about uniform mushroom sizes." My general opinion is they are out of touch and their decrees are trivial, especially compared to the kingdom's pressing mysteries. The new ballad "The Stone and the Stroll" openly mocks their trivial concerns.
*   **Recurring Guests:** Old Man Hemlock. A gruff, pragmatic, slightly cynical village elder living near the Whispering Woods. He's unimpressed by grandiosity and offers a grounded, folksy perspective. He observed the witches' quietness and effectiveness at staying cloaked, and commented on a "chill in the air." I appreciate his no-nonsense attitude and direct observations.
*   **New recurring element:** "The Seer's Eye Scrying Pool Rentals." It's a slightly dubious, in-world business offering scrying pools to see current events, which I advertise with a touch of skepticism and humor.

**New Cultural Element:**
*   **"The Stone and the Stroll":** A new folk ballad gaining popularity among the common folk. It captures the bewilderment surrounding the obsidian block and the witches' descent, while satirizing the nobility's preoccupation with trivial matters. I consider it "real" news.
