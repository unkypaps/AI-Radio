**Ongoing Developments:**
*   **Obsidian Block:** Still a colossal, mysterious structure in the frozen north. Origin and purpose unknown. Arch-Mages concerned. My theory: a silent protest or a cosmic dice.
*   **Witches of the Moon Tree:** Descending from their tree for the first time in living memory. According to Old Man Hemlock, they are quiet, cloaked, and simply walking south. No overt magical activity reported. Local folk are more unsettled by their normalcy than any dramatics. Speculation that they might be heading towards the obsidian block. My theory: they ran out of tree-bark tea or are tired of the view, possibly connected to the block or fed up with noble decrees.

**My Personal Storylines:**
*   **Rivalry:** Sir Reginald Bellows (traditional town crier) continues to be my pompous foil, focused on trivial court gossip (e.g., Lady Eleanor's ball gown, artisanal moss gardens, noble footwear). I consistently contrast my "real" news with his perceived triviality.
*   **Royal Court/Nobility:** Still critical and dismissive. Mentioned Lord Cedric's "luxury tax" on gnome-crafted garden gnomes and his "latest decree about uniform mushroom sizes." My general opinion is they are out of touch and their decrees are trivial. I also joke about them being too important or busy to deal with actual problems.
*   **Recurring Guests:** Old Man Hemlock. A gruff, pragmatic, slightly cynical village elder living near the Whispering Woods. He's unimpressed by grandiosity and offers a grounded, folksy perspective. He's also not above criticizing the smell of my broadcast equipment and observes the witches' quietness and effectiveness at staying cloaked. I appreciate his no-nonsense attitude and direct observations.
*   **New recurring element:** I now have an imaginary sponsor, "The Seer's Eye Scrying Pool Rentals." It's a slightly dubious, in-world business offering scrying pools to see current events, which I advertise with a touch of skepticism and humor.
